How to Join

Joining the Altador Cup

Congratulations! You have made the amazing decision to sell your soul devote your free time to helping Darigan Citadel win the Altador Cup! You won't regret this decision. Now how do you join us in our quest for victory? Just go to the Altador Cup Sign Ups, find the Darigan Citadel logo on the map, and click join! It'll ask you a couple times if you are sure you want to join because once you join a team you are locked into that team for the duration of the cup. Of course you will select yes when prompted because there is obviously no doubt about what team you want to join. Presto! You are now officially a devoted minion forever bound to Lord Kass valued member of DC who will help finally get us back on that podium where we belong!

Joining AC/DC

Now that you are officially a part of team Darigan Citadel now it's time to join our group AC/DC. To participate just find the board with the AC/DC:// Witty Title Here #000 title and come hang out with your fellow DC players! The board is your new home now! It's a place for us DC players to come together to encourage and motivate each other to give maximum effort for the duration of the cup. All DC players are welcome. Always remember we are a team. Please be kind and courteous to everyone but most importantly have fun!

Ranking Up

We of course want to make Lord Darigan proud and we do that by achieving the highest rank we can manage. How do you rank up you ask? Well you play the games. ALOT. Find some good music, an audio book, maybe a nice tv show and buckle in. Playing as many games as you can on any given day will increase your rank and contribute to DC's performance to best our opponent. Now I'm sure you're wondering there are four games, do I play all four?? Well if you want to and have time then by all means play all 4 games. But as long as you are meeting the minimum score requirements playing any combination of games will increase your rank. You can only play YYB. You can mix up with YYB and SHSH. It's up to you! Pick the games that appeal to you and grind, grind, grind! Don't forget to utilize the AC/DC board if you find yourself needing some encouragement.

Beware the Flagging System

In AC XII a flagging system was put in place to catch cheaters. Unfortunately some innocent players got caught up in this system. In the event this flagging system is still in place Sciger has provided some tips to help avoid being unjustly flagged.
  • Take breaks from time to time. Take 5-10 minutes to do something else other than play the AC games. The system looks for repetitiveness that never changes as this is what bots do. Try to avoid bot-like behavior so the system does not mistake you for one. Take longer breaks if you start to feel burned out
  • When playing multiple games cycle through them (i.e. play 10 games of YYB and then switch to 10 games of MSN, etc). Be on the safe side and do not play multiple games in separate windows.

Stretching Tips from Carolyn (parody_ham)

As we know endless hours in front of the computer grinding away can be pretty tough on the body. It's still important to take care of yourself during the AC and Carolyn has provided some stretches and tips to help put a little less stress on the body

Note from Carolyn: If it hurts doing the exercise, don't push it. Always listen to your body. I hope this helps! I'm not a doctor or anything, just someone who has been to a lot of PT over the last 4 years from various injuries. I hope this blurb helps!
Make sure to stand every 30 and stretch out to avoid becoming stiff. Keep good posture, avoid slouching.

For Shoulder and/or neck Pain:
  • Ice if it's hurting, heat relaxes the muscles.
  • Gently roll shoulders back for 10, forward for 10
  • For a good stretch, gently pull down your head with one hand, making a triangle with your arm. Let your other arm relax *or* if you're in a chair, you can gently grip it to deepen the stretch. Hold for 10, stretch both sides at least 5 times each
The following are all while laying down:
  • Slowly turn your neck to each side and hold for 10
  • Slowly bring your ear towards your shoulder for a good stretch, hold for 10 for each side
  • Put a towel under your neck. Slowly draw your chin door and hold for 5, repeat 10 times
  • Towel under neck, squeeze shoulder blade down, slowly bring up arms as if making a snow angel. When you feel a good stretch, hold for a few seconds and slowly release
For strengthening:
  • Using a Therapy band, do external and internal rotation exercises. You can also look for rows. Start with an easier level of band tightness like red, then move up to green or blue
  • Do a half (or whole if you're strong enough) pull-up against wall, make sure your shoulders don't scrunch up
For hand pain:
  • Straighten your right arm. Very gently, using your left hand, apply pressure at the top of your right hand so that your right fingers are facing towards the floor. Hold it for 30
  • Bring each finger to your thumb, do it 5 times. Then gradually increase the number of times each finger is brought to your thumb, 5 reps. Increase until each finger is brought in 5 times



How to Scorekeep

How to Post Your Score

So you're working hard and playing the games. Now what do you do? Well now you post your scores on the AC/DC board so we can keep track and contribute to our goal!

When you post your score the typical format is: +#### -insert game name-

Below are the scores you post for each game:
Yooyuball: # of goals you scored subtract # of goals scored against you (i.e if you score 10 goals and the AI scores 2 goals you would post +8 YYB on the board)
Slushie Slinger: Your score
Make Some Noise: Your Score
Shootout Showdown: Your Score

You can post your scores after each individual game or hoard your scores and post the total before the match ends. It's up to you! Whatever keeps you motivated.

Below is an example of what a score post might look like:
+20 YYB
+150,000 MSN
+25,000 SLSL
+300,000 SHSH

How to Scorekeep

Everyone's posting their scores and now I'm sure you're wondering what do we do with them? Well we add them up and crush goals! Our totals are not a secret so no need to worry about other teams seeing how we're doing. Determining who wins the match is much more involved than the totals on a single chat group. Scorekeeping is more for motivation than anything else.

The typical score update post is usually formatted like this:
YYB: ###/xxx
SLSL: ###/xxx
MSN: ###/xxx
SHSH: ###/xxx
@username ##:## am/pm NST

###: is the current number of points we have earned in that game
xxx: represents our current goal (set by the scorekeeper)
@username: is the username of the person we stopped calculating totals at
##:## am/pm NST: is the time that user posted

Once a goal has been reached it is nice to let the team know in the score update post and then set a new goal (i.e. 270/200 YYB (GOAL CRUSHED) New Goal: 350)

EXAMPLE TIME!!
So let's say it's the beginning of the day and you see these posts
User 1 12:17pm: +15 YYB
User 2 12:17pm: +5749 MSN
User 3 12:18pm: +455 SlSl
User 4: 12:20pm: +3600 ShSh
User 1: 12:21pm: +12 YYB

The score update post would look like this:
YYB: 27/1,000
SLSL: 455/5,000
MSN: 5,749/50,000
SHSH: 3,600/40,000
@User 1 12:21pm NST

If you take over scorekeeping in the middle of the day start from the post noted in the last score update and add new scores to existing scores.

If you are asking yourself who can scorekeep the answer is anyone! If you feel comfortable with the process and no one is currently scorekeeping then pull out that calculator or pull up Excel (recommended) and start chugging away at those score posts.
