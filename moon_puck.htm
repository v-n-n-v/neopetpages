Reborn used to be known as Smokey, an average tom cat with an adoring family. As most young cats do, he has a fascination for all things off limits and outdoors. Most days, he runs out into the woods behind his house to explore, jump in leaves, and chase local wildlife.
One day he hears his family calling for him from their back door, but he drives himself further into the woods than he's ever gone before. He stops to nap in a small den and when he wakes, he's found that the woods look very different than before.
While he napped, he'd perished in a fire and everything he knew had changed. Unable to hear or smell, he was too far from home to find his way back naturally.
Now poor Smokey wanders the woods as a spirit reborn from ash, trying to find a way back to his family. As one might imagine, he begins to look inward for comfort and answers.

Name: Reborn (aka Smokey)
Gender: Male
Species: Common house cat
Appearance: Black cat made of soot/ash, white eyes/whiskers and fluffy tail.
Affinity: Exploring, Nature, Himself
Dislikes: Being bossed around, isolation, confinement.

Smokey thinks very highly of himself and wasn't the most user-friendly feline before his demise. He's stubborn, curious, self-absorbed, and typically unappreciative of others. He has a sweet side but constantly finds that he's less aware (or uninterested) in the feelings of others around him.
He constantly thinks of returning home and has to rely on himself- which is proving to be lonelier than he ever could have imagined. In addition to being lost, he doesn't quite understand what's happened to him and what this new form of his entails. Smokey has a lot of searching inward to do!

By the time I woke up, it was daytime. Sunlight filtered through the naked branches of the charcoal-black trees. As far as I could see, the forest all around me had been stained in soot and ash, a dreary gray landscape. At least I assumed it was soot and ash - try as I might, I could not smell it. Whatever it was, it had even stained my beautiful gray coat, now dark like the trees that were around me. The previous night had been dreadful, and the heat had been insufferable for however long it lasted. However, with the dawn of a new day, I felt weirdly okay… for the most part.
My ears kept flickering, but I could not hear anything. I could take deep breaths, but I could not smell anything. For moments, perhaps minutes, maybe longer, I thought it had been the fire - perhaps it had burnt away the sounds and smells of the forest. It could be possible, couldn't it?

Trying not to think too deeply about it, I looked all around. With the trees charred, the noises gone, and the scents missing, it felt as though I was somewhere else entirely. Home was south from here… wasn't it? Oh, but I had taken a turn west at some point, then headed the opposite direction when I heard my caretaker call… My thoughts had become muddied. Unwilling to simply sit there and let the day go by, I picked a direction and began walking. Surely, one way or another, I'd make it home.
So I would walk deeper and deeper into the forest, retracing my steps to the best of my abilities - and then, someday, I'd make it home, wouldn't I? Without smell, without hearing, it would be hard… but not impossible, right? My coat may be different, but my caretaker would recognize me… would they not?

… there was nothing wrong with me, was there?
