Dragon Divination
.


Astrology is the study of the movements and relative positions of celestial objects
as a means for divining information about human affairs and terrestrial events.

Introduction

Astronomy itself is the study of the heavenly bodies by defining weight, speed, mass and how that body interacts with the other bodies of the universe. Astrologers take what we know about astronomy, and apply it to human activity.
In the most general sense, astrology is a belief system built on using observation of the heavens - genuine astronomical phenomenon - as a way to predict or understand events in the human world on Earth.
Astrology is one of the most ancient forms of divination. There are many different forms, some being: Electional, horary, locational and natal.

  • Natal astrology is the study of the individual and the life of said person. In natal astrology we explore the time, place and date a person was born and how that effects that particular individual. Natal astrology is also sometimes referred to as genethlialogy, from the Greek word genethlialogia.
  • Electional astrology is the art of choosing the best moment for any particular project or event. All projects begin with an idea and then are followed by a plan to manifestation. It is based on the concept that information about the nature and course of new ventures can be determined by examining the alignment of celestial bodies at the moment that they begin.
  • Horary astrology is an ancient branch of horoscopic astrology in which an astrologer attempts to answer a question by constructing a horoscope for the exact time at which the question was received and understood by the astrologer.
  • Astrocartography or locational astrology is the study of the lands, the features, the inhabitants, and the phenomena of the Earth. Some relation between zodiac signs and cardinal points is highly probable to have been established for astrological weather forecasts and other purposes of prediction.
  • Mundane Astrology is a branch of astrology that involves the application of astrological principles in order to study large groups of people, such as cities and nations. Traditionally been applied to the study of natural phenomena such as predicting the weather or earthquakes.
In order to map all of these factors, astrologers use something called a chart. A chart is a map of the sky with the planets and heavenly bodies included showing their location. From this chart an astrologer will begin to piece information together and draw conclusions based on the birthday, birthtime and birthplace. The most basic chart is a picture of the sky from an individuals perspective.

Astrologic Purpose

Throughout human history, astrology has been considered a genuine scholarly and academic tradition, and in many world cultures, it has a strong religious significance. The credibility of the "study" of the impact of stars on human events was dealt a serious blow during the Enlightenment era, when the science of astronomy began to bring a number of long-held astrological principles (such as heliocentrism) into doubt.

Today, astrology is typically considered to be pure superstition or pseudoscience, though this does remain somewhat controversial in most world cultures. In Western astrology, as well as a number of Eastern systems, the most heavy significance is placed on Zodiac types. In other words, the position of various stars, heavenly bodies, and constellations at the time of a person's birth, but there are numerous other variables that are measured and evaluated by astrologists and enthusiasts.

Though many of these different Zodiac types are relatively old, with key principles and measurements dating back even thousands of years, some have been developed more recently, and even attempt to combine an ancient understanding of the zodiac along with a more modern interpretation of cosmology.


Neopian heavenly bodies


The stars are our eternal companions here in Neopia, whether as guides, the subject of myth or harbingers of disaster. And there is always more waiting to be discovered!

Stars
Although they look like points of light to us, stars are really giant burning spheres of gas like our own sun. Stars come in several different colors, corresponding to the heat with which they burn. From coolest to hottest, they are red, yellow, blue, purple and white. With the exception of the twelve known constellations, the Neopian stars appear to change position rather drastically depending on who holds the telescope. This phenomenon is still unexplained!

Kreludor
Kreludor is Neopia's one and only moon, visible by night (when there aren't tremendous laser battles, that is). Although its atmosphere is thin and its gravity slight, Kreludor is home to an entire pet species, the Grundo, as well as a host of ferocious rock beasts and lava monsters. The primary industry on the moon is Kreludan metal mining, conducted by evil Sloth Drones and a kiiler guardian robot. Steer clear!

Virtupets Space Station
Home to the villainous Frank Sloth and his army of mutants, this space station is always lurking about in geosynchronous orbit somewhere over Tyrannia. Visit if you dare, but stay away from the mysterious lever . . .

Mysterious Satellite
Nobody really knows what this satellite does, but it keeps zooming around through the Neopian sky, always watching . . . Some say it's the command center of Sloth himself, where he fled after being defeated by the Space Faerie.

. Introduction to the Elements .

There are four basic elements present in nature: Fire, Earth, Water, and Air. The elemental faeries acquire all of their magic from these four basic elements. And because good and evil exists in nature, faeries of Light (good) and Dark (evil) also came to exist. Light faeries acquire their magic directly from the Sun while it is speculated that Dark faeries acquire theirs from the aura (or perhaps the "evil nature") of humans and other living creatures. While faeries of Light strive to maintain life, Dark Faeries seek to destroy it.
Only the most powerful faeries can harness power from more than one element. Fyora the Faerie Queen uses a combination of air & light magic and it is rumored that the Dark Faeries Ilere & Jhudora possess knowledge of earth magic. More food for thought: Perhaps Fyora was originally an Air Faerie that turned to Light and Ilere & Jhudora are fallen Earth Faeries that turned to Darkness.
Some believe that the world of Neopia would cease to exist without these magical creatures. Each element is needed to keep nature in a perfect balance.
