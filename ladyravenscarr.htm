Name Formats

Okay, dear, dear PC. Let's talk name formats. (In other words, Fuz and I are going to RANT.)

We do realize that to a certain degree, degrees of name quality can be subjective. We do. Honestly. But if your pet has an underscore or a number? It's NOT wn. If your pet is capped but has 15 letters in its name? It's not VWN. If your pet has more than one word in its name? It's probably not even WN. You can advertise your pet however you want, but advertising your xxxx_xxxx pet as WN will not get you better offers - just frustrate people who click on your board. Okay? Okay.

Now that we've got that off our chests, for the purposes of this historical trades database, here are how we are defining names as seen in the history:

  • Xxxxxx = VWN (one name, on the shorter side)
  • xxxxxx, Xxxxxxxxx= WN (uncapped or capped but long)
  • XxxxXxxxx, Xxxxxx_xxxxxx, Xxxxxxxxxxx, Xxxxx# = DN (multiple words w/multiple caps, multiples words 1 cap, one underscore, one number)
  • xxxxx#, xxxx_xxxx_xxx, Xxxxx###. Xxxx_xxxx## = BN (uncapped + number, capped + multiple numbers, underscore + number)
  • xxxx_xxxx### = vbn (underscore + lots of number or lots of words + lots of numbers)


Sometimes there are pets that fall in between and could be WN/DN or DN/BN. That usually just means the format is iffy and could be interpreted differently; we don't use that designation here, but we do realize they exist.

One last note: Sometimes, formats lie. They just do. It might be short and Xxxxxx but ugly, so it won't trade as well. Welcome to the PC.
