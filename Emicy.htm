Art Tutorials

Human Skulls

Basic Structure

Keep in mind that this is just the way I draw skulls. There is no right or wrong way (unless you're stealing someone's art). This is one way out of thousands and I am by no means an expert. Regardless, I hope you learn something! and please neomail me if you have input you'd like to add of your own! I love constructive criticism.







The biggest brushes are your friend.
Use the biggest, softest brush you can and use it to blend. You can use the smaller brushes, and the more textured ones later. basic shadows and shapes are more important than textures and tiny details! I cannot stress this enough.




Lighting

Fear the egg.

To show the basic of lighting, I'm going to use an egg. Ideally, you should know how to light multiple different kinds of basic shapes (cylinders, spheres, cubes, etc.) in different lighting conditions. However, I don't wanna do that. I've done that enough. lol. So I encourage you to go outside with an egg or basic shape of your own and do this in natural lighting. If you can do this in graphite or with acrylics, all the better. Digital is a great new medium, but you learn so much more doing it traditionally (I have no idea why).

I'm going to use an egg as an example because it's a simple enough shape, with a very slight change of form to demonstrate the nuances of core shadows and how they can effect how the eye sees the shape. On your first try, painting a perfect egg might take 5-10 hours. On your second try it should take half that. And if you've painted as many eggs as I have, or critiqued as many eggs, You'll be able to do it in 5 minutes.
