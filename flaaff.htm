FLUFF activities

During the season, we faeries run many fun activities on the FLUFF boards. Here are descriptions of some of the ones you might see on the FLUFF boards. You do not need to be part of the FLUFF guild to participate in these.

The FLUFFador Cup

This is the FLUFF board competition in which fellow faeries form two teams to compete against each other in each of the Altador Cup games. For more information, see this page.

Awards & Badges

Zombie created special badges to award faeries for specific accomplishments, like achieving All-Star or winning a marathon, and to acknowledge participation in activities, such as the FLUFFador Cup.

Muffin & Food Gifts!

FLUFFians often like to shower their fellow faeries with gifts of muffins, drinks, and other sweets!

Trade Lots

A couple faeries generously put up trade lots filled with Team Faerieland items and cute faerie-themed trinkets for fellow Faerieland members to offer on!

Hurt & Heal

This fun and motivating game on the boards has become popular to play with several FLUFFians. They worked together to bring Faerieland to victory in Round #7 (AC XV, 2020) and Round #1 (AC XVIII, 2023)!

Roleplay, Puzzle, and Trivia Events

During the off-season, ellie hosts some fun events on the boards that involve roleplay, puzzles, trivia, and more!

Missions, Races, and Marathons

Faeries host some fun games and marathons on the boards in order to motivate fellow members. Challenges are posted, and the first few faeries to complete them win a prize!

Missions

Zombie sets a few challenges for FLUFFians to complete. Each mission often has a few slots to allow multiple people to participate, and it operates on a first come, first served basis. Whoever reaches the goal can "claim" the mission, and there are no limits as to how many one person can claim. Such challenges include:

Send in 50 YYB score (1)
Send in 10,000+ SOSD score (2)
Send in 2,000+ SS (3)

Races

FLUFFians compete against each other to reach a set goal. The race ends when someone hits the specified target first, and the winner is thus awarded the Race Winner badge.

Each person keeps track of their own total and posts their cu.mulative score after every few games.

Races look like the following, with the blue numbers serving as examples of scores (and the score format) that a participant might post after every 2-3 games:

SS Race

First to 5,000

(After 1 game total): 600/5000
(After 3 games total): 1875/5000
(After 6 games total): 3650/5000

Marathons

In a marathon, a group of FLUFFians work together to achieve a goal, most often combined game scores or gameplays. Unlike with races, each person posts each new score contribution (usually after a few games at a time) which are tallied by a scorekeeper. The marathon ends when the combined total hits the set goal. Each participant can take a Marathon badge.

Usually run on a separate board, a marathon will look like this:

SS Marathon

0/30,000
Combined Scores

FLUFFian #1: +1200
FLUFFian #2: +1175
FLUFFian #1: +600

===

2975/30,000
Combined Scores

Both races and marathons have a set start time, and there must be at least two players participating. Anyone can start either one at any time and are free to take the appropriate badge once completed. Scores are counted separately from regular score postings, so be sure to keep a record of your personal totals so you can post them on the main scoreboard.
