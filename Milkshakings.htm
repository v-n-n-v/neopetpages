 aya's font page ! * 

Hello, dear reader! My name is Aya, and this right here is my font page, created on February 2, 2018. It's essentially a collection of all the fonts I've made in all the nine years I've been playing Neopets. Though some of the earlier fonts I've made have become extremely cringeworthy to look at over the years, they serve as a reminder of my good and bad times, on this site and in real life. Some of these fonts had been created for previous guilds, some had been requested by friends who have long since quit; some fonts had been created over obsessions I once had, and some fonts have been created to reflect whatever I like as of the moment. It's like a story book.

My font page initially started out as a portfolio, but I decided to revamp it a little bit to make things a lot cleaner (a.k.a my previous font page's layout was terrible). This will hopefully be easier for people to send in their requests. People could never usually tell that I do take them, so after years and years of contemplating about it, I've finally opened up an official page for custom fonts. Also, for anyone just casually strolling by looking for a font, premade template fonts are available! These are fonts that can have their color and sizes easily edited to exactly how you want them. Hooray! c:

Anywho, enough chit-chat. The navigation bar to the left will lead you to wherever you want to go. Thanks a lot for visiting my page! :)

 please read first ! * 

general rules

  • Don't request for fonts I am currently using.
    Unless the font itself has been put on the page of my own accord, every font I use is off-limits.
  • I reserve the right to all fonts on this page.
    At the end of the day, they're still my creations. If I decide to put one of your requested fonts as a template (which is rarely, so probability is on your side), put it onto my portfolio, or use the requested font myself, I have every right to do so even without informing you.
  • Maximum of two fonts within a month, please.
    I have my own life, please don't send me many requests at once.
  • Please don't flood my inbox with unnecessary neomails.
    I acknowledge all requests I get as soon as I receive them. If I don't reply to your request within two days, feel free to send another mail as it may have gotten lost or I may have accidentally deleted it while clearing my inbox. Similarly, any requests that had been sent while requests are closed and any requests that don't follow the neomail form will be deleted. All other kinds of neomails (e.g. questions, concerns) are welcome.
  • Give credit where credit is due.
    You don't have to scream about my page--just don't act like you made it. Linking back isn't necessary but very much appreciated!

things to know before requesting

  • 90% of the time I need a Neoboard pen.
    If you don't know what Neoboard pens are, they increase the number of characters you can put in your neoHTML. You can buy them
    at the NC Mall for 300 NC. I'll still do my best to make things work without one but can't make any promises, especially if it's a custom font.
  • Some things are just impossible to make.
    I'll usually tell you if something's way too complicated to make. These are picture fonts, not an actual canvas. Please keep that in mind. ;__;
  • I don't set any deadlines.
    There are times when I can make a font in minutes, and times when it takes me days because of lack of motivation. I will get to making your request however long that will take me, though. Please be patient with me!
  • Fonts may look a bit different from their screenshots depending on your computer.
    I use Windows 10 + Chrome but make fonts depending on your computer. PC fonts look like my usual style while I have to adapt for Macs. Instead of invisible spaces, all default fonts for Mac users will use either this symbol (░) or dots (.) instead of the regular invisible spaces. If you're a Mac user that insists on a Consolas font (the only font on Mac that allows invisible spaces), please be warned that Mac users without Consolas installed will view your font as a sort of jumbled up mess.
  • Kindness goes a long way!
    It's really not that hard to be nice. You're the one requesting a font from me, after all. I've ignored people who have blatantly ignored my rules and people who have just been downright rude before, so if you haven't heard back from me after a while, that may be why.

word font equation

step 1: choose a font face.



1. uppercase print: maximum of seven letters for non-neoboard pen users;
maximum of nine letters for neoboard pen users;
up to three-color gradients possible;
best option for non-neoboard pen users
2. lowercase print: maximum of five letters for non-neoboard pen users;
maximum of seven letters for neoboard pen users;
letters that go upwards (e.g. k, l) or downwards (e.g. j, g) may cause issues;
up to three-color gradients possible;
would probably need a neoboard pen
3. cursive: maximum of five letters for non-neoboard pen users;
maximum of seven letters for neoboard pen users;
letters that go upwards (e.g. k, l) or downwards (e.g. j, g) may cause issues;
up to three-color gradients possible;
would most likely need a neoboard pen
4. 8-bit: maximum of four letters for non-neoboard pen users;
maximum of six letters for neoboard pen users;
letters that go upwards (e.g. k, l) or downwards (e.g. j, g) may cause issues;
up to three-color gradients possible;
would probably need a neoboard pen
5. quirky: maximum of four letters for non-neoboard pen users;
maximum of six letters for neoboard pen users;
letters that go upwards (e.g. k, l) or downwards (e.g. j, g) may cause issues;
up to three-color gradients possible;
might need a neoboard pen

picture font equation

step 1: choose an image. ✨

  • It shouldn't be too dependent on color.
    A one-color lollipop stills looks like a lollipop while a one-color pizza just looks weird.
  • It shouldn't be too long or wide.
    Think of all the wasted space.
  • It should have a distinctive shape.
    A red circle could be a record button, a clown's nose, Mars, or a flattened tomato.
  • It shouldn't have too much detail.
    Think of something a five year-old child could draw quite easily.
  • Colors should go from top to bottom.
    I can do Thailand's national flag but not France's.


step 2: choose a color scheme.



one color: best suited for intricate images
best option for non-neoboard pen users

frequently asked questions ! *

Can I use [any font I'm currently using]?

  • No, don't even bother asking, please. My greatest pet peeve is someone using the same font as me. It's confusing.

Can I use [any font not made for you]?

  • Sure, but on one important condition: you must mail me beforehand and ask. If I see you using a font without my acknowledgement, I will report you for theft. And please, at least have the decency and politeness to ask first instead of just viewing page source and getting my font that way. *facepalm*

Do you mind if I edit the font you made for me?

  • Of course not! It's your font now, after all. I always add a little bit of room in neoHTML so you can edit your neoHTML font, as well as some room for color and size adjustments in both bars. I don't expect any of you to use the exact same font for an extended period of time. :P

Why does my font look different from your screenshot?

  • It's differences between personal computers. I use Windows 10 + Google Chrome, so if you use Windows 10 + Safari or Mac + Google Chrome, it might look different. Not much I can do about that, I'm afraid--I'm still trying to find a loophole.

Help! I can't get it to copy-paste right, why?

  • It's probably because you're either using Internet Explorer, an old computer, or an older version of a browser. Please switch to more updated configurations (if unavailable, mobile works just as well) and see if copy-paste works for you. If not, neomail me and I'll try to work things out.

Do you make all of your fonts yourself?

  • Every single font on this page has been made completely by me. If you see me using a font that isn't on this page, it is either a font that's been passed around so much nobody knows who the original owner is anymore (in the case of my giant pink cat), or a font made by friends or fellow font makers. Credit for Tubbs, Nyan Cat and Stretchy Cat goes to Lucy, and credit for Cinnamaroll, Pochacco, Chococat, Rainbow and Keroppi goes to Ken over at Canvas.

How do you make those blank spaces?

  • Not sure how it works on Macs, but on Windows all you need to do is turn on Numlock by pressing Fn + NumLk, and then pressing Alt+255 using the number keypad. The number keypad is different from the row of numbers on top of your letters. On old-school keyboards, the number keypad is that block of numbers to the far right.     After I get all the colors I want, I then use Sunnyneo's very handy rainbow font maker tool to get the gradient I want. Here's the link:

    http://www.sunnyneo.com/gradientfont/makerainbow.php

Do you do art trades?

  • I'm not entirely sure how to answer this. I take requests because I find it fun to do, and I don't really expect anything in return from the people I make fonts for. And I'm not really that interested in art, either. Don't get me wrong, some of you guys are massively talented with your creative works, but artworks of my pets or myself aren't really something I'm actively seeking. So in short, yes and no? You can create something for me if you'd like (and that'd definitely be a nice surprise), but it's definitely not necessary.
