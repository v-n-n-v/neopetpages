ADOPTER GUIDE


Adopter

ƏˈDⱭPTƏR (NOUN) ONE WHO TAKES OVER CARE OR USE;
(NOUN) NEOPIAN WHO WAS CHOSEN TO ADOPT A PET

An adopter is the person who is chosen to adopt a pet that is up for adoption. This is who you aim to be! The Adopter Guide is here to provide you with vital information regarding neopet adoptions and application processes, along with tips and resources to help you adopt the pet of your dreams.

Welcome! Are you ready for a full course lesson on becoming an Adopter? Let us get started!

The basic concept of participation in an application process is to apply to adopt a pet that is up for adoption by sending a description of why that pet is desirable by the applicant.

The reason behind an application process is that there is a neopian who wants to find a new, loving home for an old, beloved pet, instead of simply dumping that pet into the pound. Pounded pets may be adopted by the first person who comes across them, and that person may not be an active player, which would leave the pet essentially rotting on an unused account. Due to this fear, many neopians hope to instead direct transfer or pound transfer a pet to a specific person that seems, according to subjective criteria, like a good owner. In order to find this person, the neopian asks if there are others interested in the pet, and then chooses the person viewed as most fitting.

The first step to convincing others that you should be the adopter is to understand how adoptions work and how you can best communicate within your application.

UNDERSTAND THE Rules


Foster

FȮ-STƏR (VERB) TO PROVIDE PARENTAL CARE;
(NOUN) NEOPIAN CARING FOR A PET UNTIL ADOPTION

When an owner decides to put a pet up for adoption, that neopian becomes a foster parent, or foster for short. During that time the pet is in foster care until adopted. Sometimes a foster intentionally adopts unwanted pets from the pound, zaps or paints them, and then adopts them out. Other times, a foster may have had a pet for a long time and decided that pet no longer sparks joy and that it is time to find a new home. Regardless of backstory, the foster's goal is to find an adopter, and the applicant's goal is to be chosen as the adopter. The most important things when dealing with a foster are to thoroughly read any rules or guidelines the foster has posted for the adoption process, follow them, and be polite. However, you should also understand the foster's role and site restrictions.

YOUR BEST BEHAVIOR

TIPS TO PLAY NICE WITH OTHERS

☺15 GOOD BEHAVIORS TO EXEMPLIFY

FOLLOW THESE GENERAL GUIDELINES TO HAVE A GOOD TIME

  • Be completely honest and open about your intentions and your follow through
  • Be yourself with your writing, whether you are serious and wordy, or quite punny
  • Read any rules or guidelines posted by the foster on a petpage or on the boards
  • Check which foster account to contact to ensure you neomail to the right one
  • Make sure trading is allowed and feasible for what you want if you plan to trade
  • Find out the application deadline and ensure you send your application before it
  • Be polite towards the foster and other neopians alike, and treat everyone equally
  • Be supportive of other applicants and understand one of them may be chosen
  • Help in what ways you can if other applicants ask for advice or application help
  • Get advice on the boards in petpage, application, or character chats if you need it
  • Be humble of yourself and your application and ask for help if you feel insecure
  • Make sure you have an open pet slot and an available direct transfer as needed
  • Be an active player, keep your accounts tidy, and check all your layout links work
  • Credit everything you didn't make: code, stock images, graphics, song lyrics, etc
  • Thank the foster for time spent on the adoption process regardless of who adopts


☹ 15 BAD BEHAVIORS TO SERIOUSLY AVOID

IF YOU ACT THIS WAY THEN YOU WILL NOT HAVE A GOOD TIME

  • Seriously, do not lie about your intentions, pets, yourself, your accounts, or anything
  • Do not make false claims that a pet is a permie or dreamie without real intent to keep
  • Do not ignore the posted rules or guidelines or any keyword the foster requested
  • Do not try to ingratiate yourself to the foster with flattery, gifts, or similarly influence
  • Do not beg, whine, or demand that the foster should choose you to adopt, even in jest
  • Do not solicit adoption of a different pet that has not been listed as up for adoption
  • Do not send multiple neomails without permission to the foster or the foster's friends
  • Do not spam the foster with repeated questions like when the pet will be adopted out
  • Do not act like your application is superior or that you will be the one chosen to adopt
  • Do not belittle other applications or applicants or talk poorly of them even in neomails
  • Do not belittle yourself or bemoan that your application is bad instead of asking for help
  • Do not give advice that is condescending or critique an application without being asked
  • Do not go missing especially near the deadline when the foster will contact you to adopt
  • Do not harass a foster if the pet is removed from adoption or if the deadline is extended
  • Do not steal images, writing, or code from others because that is a reportable offense
SERIOUSLY THOUGH

Many fosters try to remain impartial regardless of who is applying to adopt a pet, but if you have been cruel, rude, or even annoying, your application may end up ignored. You should always try to be considerate of your words and their effects on others on the neopets dot com, especially if you're trying to convince a foster that you would be a good owner for a pet. A foster will not be inclined to adopt a pet to someone who seems at risk of being frozen. Be kind to yourself and to others!

At the end of the adoption process, if you are not chosen to be the adopter, feel free to congratulate the adopter, but do not mention your own desires or ask if you can adopt the pet instead. If you are chosen as the adopter, do not brag or make other applicants sad, and do your best to follow through with any pet plans you mentioned in your application, whether you applied to trade or to keep.

FOLLOW THROUGH

Application Risks

YOU APPLY AT YOUR OWN RISK

Unrewarded Time and Effort

THE WORK YOU PUT INTO AN APPLICATION IS A LABOR OF LOVE

First, you must know that there is an inherent risk of unrewarded expenditure of your time and effort. You may put a lot of work into your application, with months spent writing basically a novel, creating graphics, gathering customization items, or fully fleshing out a character, and still not end up with that pet, no matter how badly you want that pet, and no matter how much you love your concept for that pet. To address this, understand before starting that this should not consume your life. Prioritize your health and responsibilities, and treat your application like a short term hobby or creative exercise.

YOU MAY NOT BE CHOSEN TO ADOPT

Painful Emotional Responses

TRY TO NOT GET EMOTIONALLY INVOLVED IN A PET YOU DON'T OWN

Additionally, be prepared to remain detached, or, if you are unable to do so, be prepared to give yourself time to grieve. Although these are pixels, quite often it is easy to become sentimentally attached to a pet you apply for. There is great pain in losing something that was never yours to begin with. I think it has to do with the lack of closure related to something you expected as your future, even if you knew there was no logic behind that expectation. The best way to avoid this emotional hardship is to repeatedly tell yourself at every step of your application that this is not your pet, and this may never be your pet. Still, give yourself permission to love your concept for that pet anyway.

KNOW THERE ARE NO GUARANTEES

the foster chooses the adopter

THE ADOPTER MAY BE SOMEONE YOU NEVER EXPECTED TO BE CHOSEN

The foster may choose the new owner for whatever reason. The adopter is always chosen at the discretion of the foster, and the choice should be respected. The foster may decide to adopt the pet out to a friend, have a friend pick the adopter, pick the first applicant, or even roll dice to decide the adopter. Although no contests or rewards are allowed in picking an adopter, and there are many criteria that are not allowed to be demanded from applicants, the foster can still use any set of personal preferences without mentioning why it is that a certain applicant is chosen. Do not expect that one applicant deserves to be picked over another. Unless you are the foster, you don't decide.

NOBODY DESERVES TO BE CHOSEN

THE FOSTER MAY BE QUITTING

SOMETIMES APPLICANTS WILL NEED TO UTILIZE EMPATHETIC CAUTION

If the foster posted any plans to quit, applicants and friends of applicants should especially be considerate of the feelings of the foster, because in such situations emotions are high and the foster may feel vulnerability or despair. The community should come together in such times to check on that user. Usually this person has reasons behind quitting that are logical, and most often that come down to a need to prioritize offweb interests and responsibilities. However, other neopians should still ask the user whether adopting out a pet or all pets is the best choice, and encourage time to be taken before any possible rash decisions if the situation does seem precarious. It is absolutely fine to apply for the pets up for adoption from a quitting user, but be prepared to give support if it seems needed.

NEOPIANS ARE ALSO HUMANS

the foster may cancel adoption

BE PREPARED FOR THE WORST BECAUSE IT CAN END FOR ANY REASON

The foster may at any point decide to cancel the adoption process and keep the pet instead, or to end the adoption process early, though both of these situations rarely occur due to the detrimental effects on reputation and community. Honestly, the foster could just chuck the pet into the pound free for all and be done at any time because the pet belongs to the foster alone until an adopter owns the pet. The foster has absolutely no responsibility to adopt out a pet, and should never be made to feel ashamed, regardless of promises made or anything else mentioned prior. Fosters should never be harassed if there are changes to plans, even when it seems unfair to applicants. You don't know what is going on in the life or thoughts of the foster. Sometimes plans change.

ADOPTION IS UP TO THE FOSTER

the adopter has the final choice

A PET CLAIMED AS A DREAMIE TO BE A PERMIE MAY BECOME TRADE FODDER

After the adoption, it is ultimately up to the adopter what to do with the pet. The foster may have a suggestion for what should be done with the pet once the pet is adopted, and choose an adopter based on that desire, but that can't be enforced. Adopters have no responsibility to keep an adopted pet or follow through with anything related to that pet, regardless of promises made or anything else mentioned prior. Although such choices may be detrimental to reputation, and future fosters may avoid applications from that adopter, the adopter should never be harassed by the foster or other members of the community if there are changes to plans, even if it seems rude or deceitful. You don't know what is going on in the life or thoughts of the adopter. Sometimes plans change.

FIND PETS UP FOR ADOPTION

RELY ON THE FOSTER MORE THAN RUMOR

5 WAYS TO TELL IF A PET IS UP FOR ADOPTION

  • The foster has created a thread on the neopound board to advertise the adoption
  • The foster posts about the adoption in a species specific chat or adoption thread
  • The foster has an adoption notice on the petlookup or petpage or on another pet
  • The foster specifically tells you so, or posts about it on a neoboard that you read
  • The pet is listed at an adoption agency or an adoption page or directory petpage

UFA PETS MIGHT BE MENTIONED ELSEWHERE BUT DON'T TALK ABOUT IT

You may also want to double check that the pet is still up for adoption, and not canceled or already adopted, before you start putting work into your application. Look around a bit or politely ask.


SCOUTING POUND CHAT

THE DRUDGE OF SCOURING THE NEOPOUND BOARD FOR UFA THREADS

Hang out on the neopound board and refresh until you see a thread with one of the following words: adopt, adoption, ufa, ufqa, ufvqa, or apply. If you find one, look inside to see whether the foster wants a board post application or neomail application, and what rules and guidelines are posted. Feel free to ask questions in the thread if you are confused about anything. Most fosters and other applicants will try to answer promptly. There's usually someone there to bump. The hardest part of this method is that you'll need to take time out of your life to spend refreshing the main board. Hahaha. Good luck.


ADOPTION AGENCIES

HOW TO NAVIGATE AND ADOPT A PET LISTED IN AN AGENCY

An adoption agency is a directory petpage for pets that are up for adoption. Such pages are not the same as directories that list pets up for trade. Adoption agencies may be run by a team, a guild, or one person. Sometimes the pets listed are only owned by the foster or members running the agency, and sometimes the agency allows any foster to send a neomail to request having a pet listed as up for adoption with the agency using a listing form. Some agencies only list pets of a certain type, such as unconverted, battledome, or pets with color specific clothing. Each agency is run differently, and you will need to read the rules to understand how to apply for a listed pet, as well as follow any listed guidelines for what the agency is looking for in an adopter to approve an adoption. Be respectful and only apply for a pet you actually want to keep, unless retrading is allowed or the agency allows pets to be adopted for the color specific clothing. The one consistency is that you will need to neomail the specific foster who owns the pet up for adoption, as per regulations. Read each agency page carefully.

DREAM PET LISTERS

ADVERTISE WHICH PET YOU ARE LOOKING TO ADOPT

Dream pet listers allow prosepective adopters to list a pet they would love to adopt. Usually the dreamies are listed by color and species. Through these pages any fosters are able to look through what users are seeking, and if they own a pet listed as a dream, they are able to contact that user and ask if that person would like to adopt their pet. Sometimes the foster will happily hand over the pet immediately, and others will be interested in asking the lister why the pet is desired. Like adoption agencies, these sites have their own ways to list and their own sets of rules. Read each page carefully.

PROJECT PAGES

SHOW THE WORLD THAT YOU ARE SERIOUS ABOUT THIS DREAMIE

A project page is a petpage devoted to a user's dream pet. These are different from petpage applications in that the user is not using the page to apply for a pet. Rather, the page is used to advertise the intention to one day find and aquire such a pet. Sometimes a project page will include a disclaimer that the user was not forced to make the project page, but, since it is not an application for any specific pet up for adoption, there is no real need to do so. However, if the project page is updated to include information about a pet that is up for adoption, a disclaimer will need to be added.

Many project pages actually start out as a petpage application for a pet that the user was not chosen to adopt. Rather than discard all that work, the applicant may choose to turn the page into a dreamie project and cultivate it until the day a pet is found that fills the hole in the forlorn applicant's heart. All information regarding a specific pet name must be removed if the page is converted into a portfolio.

Generally a project page will otherwise follow the same mechanics of a petpage application as far as content and layout. Think of these pages as a shrine to a wish. The user may have a well thought out concept for the pet, and may have a fully fleshed character, writing, graphics, illustrations, and convincing reasons why that pet is desired. Often these pages are made in the hope that senpai will notice how much work was put in, and the proof that the user is serious may convince other users to help in the search for that dreamie. Rarely, a foster may see a project page for the specific pet that foster has up for adoption and contact the owner of the project page about applying for the pet. A project page should be made for self indulgence without expecting that a star will fall from the sky, but sometimes dreams do come true if there's a foster out there who really loves a project page.


UNSOLICITED INQUIRIES

IF A PET HAS NOT BEEN MENTIONED AS UP FOR ADOPTION THEN LAY OFF

While it is somewhat permissable to send a polite neomail to ask if a pet is up for trade if the petlookup is a blank basic and has no Not Up For Trade signage, the same is absolutely not true with unprompted inquiries about adoption. It doesn't matter if this pet is your dreamie, and it doesn't matter if this is the most important name on the site to you and no other variation will work, because you can't barge in and ask someone to give a pet to you without even a trade offer incentive.

Absolutely under no circemstances should you ever cold mail a pet owner to ask if that pet is up for adoption. This is rude to users who do not know you, and can be especially frightening to someone who is unfamiliar with the Neopound. Not everyone has Not Up For Adoption signage on pets, but contacting a user who has never mentioned putting a pet up for adoption to ask for that pet is considered extremely uncivil. It can also be classified as begging, particularly if you mail repeatedly.

Never cold mail a pet owner to ask if that pet is up for adoption.

BOARD POST APPLICATION

BOARD POST APPLICATION

GENERALLY REQUESTED WHEN A PET IS UP FOR VERY QUICK ADOPTION FOR 1-2 DAYS

With board posts it is best to be succinct about yourself and your ideas. You have limited characters, but try to fit it all in one board post. Boards often get many posts at once, so if you and others try to break applications into multiple posts the board will quickly become confusing and frustrating to read.


MAIN BOARD POST APPLICATION CONTENTS
• INTRODUCTION: Your alias, self description, and account information
• PET PLANS: Why you want this pet and what you would do with this pet
• WHERE TO SEND: Which account has an open slot and direct transfer
• EXTRAS: Something else you want to share if you have room, like a petpage
• REQUESTS: Anything else the foster asked for, like a certain emote to be used

NEOMAIL APPLICATION

PETPAGE APPLICATION


PETPAGE APPLICATION

SOMETIMES SENT BY CHOICE WHEN A PET IS UP FOR ADOPTION FOR 1-2 MONTHS

If the foster is willing to read a petpage application, you can choose to make one as long as you include a disclaimer that you were not forced. It is against site restrictions to submit any petpage application without such a disclaimer, as it is considered by The Neopets Team to be extreme and not in the spirit of a healthy community to expect such applications are necessary for adoptions (source).

A petpage will give you a lot of room to describe yourself, your plans, and express creativity with art, whether written, drawn, or designed. If the foster accepts petpages, then finish your petpage first, then send a neomail to the foster with a link to the petpage, explain that it is a petpage application, and include a brief message about yourself. The neomail with the petpage link shouldn't be long.

In your petpage application, you may include as little or as much content as you like, but if your application could moonlight as a ten page essay, then you should first ask the foster if long petpages will be read, as it may be overwhelming to read, especially if it is long blocks of text unbroken by images, much like this Adopter Guide here. Good use of design elements helps solve that issue.

You will need some type of html, hypertext markup language, coded layout that includes css, cascading style sheets, within a style tag section, and a main body section that uses the css as reference for how to make the content look when you write content within the body of the page. You can read html and css tutorials and code your own layout from scratch, or you can use a premade layout. Many users offer free premades that you will be able to use as long as you give credit.

You should note that modern coding often doesn't work on neopets because this site breathes with the screams of 90s era dial up muffled by terribly mismanaged filter systems. If you're at a complete loss, head to the Help neoboard and make a thread to ask for layout coding help. Sometimes people in pound chat may also know the applicable workaround solutions to the illogical mysteries of this alternate dark timeline realm. Your code may not end up winning any beauty contests. The struggle is indeed real. Do your best to make the layout function across mobile and different screen resolutions.

You may keep your petpage content as simple as you would a neomail application, albeit with more room to explain, but you must additionally solve the issue of the layout, and include a disclaimer and credits section. If you want to give more detail, there are other topics you can include, such as petpet plans, customization plans, fanart, and information on your other pets. Furthermore, you could create a character out of the pet and describe the character such as with a biography and short story.

• MOODBOARD: Set up a board with images and words that evoke a desired mood

  • Write credits for yourself and others in the credit section
  • You can credit where the content is on the page instead, or as well
  • You can use credit lines below the content itself or use a hover credit
Make crediting easier for yourself by writing down where you found each content element you plan to use, and who created it, as you gather it. It's tedious to backtrack to find the source again.

You should still make sure any famous content you're using is safe for Neopets and under fair use.

NEOPOUND TERMINOLOGY

Top 50 Terms to Memorize

JARGON NECESSARY TO UNDERSTAND WHEN CHATTING ON THE NEOPOUND BOARD

Adopter One who adopts a pet
Foster One who owns a pet with intent to adopt out
Fostering Gathering pets with the intent to adopt out, often after zapping
Zapping Using a secret laboratory ray to alter the color/species of a pet
Application A method used by a potential adopter to be chosen to adopt
App Shorthand for application and used much more frequently in reference
App Chat A thread on the neopound board for applicants to discuss applications
UFA Up For Adoption; Usually with rules or guidelines posted by the foster
UFQA Up For Quick Adoption; With a deadline in a day, week, or month, often by neomail
UFVQA Up For Very Quick Adoption; With a deadline in a day or week, often by board post
UFT Up For Trade; Used when a user wants to take offers to trade a pet for a pet
Shuffle Thread where pets, often unconverted, are listed for bids of the same pet types
Auction House Thread where pets are listed for bids of any type to trade within a limited time
PPA Personal Pet Auction; thread by a single user for bids to trade within a limited time
Pound Transfer A way to move a pet to another account with no safety or guarantee
Direct Transfer A way to move a pet to another account with a sure and safe transfer for a fee
Custom A custom paint/morph using neopoints, premium, or fountain faerie quest
Premium Custom A custom paint/morph using a method in the premium neopets service
Fountain Faerie A quest giver who allows access to a rainbow fountain pet color change
FFQ Fountain Faerie Quest; A quest used to give a pet a fountain color change
RW Real Word; A pet with a definable word as a name such as Adopter or Gathering
RN Real Name; A pet with a name used as a proper noun such as Sasuke or Europe
CW Compound Words; A pet with a name of two words such as Timelord or BadWolf
MS Misspelled Word; A pet with a misspelled word as a name such as Theaterical
BD Battledome; A pet with trained stats for the battledome
SBD Starter Battledome; A pet with beginner stats for the battledome
UC Unconverted; A pet with art from before the conversion disaster of 2007
T# Tier followed by the number; A user created value system for unconverted pets
VWN Very Well Named; A pet name that is short, capitalized, and pronounceable
WN Well Named; A pet name that is easily pronounceable with only letters used
DN Decent Named; A pet name in compound format or with one underscore or number
BN Bad Named; A pet name that is unpronounceable with underscores or numbers
VBN Very Bad Named; A very long pet name with numbers, underscores, and odd case
4L Four Letters; A pet with a name that is four letters long
4C Four Characters; A pet with a name that is four letters and numbers long
4# Four Numbers; A pet with a name that is four numbers long
Cvvc A name four letters long starting and ending with consonants around vowels
Cvcvc A name five letters long with alternating consonants and vowels
Xxxxxx A name formatted with the first letter capitalized
xxxxxxx A name formatted fully in lowercase
XxxxXxxx A name formatted with a capital in the middle
Xxxxx_## A name formatted with an ending in underscore followed by numbers
NC Neocash; A neopets virtual currency purchased with an offweb national currency
NCC Neocash Chat; The colloquial acronym for the NC Mall neoboard used to trade nc
AC Art Chat; The colloquial acronym for the Art neoboard; Or Altador Cup acronym
PC Pound Chat; The colloquial acronym for the pet based Neopian Pound neoboard
BC Beauty Contest; An official neopets popularity based illustration contest
NM Neomail; The primary neopets postal service used for interpersonal communication
PP Petpage; A free neopets hosted webpage using a pet name as a domain name
PL Petlookup; A page to view a pet and information related to that pet
UL Userlookup; A page to view a user and information related to that user
Portfolio A petpage used to give information about a user and pets or characters
Project A petpage dedicated to a concept or character for an unowned dream pet
Premade A user designed and coded layout created for free layout use by neopians
Adoptable A user illustrated graphic intended to be used by neopians to decorate pages
Makeable A user illustrated lineart intended to be colored by neopians to depict a pet design
Pound Surf The act of refreshing the Neopound Adopt A Pet page to find abandoned neopets
Pound Catch The adoption of an appealing abandoned pet from the Neopound Adopt A Pet page
Dreamie A dream pet that has been sought after for many months or years with adoration
Permie A permanent pet that a user intends to keep for years, or until neopets shuts down

If there are any additional terms you see that you don't understand, don't be too shy to ask someone on the board to teach you. Despite the notorious pound drama, most users are very happy to help you learn and settle into the community!

I wrote this guide to help others adopt their dreams. I've had a lot of help over the last two decades to achieve my own, and I gotta pay it forward. I seriously do adore my pets, and I'm far from humble regarding the characters I've made for them, but I wouldn't have a single one of my beloved permies without others supporting me, and fosters trusting me and believing in my dreams. I've had to learn several of the taboos in this guide the hard way, from crying with utter heartbreak over unobtained pets, to the cringey mistake of asking an adopter if they'd trade. I was around in the days before required petpages were banned, and I've been through an adoption that was a two year process and involved referrals from the community, half a novel, and an entire gallery of illustrations to become one of three finalists, only for the pet to be pounded. And I'm still bitter 13 years later. I've adopted a pet and then had people say that I didn't deserve it compared to the other applicants. I've adopted a pet with great excitement and then a month later realized I'd lost interest and had to find a new adopter. I've been the applicant who sent too many neomails, and I've been the foster whose inbox was filled. I've had a foster cancel the adoption process a week before the deadline, and I've been the foster who took days to make a decision after the deadline. I've made the mistake of not crediting correctly. I've had art stolen by users who didn't understand it was wrong. I feel like I can honestly say I've seen it all. However, now I've been able to share that experience to help others have an easier time on the path to adopting their dreams. I hope that's what you'll be able to do now. There's no point in wishing on a falling star. Wish on yourself instead and take matters into your own hands. Wait for the chance to adopt, and, when you see it, go forth and catch that falling star!

I believe you'll do well in your next application!
