Test results of Glowing Cauldron in one-player battledome...250 uses:

10 reflected fire icons (see note) = 223/250 (89.2%)
drain hit points = 18/250 (7.2%)
spit smoke (nothing) = 9/250 (3.6%)

NOTE: Since it's figured as reflected icons rather than normal damage, you DON'T get extra icons if you fierce/berserk with it.
NOTE: In 1-player, the cauldron uses your opponent's strength when calculating damage (unless your opponent is frozen). For example, my testing pet with no strength boost does the EXACT same damage with it as my powerful pet. Also, it CAN be defended in 1-player (read about reflection from my site on my UL). Damage is also the same whether you defend, use an ability, berserk, and so on since it is considered reflected damage.
NOTE: In 2-player, it uses the strength boost of whoever hits Go first.. The hit point drain functions like the battle plunger's: it does not give them to you, it's just unblockable damage (it used to take stats from your opponent and give them to you but it doesn't anymore).
SUMMARY: All in all, with a strong pet I'd strongly prefer a kiln to the cauldron. More icons (11 rather than 10), you can get extra icons with fierce/berserk, useful in both 1- and 2-player, no chance of doing nothing, etc. However, a weaker pet could benefit substantially with this weapon especially going for avatars or DoN challengers.
