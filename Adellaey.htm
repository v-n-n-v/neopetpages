Why Rate?

Rating a palette is a way to let me know how much you like or dislike a palette. If a lot of people give a certain palette a low rating, then I know that I need to either modify the palette to improve it, or remove it completely. Positive ratings contribute to the Highest Rating section where users can see which palettes others have had the most success with or just like in general.


What You Can't Rate

Frequently Asked Questions

01. Why can't I rate User Submitted palettes?
The main reason I allow palette ratings is so that people can let me know how I'm doing with my palettes. If certain palettes get a low rating or negative comments, I know I need to modify the palette (i.e., change colors) or remove the palette completely.

Since I did not create any of the palettes under the User Submitted section, it would not be right to allow ratings on these palettes and then to alter a palette that is not mine to change. Kleur is essentially just hosting these submitted palettes.

If you want to send a nice compliment to the user that created it, by all means, do so. But do not harass the user if you do not like the palette they created. Please be respectful and courteous.
02. Can I use the coding for the way you display the color palettes? I'll credit you.
No, please do not use my coding, with or without credit.
03. How did you come up with the site name, Kleur?
I wanted a short, unique, and aesthetic site name so I looked up translations of the word "color." According to a translation site, "color" translates into "kleur" in the Afrikaans language.
04. Are you aware that there are other creative-commons sites out there that offer color palettes? You can't ask for credit for the colour palettes you obtained from there.
I am aware that there are other sites that offer a large variety of color palettes. As stated in my credits in the Sitely section, "all palettes were created by me, unless they are in the User Submitted section," therefore I can and do require credit for the ones I created. You may find all my palettes posted on another color palette site, under the name Aurantiaceous.
05. How did you make the little "notepad?" Can you teach me?
All it is, is a textarea with css style attributes. Please do NOT steal my coding, nor the image, nor the exact same look of it. If you do not know how to code one, that's too bad; I will not teach anyone how to code it. I've taught people how in the past and they refuse to give me credit of any sort, and copy the design exactly like mine. It's frustrating and takes away the uniqueness of something I created for my site.
06. Can you please make me a layout?
I do not take layout requests under any circumstance. Please do not ask me to make you a layout. Visit Plethora for a list of sites that take layout requests.
07. Why do I have to give credit for using a palette? It's just colors.
True, they are just colors. However if you're making the decision to use a palette it's either because you needed a color scheme and were having difficultly choosing colors, or you simply liked a combination you see here.

Either way, you did not come up with the color palette. Believe it or not, I've invested a lot of time creating these palettes. You need to give credit where it is due.

If you still don't understand why you need to give credit, read this.
08. What programs do you use to make the palettes?
I use Adobe Photoshop CS5 for it's eyedropper tool and color picker tool. You do not need expensive programs like Adobe Photoshop to make your own color palette. There's some great, free, online tools that can help you to make your own.
09. Why are the palette ratings and date added missing on the palettes in the Seasonal, Holidays, and Newest section?
Mainly because the palettes that are under those sections can be found alphabetically with all the additional information. The palettes in those mentioned sections are just sorted palettes. I just want to provide you with the best palettes, in my opinion, that fall under those sections without any other details/information.
10. Why do some of the palettes have white text? Are they special or something?
Nope, not special! It's done to make it easier to see the hex color code against darker colored backgrounds.

Colors have the ability to dramatically affect moods, feelings and emotions. It's a power tool used in real-life, and on the web. While some colors mean different things to different people, there are certain connotations of colors that seem to be universal.

Connotations of the color black: Chaos, chic, darkness, elegance, evil, fashionable, formal, intense, mournful, mysterious, powerful, rebellious, serious, solidarity, sophisticated, strong, stylish, trendy, wicked.

Black can also be associated with death, fear, night, and Halloween.

Hex Color Code

#000000


With Other Colors

  • Be careful when using black with very dark colors. If the shades are too similar it can diminish readability of text in layouts and/or graphics.
  • For a conservative look, pair black with greys or a light blue.
  • For a sleek and sophisticated look, try using bright oranges or blues over a black background.
  • You can really play up any bright colors with black, such as shades of yellow.
