Technical Notes:
- To place items at different angles press the Shift key while rotating. This will let you turn by 10 degree increments.

- To place items on top of walls try rotating to -180 or any diagonal position.

- Neohoming becomes expensive fast, try to look for cheaper alternatives when a plan becomes costly.
e.g. Wock til you drop posters instead of zen drawers.
Also, Loriengal9 has compiled a list of cheaper furni items here: Takashi462

- It is easier and more efficient to plan out a home before beginning to build. Cuts down on demolition later.




T.V.


Step 1: Assemble parts. Approx. 3-4 wock til you drop poster, 1 appropriate poster or window, 4 zen drawer.
Step 2: Overlap wock posters to form a black bottom.
Step 3: Rotate wock poster and overlap smudge on bottom of other wock posters.
Step 4: Place in screen of television.
Step 5: Rotate and place zen drawers to serve as sides of television box.
Step 6: Place other zen drawers on top but behind screen to serve as back of television. Place finished T.V. appropriately in room.

Step 1: Assemble parts. Approx. 4 brown petpet bed, 1 iron mirror
Step 2: Rotate 1 of the petpet beds so that the small square will be at the top. Place one petpet bed on top.
Step 3: Place another petpet bed just slightly offset of the previous petpet bed.
Step 4: Place another petpet bed just slightly offset in the other direction of the previous petpet bed.
Step 5: Place mirror to serve as screen.
Step 6: Place finished laptop in room in a position so that the excess will be covered up.

Piano


Step 1: Assemble parts. 7 zen drawer, 2 wock til you drop poster, 8 small table lamp, 1 suitable chair.
Step 2: Rotate the lamps so the large side faces down. Overlap the table lamps alternating leaving marks.
Step 3: Rotate the posters to -90 degrees and place underneath the lamps so that no white is showing.
Step 4: Place two drawers facing each other to form a piano leg. Create two legs on top of the lamps.
Step 5: Rotate the drawers so that a small white line appears and place on top of the lamps.
Step 6: Place one drawer on top of the two others in the previous step.
Step 7: Place chair and any character to finish the piano.

Fireplace


Step 1: Assemble parts. 1 rock chair, 6 stone box, 1 hewn stone sofa.
Step 2: Place two stone boxes on top of rock chair. One at 90 degrees, the other at -90 degrees.
Step 3: Place two more stone boxes on top of previous boxes. Move them slightly inward and upward.
Step 4: Repeat previous step.
Step 5: Top the entire unit with a hewn stone sofa.
Step 6: Place against wall in room to enhance the illusion of height.

General Stair Tutorial


Explanation: There are many kinds and ways of doing stairs. This will be a general explanation of the details for successful stairs.
The Necessary Parts are what makes stairs stairs. It's not necessary to use what is pictured here, but something of the sort will be required.
The Unnecessary Parts are what are used in the above picture to make that design work. They may or may not be required for a different design.

Necessary Parts: Steps (Classic Dining Table), Bannister (popcorn garland and small table lamp and violet shelf unit)
Unnecessary Parts: Shading for doorways (Wock til you drop poster and zen drawer), Height helper (fireplace, round green table, zen mirror), Flooring (dried bamboo mat).

Placement: For the stairs to look as they should a ground level should be determined. This is easiest accomplished by aligning with the bottom of the walls in room.
For a look of height as the stairs go up or down, the steps should be aligned to go above or below the ground level. If there is difficulty moving items there see Technical Notes above.
Also, it is important to look at the coordinates of the stairs to make sure all of the stairs are equidistant and staggered apart from each other for straight-looking stairs.
