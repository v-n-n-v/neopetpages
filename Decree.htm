Biography


Full Name: Drakicho
Alias: Drak
Age: 27, according to records
Species: Anti-existence (human)
Gender: Male
Eye Color: Silver
Hair Color: Silver
Alignment: Chaotic neutral
Likes: Seeing new sights, coffee, browsing social media, freedom to do as he wishes
Dislikes: Unnecessary battling, boring tasks, responsibilities, crowds of people invading his personal space and getting too close to him



Soul Emblem Type: Entropy / Creation
















On Aethar, Drak takes the form of a tall, young man usually donning a long, flowing coat. The end of his usual purple coat is slightly raggedy with soft white lining, with a similar shade of white on his wrist cuffs. Under his coat is a plain white shirt and long black pants that cut slightly above the ankles of his black boots. He has long, silvery hair, platinum pupils, and is almost always found bearing a calm, calculating face, usually accompanied with a grin. This is his "illusion" form, which allows him to traverse the city more conveniently. He upholds this form through the use of his soul energy.

In his true form, one takes quick notice of the sword lodged through his chest, with the blade piercing out to the front. His purple tail is also revealed, along with the weapon seemingly attached directly into his right hand. The right side of his face is a husky grey with a gaping hollow eye socket, with a strange, white eye suspended in the center on three vein-like strands. His mouth on that side of the face is also replaced with a row of large sharp, overlapping teeth. Tendril-like appendages are attached to his right shoulder, and two metal claws are attached to two fingers on his left hand. A shackle and chain is attached to the end of his tail, and a chain is also wrapped around the blade in his chest.

He can also transform himself into a dragon-like creature with many of the features of his true humanoid form (which is his natural form). He uses his dragon form in rare situations, such as personal travel, and prefers to fight on his human form when possible.

















Drak is generally calm and reserved, easygoing, and tends to take things lightly on the surface. However, he takes his life very seriously and is prone to making paranoid decisions when he genuinely feels that his life is threatened. He holds immense power within his body (however, it is "sealed" by the sword in his chest) and recognizes the responsibilities that come with it. Nonetheless, he rarely shows this side of him openly and goes about his daily life quite cheerfully. He is completely mute, and therefore tends to communicate in gestures or writing, or by allowing Cer to be his voice.


Drak can seem unsympathetic or uncaring at times, as he is still fairly proud of himself and his position. He is very cautious when it comes to opening up to others, but does show some degree of genuine concern for certain individuals; this tends to be rare, due to his distant past. Prior to battling Keio, the shadow king, he befriended two individuals which he placed a lot of trust in. One lost their life, and the other was nowhere to be seen, and Drak felt responsible for both of these outcomes. Nonetheless, he carried along their words of wisdom, and their desire to live in a world where they didn't have to fight for their lives constantly. To this end, Drak reconnected with the world soul, Dea, soon after he took the throne.

He tends to do things himself (and in his own, unconventional way) but he is also found to be quite lazy in other mundane tasks and chores, which he typically leaves for Cer. In his home realm (the anti-existence realm), he is treated as high royalty.

He detests combat (and fighting in general) and prefers to avoid it if possible. He will not hesitate to slip away and flee from a heated situation.

However, this does not mean that he will hesitate to take a life if needed, although he usually does not condone mindless, meaningless slaying. Cer also remarks that by avoiding aggression, Drak will not be tempted to unleash his full power, which he chooses to remain sealed for various reasons. The primary reason is that he is known to drop into a bloodthirsty frenzy after the release, and he is unable to fully control his power at that state, in fear of repeating the same mistake he did in the past, in the anti-existence realm.















Most of Drak's power is sealed within his body, but he still holds the capacity to exhibit certain abilities. Most noticeably, he is able to project illusions directly to any individuals or to all individuals surrounding him (he can perform both simultaneously). The nature of these illusions is simply visual and can still be broken with ample concentration; knowledge of the presence of an illusion may distort or weaken it as well. It also seems to not affect certain individuals, but it can be transmitted through screens (so cameras will typically display the illusion, unless the individual viewer is capable of breaking the illusion). This is his main method of concealment or escape, however it falls apart if the illusion is ineffective against his opponents.

Drak is also connected to Cer telepathically, and they can communicate with each other utilizing one of Cer's abilities. Drak's true right eye also has the ability to share sight with Cer: if activated, Cer is capable of seeing through Drak's eye telepathically and from any distance. This ability is typically used to aid in Drak's escapes, allowing Drak to relay his location and situation in real time for Cer to adequately assist in the venture.

His most powerful capability is his ability to manipulate soul energy. When in his illusion form, he's still capable of channeling this energy in the form of shields, shockwaves, etc. but is most effectively channeled through the two weapons on him: the sword on his chest and the weapon on his right hand. He can conduct a small amount through the claws (or rings, in his illusion form) on his left hand, which is usually enough to perform simple defensive or evasive maneuvers. He can fire a concentrated bullet of his own soul energy through the weapon on his right hand, which can actually be quite destructive when coming in contact with a physical object or entity. He only uses this in extremely dire or specific situations.

In addition to this, his own soul energy provides him with a fair amount of immunity to pain. Injuries and wounds can still damage his body, although it does seem to provide him with a higher physical tolerance of said wounds. He also tends to recover more quickly than other individuals, but it is not instantaneous.









Place of Residence


After their meeting in Aeolus City, Drak and Cer moved to Hallow, and they currently share a small apartment on the second floor of a café in the center of the city, which seems to be largely for the reason of convenience.

Occupation


Drak does not hold a permanent job, however he typically acts as an informant of sorts for clients of all backgrounds and affiliations. He may also take on other certain one-off jobs, like a delivery, depending on the nature of the request. His connections to others in the underworld are solely professional, but he does this in order to stay better informed of possible threats.

In between jobs, he tends to enjoy quietly walking around Hallow (usually with Cer tagging along). The places he tends to visit are seemingly random, although it may also be simply to avoid being cornered in one spot by someone that is seeking to dispatch him. Although he does not travel often, he likes exploring the hidden crevices and sights of the nearby cities.













Cer

Cer is almost always seen alongside Drak. Cer, after several life-changing encounters, devoted his life to Drak, assisting with his tasks on the surface, although in public they simply act like very close friends. The two are also capable of communicating through telepathy (thanks to one of Cer's abilities) and sharing sight (thanks to Drak's unique eye), so communication can be performed extremely exclusively between the two in silence, however Cer still prefers to speak aloud in response, often appearing as confusing, one-sided conversations. Cer, because of this, also occasionally acts as Drak's interpreter or "voice".

Sen

Drak is found to be the anti-existence of another powerful being, Sen, the Lord of the Abyss, and therefore shares power levels. They hold a distrustful relationship, but are obligated to protect each other, since due to the nature of anti-existence, when one of them perishes, the other will as well. His lighter attitude deeply contrasts with Sen's serious and worrisome nature, and their methods of facing combat (Sen would prefer to crush their enemy instead of fleeing) often become an issue, but if they are forced to work together, Drak would usually agree to Sen's methods, even though he would openly express his detest.

Others

Drak is also found to have a few connections to certain information brokers located in several of the largest cities in Aethar. Rain, who was created from a project he indirectly funded, is his personal guardian (alongside Cer) although she usually remains hidden until called upon.

There are also a few individuals (namely Talor and Kai) seeking to eliminate Drak for various reasons. As a consequence of this, he usually makes an effort to be extra cautious of his surroundings in order to avoid a fight.

Biography


Full Name: Ceran Tyri
Alias: Cer
Age: 26
Species: Existence (human)
Gender: Male
Eye Color: Blue (modified)
Hair Color: Blue (naturally blond)
Alignment: Lawful neutral
Likes: Anything about the anti-existence, learning new facts, starry skies, suits
Dislikes: Physical pain, prison cells, being ignored, his lack of strength



Soul Emblem Type: Entropy
















Cer is a skinny, young man with a bit of a frail build, bearing square, thin-rimmed glasses and long dark blue hair. His natural hair color is actually blond, but he prefers to keep it dyed. His usual choice of attire is a black suit or casual jacket. Shackles are attached to both of his ankles, dragging the short chains as he walks. He wears plain white shoes below the shackles.

On his right hand, two black crystal-like attachments are fused to the top of his right thumb and index fingers. At will, these crystals can form excessively long, thin blades. This allows him to perform specific surgery practically anywhere, utilizing the blades as tools. It also potentially provides him with a means of self-defense, although he does not have the strength nor the speed to utilize these blades effectively.

His two human eyes were replaced with two Artifacts, which are modified magical eyes enchanted with very specific purposes that utilize both the wearer's and the receiver's soul energies. His left eye socket holds the Eye of Foresight, and his right eye socket holds the Eye of Clarity. To most people, however, the fact that his pupils look modified is usually passed off as special contact lenses.

















Cer, before meeting Drak, was a typical hardworking student in Aeolus City. His father was a surgeon as well, although his difficult and strange work schedule meant that he was rarely home for his only child, and even after his mother's disappearance when he was 12, Cer never really established a meaningful relationship with his father. Due to this, he became secluded and introverted, burying his feelings in his extensive medical studies. Nonetheless, he inherited his father's extremely detailed and careful handling in surgery, as well as the composure and patience. Because he found refuge in his pursuit of knowledge, he became a very curious individual, going lengths to learn more about the topics that interest him, and going about meticulous and occasionally dangerous methods.

One particular subject that he came across early after he became a certified surgeon was the idea of anti-existence, after operating on a particularly abnormal patient. They were both obscure theories at the time (it remains as so to the present day), and so substantial publications and reports on both topics (especially the latter) were extremely scarce. Nonetheless, the concept deeply interested him and he began to obsess over attempting to gather more information on them, to the point that it would occasionally interfere with his work.

After a fateful meeting with Drak, however, and the replacement of his human eyes with the Artifacts, he found a meaningful purpose, and decided to follow Drak (after all, Drak did save his life). He continues to slightly obsess over collecting knowledge that interest him, but he places priority on the safety and comfort on both of them.

He is terrified of physical pain, which was worsened due the events leading up to and after his meeting with Drak, and constantly revived through the fact that he is able to predict when he is about to receive said pain (due to the nature of the Eye of Foresight). He places great trust (and perhaps over-reliance) in Drak, however, and he knows that Drak is aware of this fear.

After their encounter, Cer began to open up a little bit more, becoming less shy and introverted, especially since he plays the part of the "voice" for Drak (as he is mute). It also seems that Drak's easygoing nature brushed off a little bit onto him. Although still socially awkward, he is generally positive and even impulsive and silly at times. However, he is not afraid to question things openly, although he clearly pays more respect when Drak is involved. He occasionally acts as the voice of reason when Drak is prone to making illogical decisions, especially when he feels threatened for his life.

It seems that Drak chose Cer specifically for numerous reasons, one being his unwavering loyalty, another being his determination. It also seems like Cer may have a specific soul energy "phenotype" that resonates well with his own, and was proven to be crucial when Cer realized his true purpose. His biggest fear is letting down Drak, and this builds up as he realizes his weaknesses. Despite this, he strives to become stronger so he could fulfill this fate, and repay Drak for what he did.















His Artifacts are his only source of supernatural power. As a pair, they utilize both Cer's soul energy as well as the receiver or target of interest. In most cases, such as predicting the future around his immediate area, he uses Drak as the specific receiver and therefore he is able to carry out the task without much or any strain on his own soul energy reserve. This also allows him to speak with Drak telepathically very easily and naturally, and at any distance.

The Eye of Foresight was extracted directly from Kai the raven god and slightly modified to be able to better control its unique properties. Drak obtained it at some point and claimed it for his own, and created a prototype eye to go along with it, the Eye of Clarity.

The Eye of Foresight, located in his left eye socket, allows him to predict the immediate future. In most cases, this is no longer than a few seconds, up to a few minutes in advance. Distances much longer into the future require substantially more energy, which Cer cannot carry out on his own. The prediction takes the form of a vision that flashes before the actual moment in time is reached.

The Eye of Clarity, located in his right eye socket, allows him to read minds, to a very specific extent, and speak telepathically with certain individuals. Mind-reading requires specific prerequisites, such as eye contact, as well as a lack of a fortified mind (with enough concentration, the receiver can break the flow of thoughts). In addition, the data is only given as audio, and only relates to the thoughts that are directly surrounding the moment of contact. However, despite this, this becomes invaluable for effective escapes.

The eyes, since they are instilled with Drak's soul energy, also allows Drak to share his sight with Cer, through the use of Drak's true right eye. Cer can also activate either or both of the Artifacts while this is occurring (Drak's sight also counts for the prerequisite of the Eye of Clarity), allowing for Cer to relay details in real time. When activated, the eyes glow a faint light blue, and his irises retract.

It seems that the Drak's true purpose for instilling the Artifacts into Cer was to force him to eventually predict the king's death, or events surrounding it, in order to (theoretically) indefinitely delay his end.









Place of Residence


He currently shares a small apartment on the second floor of a café in Hallow with Drak. He does most of the chores and housework around the living space, and tends to like to keep things organized.

Occupation


Cer currently works as a surgeon on standby and on-call for a local hospital. He is only called upon to perform extremely detailed and meticulous surgery that others had no success operating on. This often involves beings with abnormal existence states (such as anti-existence) or otherwise troublesome individuals. This allows for Cer to keep track of such beings and keep tabs on them in the occasion that they become a specific threat. Due to the specific demand of his job, he gets paid generously, allowing Drak and Cer to live quite comfortably.













Drak

Cer is most closely tied to the anti-existence king, Drak. In pursuit of some private reports, he was led into a trap by a shady group in the underworld. They threatened to end his life, however, Drak appeared to intervene, and assisted Cer in his escape. As they made their way out, Drak suddenly knocked Cer unconscious, knowing that having to protect him would slow him down immensely.

As Cer began to regain consciousness, he found himself tied to a table. He looked to his side in fear, staring deeply at the numerous surgical tools that he had so well familiarized himself with. The room was dark, aside from the blinding lights that shone on him. He could not make out any of the faces that operated on him. Cer's body originally rejected the Artifacts, causing his entire self to scream out in pain. Eventually, the pain overwhelmed him and he faded back into unconsciousness.

He awoke again still on the table, but he had become untied. All of the others had left the room, but Drak remained, stepping out of the darkness to greet Cer and introduce himself for the first time through telepathy. Cer struggled off of the table in disbelief and backed himself onto the floor. There, he took the time in attempt to make sense of the situation. At that point, Drak revealed the weapon on his right arm and aimed it directly at Cer. The surgeon scrambled back in fear, only to be stopped by a wall behind him. The room was still dark, but to his disbelief, he was able to see much more clearly than before. He raised his arm in futile self defense, only to see a vision flash before his eyes - Drak fired his weapon, directly towards his head. He cringed, knowing it was too late, but ducked nonetheless. The vision immediately dissipated, and he found himself back at the moment right before the weapon's activation. Confused, he ducked and leaped forward instinctively, a split second before the weapon itself fired, and he narrowly avoided the burst of destructive soul energy. He heard the wall explode and crumble behind him, and although he felt the shockwave behind him, he was left mostly unharmed.

He turned around in disbelief, staring at the pile of dust and rubble behind him, knowing that it could have well been his own gruesome end, if it wasn't for that strange vision. It was there that he began to truly realize that something had happened to him that changed his life forever.

After that, Drak smiled and silently handed him the file that he attempted to reach. Cer glanced down at the report in complete shock before letting it slip from his hands, at that moment realizing who he was dealing with all along.

Others

Since he never had any deep connections with his parents, he sought to find a usefulness and purpose, which he found with Drak. He does not speak much with Drak's other guardian, Rain, but she, as well as others such as Dea, acknowledge Cer out of respect of the one who placed so much trust in him.

Cer also holds a long-time friendship with the travelling circus ringmaster Actu, the latter being extremely intrigued with the strange energy emanating from the Artifacts.

Biography


Full Name: Unknown
Alias: Sen
Age: 29, according to records
Species: Existence (human)
Gender: Male
Eye Color: Purple
Hair Color: Black
Alignment: Lawful neutral
Likes: Battling, destruction, traversing space
Dislikes: Pure chaos (like the Insanity), meddlesome birds, arrogant bullies (no matter what their social status is), liars



Soul Emblem Type: Void
















In Sen's natural form, he is seen as a towering beast of pure dark matter, about the size of an average planet. He is capable of navigating through the vacuum of space in this form. He resembles an aquatic animal, mostly consisted of black dark matter, but occasionally bearing several pieces of armor. He uses two large, clawed pectoral fins and two smaller, leg-like structures, also clawed, mostly to assist in maneuvering in space, but also to grasp solid objects if needed. One primary dorsal fin lines his spine, and two other smaller fins are located on both sides and roughly halfway down his body. His tail fin is aligned horizontally like a whale's, and is thin and pointed. He is capable of opening his jaw to reveal a black hole within the core of his body, in order to clear the remains of a destroyed planet. Two pairs of dark red eyes are embedded on each side of his head, floating in a sea of dark matter.

When he descended onto Aethar, he took on a human form, locking away his power into his left arm, which is further secured by a large metallic cylinder. Sen is seen as a relatively tall and well-built male with light skin. He has spiky, pitch-black hair partially dyed purple, partially veiling the mask on the left side of his face. The white mask contains the remnants of one of his original eyes, which grants him remarkable vision. His right eye contains a dark purple iris. He often wears a black vest, trimmed with gold and tapering off into a single "tail" on his left side. There is a sharp fin-like protrusion along the edge of his vest that runs over his left shoulder. He is usually seen with a dark purple scarf with black tips. He wears an armored black glove on his right hand, used for both defense as well as offense.
The cylindrical structure around his left arm contains a pointed armor plate, much like the one on his right arm, curving upwards. It seems like the cylinder contains several layers below its main casing, but he is almost never seen without his cylinder when wandering on Aethar. However, in the rare situation that he is seen without his cylinder, his left arm is seen engulfed in his signature dark matter, ending in sharp claws.

















At first, Sen believed that his sole existence was to destroy, but destroying in order to create balance, rather than chaos. One day, however, he destroyed a particular world that had living creatures, souls, living within it, something that is extremely rare on the cosmic scale. Not knowing the result, the souls that he absorbed into him, however, tore into his own sanity and mind and tortured him with nightmares of fear, memories, and guilt. He felt true, raw emotions at that time, something he has not experienced before, and it scarred him. When he arrived on Aethar much later, he recalled this nightmare, but he descended onto the world with a desire to protect it, in attempt to atone for what he did.

He avoids pointless destruction, at least on a larger scale. However, he very much enjoys the thrill of competition and loves battling. Even with his power almost completely locked away, he is still very capable in combat, especially hand-to-hand combat, despite the handicap on his left arm. He is also fairly strategic, yet plays very aggressively. He will not back down to a challenge, but rarely loses nonetheless. He is still capable of unleashing a bit of dark matter energy from his right hand – which in itself is already quite destructive – but he only uses this in extremely dire situations.

Down to his core, however, he still craves destruction, and although the sealing magic on the cylinder is usually enough to mostly prevent him from unleashing that instinct, he constantly fights the temptation. This is further complicated by the Insanity, which slowly deteriorates his control over his chaotic desires.

He is quite cold on the surface, and fairly untrusting. He's fairly judgmental and weighs heavily on first impressions, although one is still capable of changing his opinion. Although he does not actively seek opponents, his reputation in the underworld of Aethar is usually enough to draw aggression towards him. He will usually not go as far as to take the life of an aggressor, but he has no specific objections against it if he feels that it is what the other deserved. Due to his past, he prefers to not be present for an individual's last moments.

He is searching for something on Aethar, and is the reason why he locked away his power. The other reason why he decided to arrive was to investigate and rid the world of the Insanity aura before it spreads any further, which he has a personal agenda against since it challenged his willpower. He is well aware of the anti-existence but tends to not meddle in affairs regarding them.















Dark matter itself is very destructive, and even one drop of dark matter is capable of creating an explosion capable of leveling buildings. It is because of its extremely destructive nature that Sen decided to lock it away.

The cylinder on his left arm contains especially powerful sealing magic, preventing him from unleashing his full destructive capabilities. However, although he is able to unlock this seal on his own, the nature of the lock requires his sanity to be intact, and therefore acts as a safeguard to the Insanity, at the cost of having immediate access to his power.

The glove on his right arm allows him to channel a bit of his power through his hand for combat, similar in function to the blades on Drak's left hand. Although most attacks leave him unscarred, any damage done to his human form heals extremely quickly.

In his natural form, however, he is capable of manipulating his dark matter powers to the fullest, from firing a concentrated beam of energy to summoning dark matter orbs to act as protection or as missiles. He is capable of consuming celestial bodies much larger than himself by creating a powerful gravity core to slowly rip it apart enough for him to absorb it himself, which he did so often in the past.

He transforms anything he consumes into pure energy, which he either adds to his own power or releases it back to the vacuum of space for the purpose of maintaining realm stability. Since his descent onto Aethar, however, he has refrained from consuming planets at all.









Place of Residence


Sen doesn't really have a specific place of residence, and instead acts more like a traveler, although he mostly frequents Hallow and Aeolus City. He seals himself in a sort of pocket dimension when he decides to retire for the night, able to be opened and closed only by himself. He visits Hallow quite often, mostly to keep close tabs on his anti-existence counterpart. He also owns a drinking establishment in the downtown district in Hallow, which he visits on occasion to collect information from his assistants, as well as general upkeep of the facilities.

Occupation


When he is not managing his tavern in Hallow, Sen advertises himself as a very capable mercenary, and collects most of his revenue from this method. Many institutions are willing to hire him on the grounds that he is capable of leaving a merciless wake of destruction wherever he is assigned to attack. However, he only takes jobs that he feels fits within his sense of judgment. He also collects money from underground competitions, hidden from most of the city.











Aupera

In the distant past in the vastness of space, Sen encoutered a specific Verinu by the name of Aupera. They convinced Sen that they knew his true purpose. After he began destroying worlds, they fed on the remains like a parasite. However, after the destruction of the soul-filled world, Sen retaliated against them, in attempt to free himself again. He attacked Aupera, and they fled, mortally wounded. As it turned out, they crash landed on Aethar, where they took on a different persona to hide from Sen.

Ili

Sen works closely with the Observer, Ili, in maintaining balance and stability in the various inter-connected realms, although he mostly works from the surface realm. Ili treats Sen with much more respect, since he mostly works on his own accord. However, Sen is usually willing to assist Ili, recognizing the celestial being's unique knowledge of sealing magic, such as in capturing and providing power to seal the demon god at his most powerful state. They mostly carry out conversations through telepathy within the vacuum of space, but the nature of these conversations is almost strictly formal.

Drak

Sen is the existence counterpart of the king of the anti-existence and the most powerful anti-existence, Drak. They share power levels when in their unsealed forms, but the nature of their sealing magic differs slightly (Drak has sealed his soul energy mostly via the sword on his chest, while Sen sealed his within his cylinder). His whole character idea revolved around the whole cool, calculated glasses-character stereotype. In order for him to connect with the disappearances, I made him a master of realms, which also allowed me to potentially use this as a link to the imprisonment of the gods of time. All-in-all, I felt that this character simply fell into place quite easily, and I was very happy with the idea of another evil birb for my flock.

➤ Rati // The Spirit of the Galleon

Glossary of Terms

➤ Soul Energy: The form of "power" in this universe, it can be utilized in various functions, such as to cast "magic", amplify physical combat capabilities, and affect emotions and thoughts. Soul energy users also tend to have longer lifespans, as it counters natural aging. Comes in various forms ("types"), and is most effectively channeled through metal.

➤ Aethar: The primary world in which the story takes place; an Earth-like planet with modern technological developments, including the internet. Several developed cities with unique cultures, largely at peace. Many traits are similar to Earth, and contain humans and animals alike. Magic (soul energy use) is rare, but not unheard of.

➤ Surface Realm: Often used interchangeably with Aethar, but it also contains the surrounding galaxies. Its opposite is the Anti-Existence Realm.

➤ Existence: A normal individual born or created on the surface realm (which includes Aethar). This also includes all creatures and animals.

➤ Ordeal / Anti-Existence Realm: The "underworld" of the story, which is a realm/world with a landscape filled with dark purple, flesh-like structures and bone, although most are as tough as minerals and rocks are like on Aethar. Anti-existence inhabitants are created or formed here. Pink oceans and rivers of Vior are scattered across the land. Raw Entropy soul energy is visible in the sky.

➤ Anti-Existence: An individual that primarily resides in the Ordeal, and is created in one of two ways. The most common anti-existence are created by the world soul Dea, and are typically weaker. Anti-existence can also be formed from coalescing soul energy mirroring a powerful soul energy user in the Existence realm, creating a Soul Link between the two individuals. Most anti-existence are capable of independent thought, but size and shape can vary dramatically.

➤ Soul Link: A link that connects a soul energy user on the Existence Realm with one that was created to mirror them in the Anti-Existence Realm, as a result of the pact that created the Ordeal. Only when an individual in the Existence Realm acquires enough soul energy power does the possibility of a Soul Linked individual arise, and not all users in the Existence Realm have an anti-existence counterpart. Power levels are mirrored, as well as large-scale changes such as major injuries. The end of one is the end of the other, and links cannot typically be severed by any means (only one exception has occurred).

➤ Verinu: The original, primordial beings that inhabited the universe at its beginnings. They are the only individuals capable of wielding Origin soul energy, and are always very powerful soul energy users, as they survived the harsh environment of unstable wildspace. Notable Verinu in the story are Keio and Dea (who agreed to a pact connecting Dea and Eche together as mirroring realms), and Otaye.

➤ World Soul: A being that inhabits and acts as the soul energy "core" to the world or realm. Aether's world soul is Eche, as the anti-existence realm's is Dea. The two are also linked in a special Soul Link in which massive-scale destruction or modifications to one are mirrored in the other. The world soul is directly able to control natural structures rooted on their surfaces, but is limited in their range of sensing what happens. When a living individual dies, their soul energy is naturally reabsorbed into the world soul.

➤ Time Gods: The two immortal time gods, Kai and Mourn, came into being alongside the Verinu, but they were born with the burden and power to oversee the timeline. Kai watches the future, as Mourn observes the past. The two time gods did not meddle with the inhabitants of Aethar at first, but took special interest in the world as it developed. Kai fell from grace when he abused his power to take soul energy from the dying.

➤ Non-Existence: This title refers to Keio, the previous "Shadow King" of the Ordeal. During the revolution that overthrew his rule, he "wiped" his existence from both realms, permanently shifting the timeline and removing his "existence" from the flow. Specifically for the anti-existence involved with Keio, this essentially resulted in a memory reset regarding Keio, and Drak became the original and only ruler of the realm. In his new, fractured form, he continues to flicker into existence, but as a complete anomaly, only allowing select individuals to retain memory of him.

➤ Co-Existence: This title refers to Rain, who is an artificially created being combining the essence of an anti-existence and existence, who are typically never capable of being hybridized. As a result, her physical form is fairly unstable.

➤ The Seven Aspects: In the ancient past, the Aspects were powerful beings that the realm-shifters (Otaye) looked up to (some, worshipped) as they traversed through the cosmic realms of existence. Although they recognize that there are other beings out there that may be more powerful (and ancient) than them, they were designated based on their powers and their personalities. See below for more details.

➤ Vior: The light pink liquid that comprises the main standing "water" in the Ordeal. As raw Entropy soul energy often seeps into the Vior, it has innate healing properties, and certain locations around the Ordeal contain notably potent Vior.

➤ The Shroud: The outer borders of the Ordeal, furthest away from the central capital city of Drakar. Often obscured by clouds and storms. More dangerous feral anti-existence beasts reside in the Shroud, and there are rarely any permanent settlements, with several nomadic groups forming the primary inhabitants of the Shroud.

➤ Soul Emblem: A soul energy user's unique "profile" of their soul energy, manifesting the form in which their soul energy appears internally, although it may not always match their outward appearance. Summoning one's Soul Emblem puts their soul energy on full display, completely vulnerable, but it can be used to connect directly to another soul energy user. It always takes the form of a silvery diamond prism, marked with the soul energy of the one who summoned it.

➤ Soul Weapon: Weapons that are infused with some level of soul energy or capabilities to channel soul energy. Notable named Soul Weapons of history are Soulfear (held by Dyserve) and Voidheart (held by Chrono). Drak's sword is a specially created soul weapon that was forged separately, and several other soul energy users use some form of metal weapon to channel their soul energy.

➤ Artifacts: Specifically used to refer to the Eye of Clarity and Eye of Foresight, two powerful artificial eyes that are currently in the possession of Cer. Although knowledge of their existence is fairly well-hidden to the public, those keen enough to realize the Artifacts' potential seek to obtain them.

➤ Soul Resonance: Not to be confused with Soul Links. The level of sync that two soul energy users (or two individuals in general) display regarding sharing or linking soul energy. This is an abstract measure of effectiveness and can also vary from time-to-time between two specific individuals, and does not affect outward emotions.

➤ Common: The universal language spoken on Aethar. Other languages and dialects do exist but on a much narrower scale. Written, it is displayed with letters of an alphabet.

➤ Pyre: The universal language spoken on the Ordeal between anti-existence. Written, it is displayed in a series of glyphs or runes (although written Pyre in the Ordeal is much less prominent compared to written Common on Aethar).


Soul Energy Types

Entropy (light blue): The most common soul energy type, as it is soul energy in its purest form, and can be seen freely drifting in the Ordeal's atmosphere, like an aurora. Entropy soul energy, as the name suggests, is flexible and unstable, and can be melded for multiple uses by various users. Its most recognizable form is a wispy, light blue.

Essence (green): "Life energy", or simply, the world soul of Aethar, Eche's, form of soul energy. All life born on Aethar contain some amount of Essence energy, although only specialized users can take advantage of their own (or others'). There may be certain factors that amplify or modify Essence energy to make a user more powerful. Its most recognizable form is a lightning green.

Death (orange): The raven god Kai's specialized soul energy, which has been spread and manifested in the soul energy weapons crafted during the First Demon War by the demon god Ishi. Death soul energy is a version of cursed, corrupted Essence soul energy, and therefore actively counteracts against most other types of soul energy like a poison. Its most recognizable form is a thin branching, vein-like orange.

Insanity (red): The demon god Ishi's specialized soul energy. Different from Death soul energy in the sense that it is not a specifically corrupted form of soul energy, but rather is a completely different aura created from the demon god's own twisted emotions and power. Those that are afflicted with this or carry this aura in some way typically display symptoms of insanity. Its most recognizable form is a faint mist-like red.

Rune (yellow): A special form of soul energy that draws from methodology (and a sufficient power source) instead of specifically one's own soul energy reserve. Although a Rune soul energy user can use their own soul energy, it does not require it, as the way the runes are drawn determine its effect and potency. Created by the Runecarver, Zero, but his research was scattered through time, leaving only specialized users to pass on its methods. Its most recognizable form is a cra.cked yellow, often with light beams emanating from an active rune.

Origin (white): Soul energy in its most primal form, only usable by Verinu, as it instantly transforms into Entropy energy if transferred. Origin soul energy is the collected form of the untamed soul energy of the universe's beginnings. It does not display a specifically recognizable form aside from being a pure white, as it combines and supplements a secondary soul energy type.

Creation (light purple): This is the spark of soul energy for most anti-existence, as this is the world soul of the Ordeal, Dea's, form of soul energy. However, very few are able to actually wield Creation energy as it is often transformed to a different type (usually Entropy) within an individual if used. Drak is notably able to control this due to his connection to the world soul. Its most recognizable form is a fire-like light purple.

Void (black): The soul energy that resides in space in the form of black holes and similar anomalies, notably wielded by Sen, the Lord of the Abyss. Void soul energy is simultaneously the control of space and the power of destruction that comes from it. In its raw form, it is extremely volatile and causes an explosion in contact with any material surface, akin to antimatter. Its most recognizable form is a blob-like, inky black.

Memory (magenta): The Timeless One, Mourn's, specialized soul energy. He is the only individual capable of using this energy type, as it is formed from abstract, coalesced memories of the past. It is typically only visible to those that are involved with the memories associated with it, unless Mourn is using it to amplify his combat capabilities, where it appears visible to all. Although its most recognizable form is glowing magenta orbs, Mourn can spin this energy into a glowing thread.

Mist (dark purple): Specialized soul energy by the anti-existence Talor. Much like Insanity, it can permeate as a gas, and allows for Talor to directly influence or even manipulate the minds of those that are affected by it. When concentrated, its recognizable form is a smoky, cloudy dark unsaturated purple, but it can be hidden to be nearly invisible to most normal humans.
