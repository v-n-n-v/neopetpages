Changing Colors

In the provided codes you will quickly notice that everything is gray. Why? Because it just makes things easier when editing templates :3 Every color is assigned a hexcode with 6 numbers. For example, black is #000000, blue is #0000ff, red is #ff0000, etc. All image editing programs have some sort of color picker to find the right hexcode for the color you want. If you don't have an image editor, there are many resources online that proved hexcodes for you.

Backgrounds

Background CSS can also be shortened! The order for backgrounds is color, image, repeat, position.

Body" refers to the entire page, but you can also change the background of specific sections by replacing the body selector. (selector is the section you are styling usually positioned in front of the "{" and declaration is each style you add to it like color: #000000)

Border

Now to my favorite part: BORDERS! It might seem boring to some, but I think borders add a little something to the design of some layouts. Let's start with the shorthand order: width, color, style.

Yeah, out of habit I tend to switch the color and style, but it works either way so I leave that up to you. ;) The different border styles are: solid, double, dotted, dashed, groove, ridge, inset, & outset. Have fun experimenting with different border styles, colors and sizes!
