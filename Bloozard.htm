Is the NT every other week now? Why?

Yes. The Neopian Times is published every other week now. This change was made to accommodate a smaller staff and a smaller user base. At the time this change was made participation in the NT was low, and the editors now have more responsibilities than just managing the NT and/or other creative contests.

I've added "Return to Weekly NTs" to the suggestions list under whishlist with the understanding that a few conditions must be met for it to be possible to return to weekly issues

Where's the submission form for the NT?

Right here https://www.neopets.com/ntimes/nt_submit.phtml
You can find it at the bottom right corner of any page of the Neopian Times!

When's a good time to submit to the NT?

The current NT is every other week. If you are aiming to submit a work for a specific issue, do not submit until after the previous issue has been released. For example if I want to get into issue 998, I should not submit until issue 997 has been published.

It is not currently recommended to submit immediately after the issue comes out. The editors clear the queue/backlog after publishing each issue. If you submit too early you may accidentally be cleared with the other old entries. Wait a couple hours to be safe. Saturday and later is usually clear.

It is currently recommended to submit no later than Monday the week the NT publishes (4 days before the NT comes out, not counting Friday), though chances are still decent if submitting on Tuesday or very early Wednesday. Lately acceptances have finished going out by Tuesday/Wednesday. This is not a hard rule! The editors schedules change each week and sometimes acceptances are still going out Thursday/Friday. If you have an entry ready to go right before the NT is published, it's still worth submitting but your odds will be lower!

Submissions tend to go out in waves. There is often a wave of acceptances the first week of a cycle, then another wave the second week. Themed issues will sometimes have more waves. Issue 1000 had one wave, where they kept everything secret until right before publishing. Again, this is not a rule, just an observed pattern.

This is all based on personal experience and observations from the NT submission + reply tracking board on the Neopian Writers neoboard. Check in to see when people start getting their NT letters!

When Can We Start Submitting for Milestone Issues?

Milestone issues will have the same submission period has regular issues. DO NOT SUBMIT UNTIL THE PREVIOUS ISSUE HAS BEEN PUBLISHED UNLESS TNT SAYS THEY'RE TAKING EARLY SUBMISSIONS

I personally recommend including in the comments box your intent for the entry to be for the milestone issue. Eg. "For 1000
This does not guarantee publishing in the milestone issue. If you decide to submit early, it's also less likely it will be published before the desired issue (but not guaranteed!)

See Question above for more info on submission times. Visit the Writers neoboard if you have more questions!

How many times can you submit per issue?

There is no official cap. But personally I would advise against more than 3-5. It can take a long time to review works. Submitting too many at once will slow down the editor more than increasing your chances.

How many times per issue can you be published

Only one publish per issue per person! Sometimes mistakes are made and someone might be published twice by accident.

How do I add collaborators?

You can have up to two other people collaborate with you on an entry (3 total!)

Add their names to the comment box. New rules state you must also add what they worked on. For example a comic collaboration might look like this "userA:Writer userB:Art". (the submitter should include theirselves in this list and what they did)

Can I collaborate with more than one group?

Each person can only be published once per issue, regardless of if they're a collab partner or the primary submitter. Collaborating with multiple groups could cause secondary groups to be disqualified from that issue because one person is being published already, and the secondary groups will need to resubmit for a later issue.

What's the Great Collab?

The Great Collab is different than regular collaborations. They are themed issues held once a year where the theme is chosen by users. Volunteers collect the names of participants and work with the editor to get as many people published as possible. Being on the list does not guarantee publishing, and not being on the list does not exclude participants so long as they're still on theme. This was something that users started doing on their own long ago, working together to all submit something with the same topic to the same NT, and TNT eventually was kind enough to make it an official event.

You can still collaborate with others on your entries as usual.

The next Great Collab Topic is Machines in Motion for the NT on August 9th, 2024. The deadline to submit to the user list is July 26th, two weeks before the NT will be published. (You can still submit after that as an independent participant. Everyone will still submit through the usual submission form).

Read more about the great collab on the Neopian Times Collab Page or by swinging by their neoboard.

How do I know my entry was submitted successfully?

  • When you first submit you will be taken to a page with blue text saying "Your entry "ENTRY NAME" has been submitted successfully
  • If you do not see this page, try resubmitting again with the EXACT SAME TITLE (Many browsers offer a drop down list of previously used titles to select from so you know it's the same) and resubmit your work. If it's in the system it will tell you, you've already submitted a work with that title

How come my article/story submission won't go through?

If the submission form is rejecting your entry try:

  • Check your word count. If it's over or under it may fail to send
  • Check your HTML formatting. It should use square brackets (see 'How Do I Add Formatting' question below)
  • The filter may or may not be counting your formatting as word count. Try increasing you word count. Or decreasing if that doesn't work.

How come my comic submission won't go through?

There are a few common causes to a comic submission being rejected by the submission form (not by the editor)

  • Check the format of your image. It must be .jpg or .gif (.JPGE will NOT work!)
  • Make sure it's less than 200kb. Sometimes it will say it's 200kb but it's actually 200.1 and that's still too big. I recommend 198kb as a safe cap.
  • Make sure your comic is 470px wide or less. Occasionally the system will bug and accept the entry anyways, but it's safer to keep it 470px or under.

I got a "Too Many good Entries" rejection neomail. What does that mean?

It means what it says on the tin. There were too many good entries that issue and the editors were not able to publish them all, usually due to time, or wanting to ensure there are enough entries for the next edition too. This response is especially common if you've submitted a non-themed piece to a themed issue. If you have submitted the same work multiple times and still keep getting TMGE, consider checking over your work to see if you can improve anything, or changing the timing at which you submit the work.

What does TMGE mean?

TMGE means "Too Many Good Entries". It is a common neomail response when your work has been rejected. See above.

Can My Work be Held Over to a later issue?

No. The NT not longer does hold overs. If you did not get into the current issue, you will have to resubmit.

When Should I Resubmit?

  • If you've received a TMGE neomail it's safe to resubmit. They may still be clearing the queue if you submit too soon though. Wait an hour after receiving the neomail to be safe.
  • If the NT you submitted to has been published, and you haven't heard anything back try resubmitting. If it tells you that an entry with that name already exists, add (RESUBMIT) to your title or change it in some other way. Wait a few hours or a day after the NT has been published before submitting. They might still be clearing the backlog.

How do I submit a thumbnail for comics?

Do not use the thumbnail submission box. It is currently broken Nov 22, 2023. Instead host your thumbnail offsite. It must be 150x150px. It can be .jpg, .gif OR .png (thumbnails only! the comic itself must be jpg or gif!) Include the url link to the img in the comment box. OR post it on a petpage, preferably at the top where it is easy to find or on a page with nothing else on it. If you run out of comment space you can paste only the last part of the url containing the pet's name. EG. /~bloozard would be used to send the editor to this page.

How do I submit a thumbnail for Articles/Shorts/Series?

Include a link to your thumbnail in the comments box. This can be an img link or a link to a petpage hosting it (make sure it's easy to find, top of the page). Same as comic thumbnails, it must be 150x150px and can be .jpg, .gif or .png.

How do I add formatting to written works? (Articles, Shorts, Series)

Use SQUARE BRACKETS [] for code. Still follow HTML coding rules, but replace all the angle brackets with square brackets instead. The editors will replace the brackets when publishing with html brackets.

How do I add pictures to written works? (Articles, Shorts, Series)

Use [img src="IMG URL HERE"] You can include images from on site, or host your own offsite and use that URL if you have an image you or your collaborator made yourselves. You may not use non-official art made by others without their permission.

What's not allowed in the NT?

  • Excessive or graphic violence or bloodshed: fighting can still happen! But keep it PG!
  • Graphic description of death. Death is hard to write for the NT. It can be done, but it must be alluded to. See Editorial 727. This is an old editorial though and the current editor may interpret the rules differently. Things like "the character died then woke up as a zombie/ghost" can be fine if it's not too detailed for instance.
  • Romance. This rule has been relaxed recently. Romance should not be the plot of the story, but it's usually fine to refer to boyfriends, girlfriends, parents, spouse, husband, wife, etc. Some stories describing a date have made it in in recent times, without ever calling it a date.
  • Real world brands are a no. Sometimes people will reference art programs they use for how to articles, but I can't recall to what extent they're named.
  • Neopets are people too. They should not be depicted as mindless animals, even if they're quadrupeds or an owner's pet. They can speak and have opinions and feelings! (yes, as TNT reminds us. Even Unis acting as mounts)
  • Species colours that don't exist on site: you can get away more with this in comics as a joke or unique character design SOMETIMES. But in general if you're writing a story, you can't have an Avocado painted Aisha, because that's not a colour option for aisha's, and you can't have a plaid painted anything because that's not a colour option.
  • Big secrets for games, avatars, hidden places, places that don't exist *cough* etc. " (direct quote from handy tips page, not sure how much this one has relaxed over the years)
  • Discussion of scams, frozen accounts, or similar."
  • Anything written by anyone other than you. Please do not submit someone else's work for any reason, ever, period." (This includes AI. AI is not considered to be your own work. AI work is not allowed in creative contests)

Also check the official Handy Tips page for the neopian times!

What about Technology? Is That allowed?

Yes! Despite the handy tips page saying otherwise, as of Editorial 946 much more technology is allowed in the neopian times!

How do I submit a Comic Series?

One at a time per issue using the regular comic submission form!

The old method for submitting comic series was to submit the whole series all at once, through the normal comic submission form. This meant each page needed it's own submission labelled Part 1,2,3, etc. The editor would then review the whole series all at once and put the extra parts on hold if it was approved.

Hold Overs are not currently a thing, as the backlog gets cleared after every issue, so we have to submit each new part after the previous part gets published.

There is no guarantee that all parts will be published one after the other. You might experience some issue skips, especially for themed issues if they're too full.

Are they still accepting puzzles in comics?

After not being seen for months, puzzles showed up again in issue 1004, the Illusion day issue of the NT. So it would seem the puzzles are still accepted, but either fewer people have been submitting puzzles, or they've had a reduces priority when publishing, as no other have appeared since.Jun 14th, 2022

Can we submit from our side accounts?

No, not anymore. In Editorial 777 it was stated that we could submit from side accounts for normal issues that don't award prizes. But now all issues award prizes so it can be assumed that we can no longer submit from side accounts. This has not been stated officially, but would line up with other creative contest rules. (Beauty Contest Can be submit from side accounts, but Art Gallery cannot)

Editorial 936

There are always at least three Continued Series that are included in each edition of the Neopian Times. When one of these three series ends, another is chosen and added to the "New Series" section. Since Continued Series can run for many editions and tend to be the longest pieces of writing submitted to the Neopian Times, they require the most time to edit and set up. If we started a new series each week, the Continued Series section would fill up too quickly and be far too overwhelming for the editor. If you want to improve your chances of being accepted to the Continued Series section, I recommend paying close attention to the current running series and submitting your series once one of the ongoing Continued Series is wrapping up. We will also start holding over new series entries once the existing continued series only have a few parts left!

PETPAGE NOTE: These days we often have more than three series running! Especially after collab editions! But it is still a loosely followed pattern with series publications.
