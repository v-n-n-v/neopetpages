welcome to my little hut. ahh, i guess you came here to have your fortune told, eh? well... here it is...

You get the Island Mystic avatar when you go to the Island Mystic Hut on Mystery Island and receive a fortune with the word "Kyrii" or "Kyries" in it. That's easy enough, but ever since 2017 I had wondered, is there a pattern or explanation to how and when you get an eligible fortune?

what we did

People who visited the Island Mystic Hut would share their fortunes with me, either via a board on the Avatar Chat or by sending me a Neomail. If they didn't want to share their fortune but got the avatar, they could just post that instead.

I kept track of two things: the four-letter strings of usernames that got a Kyrii fortune each day, and the fortunes themselves that people got.

what we know now

In September 2022, the Island Mystic algorithm was figured out, and I am sunsetting this project. See the Solved! tab for more information. If you still need the avatar, feel free to Neomail me and I'll try to help you figure out when you'll get it.

 
I started tracking the Island Mystic in October 2017 because I wanted to see if we could figure out when a certain username would be eligible for the avatar. I was hoping that the mechanics would be similar to the Turmaculus and that a username would be eligible every so many days, or on a certain day of the month, or something like that. It quickly became clear that that was not the case. I'm not a computer programmer or a data scientist, and in the last year or so of the project I had gotten to the point where I knew I was not going to be able to solve it myself based on the information we had.

Fortunately there are people in the world who have better skil.ls in this area than I do! You may have seen off-site that some brilliant folks combined the data collected here and information about the Island Mystic algorithm to figure out when username strings will be eligible. So far their calculations seem to be accurate. I am very proud and relieved that this project has reached its goal and that the data EVERYONE submitted has been helpful. We collected dates and fortunes from at least 1,126 usernames and 977 unique username strings over the course of five years.

This project and board were sunsetted October 12, 2022 and I will no longer be tracking fortunes or the dates that people get the avatar. This petpage will remain online indefinitely.

I would like to thank Dice and juvian, my friends (particularly 1337r3st0ck4) and the Jellyneo staff who were great supporters of this project, and everyone who checked their fortunes and contributed information over the years.
 

what we knew

  • A fortune is eligible for the avatar when it includes the word "Kyrii" (singular) or "Kyries" (plural). The Neopets pronunciation guide lists the plural of "Kyrii" as "Kyrii," but Jellyneo has a list of all possible fortune phrases, and only "Kyries" appears in the list. Oddly enough, "Kyrii" does not.

  • You can try for the an eligible fortune, and the avatar if you don't already have it, once a day on your main and/or your side accounts.

  • When you get the avatar, you should get a "Something Has Happened!" notification on the page.

  • You can get a new fortune each day even if you already have the avatar.

  • If you change the site language, you can get two different fortunes each day, one in English and one in any other non-English language. The non-English fortune is a completely different fortune (not a translation of the English fortune), meaning that you can essentially have two chances on each account to try for it. However, all the non-English fortunes are translations of each other, so there's no benefit to trying multiple other languages a day. It can also be tough to get the site to switch back to English and stick; some people have reported success with changing the language at the NC Mall rather than on the Island Mystic page.

  • Fortunes are assigned based on the first four letters of your username, which I refer to as a "string." For example, the users thelastparticiple and thelarkascending would get the same fortune on a given day because they both start with the same string, "thel-." The users thelastparticiple (thel-) and thefirstdeclension (thef-) would not.

  • However, multiple username strings are eligible for the avatar on the same day. So even if thelastparticiple and thefirstdeclension get different fortunes, it's possible that both will happen to include the word "Kyries" and both users would get the avatar.

  • Your Neopets username can include the letters a-z, the numbers 0-9, and underscores (_). That means there are 1,874,161 possible four-character username strings, so thousands of strings are eligible on each day. The most I ever saw was five, but that's probably because a lot of those are strings that no one uses for usernames. We're more likely to see reports from people with usernames starting with nigh- and star- than z1_k.

what we wondered

Some theories that we floated (though most of them were incorrect) included:
  • Many people have noticed that young or new accounts seem to be lucky with some of the random avatars, including this one. I personally suspect that some of this is confirmation bias (we're more likely to notice a young account getting the avatar because we're surprised, or a person with a young account is more likely to post on the board since they're excited about it) and some is actual coding (it makes sense that young accounts would experience a certain amount of luck to encourage them to keep playing).

  • Several people asked me, what happens for people whose usernames only have 1, 2, or 3 characters? We finally got some data on this. On June 7, 2020, pin got an eligible fortune, but someone else whose username begins with pink- did not. The next day, pin checked her fortune again, as did someone whose username begins with pino-, and they also got different fortunes. If you have a 1-, 2-, or 3-character username, I'd be very interested in seeing more information, especially if we can find other people with a longer usernames that start with the same character(s) who would also be willing to check. Usernames that are shorter than 4 characters are marked in the date page with an (s).

  • If you view the Island Mystic page when logged out, you can also get a fortune, which will be different from the one(s) you get when logged into your accounts. I'm assuming that this is a "generic" fortune, but I'd like to know if it's the same one that someone with a certain username gets every day.

what we ruled out

Here are some theories that have been suggested but disproven or discarded at this point:
  • Usernames are not eligible for the avatar on any kind of repeating, predictable schedule, such as every X days or on the same day every month. (See this page for the varying numbers of days between eligibilities.)

  • Usernames are not eligible on the same day each year. We successfully debunked this theory in 2019. astr- was eligible on October 17, 2017; on October 17, 2019, someone else created a new account that also started with astr- and did not get an eligible fortune or the avatar.

  • That said, for a while, I theorized that Neopets' weird counting system might be a factor there. Some of you have probably noticed that sometimes your account's age as displayed on the Neoboards differs from your account age as displayed on your userlookup. I believe this is because one counts the age in days and one counts the age in hours. However, I tested this in April 2020 with no luck. thep- was eligible on April 25, 2019 but not on any day in April 2020.

the actual fortunes

For the first two years I only tracked which username strings were eligible on a given day. Starting in December 2019 and inspired by 1337r3st0ck4, I began recording the actual fortunes that people got and submitted. I collected 17,982 between December 2019 and October 2022, and this information was used to update the Jellyneo page on the Island Mystic as well as figure out the algorithm.

It's important to note that there is a disproportionate number of eligible fortunes in this list, since people are more likely to check their fortune/post on the board if they don't have the avatar and are trying to get it.

Of the 17,982 fortunes, 12,408 were in English and 5,574 were in another language (mostly French and Dutch). 1,016 were eligible for the avatar. There were:

  • 0 instances of the word "Kyrii" in English
  • 1,002 instances of the word "Kyries" in English
  • 0 instances of the word "Kyri" in non-English
  • 13 instances of the word "Kyris" in non-English
  • 1 instance of the word "Kyriis" in non-English
Simple math suggests that that means there's a 5.66% chance of getting an eligible fortune. If we look at the English fortunes only, that bumps the chances up to 8.08%. An 8% chance would mean that you'd need to visit the Island Mystic for approximately 12.5 days to get the avatar (although the actual average is probably lower because this list includes more eligible fortunes than normal). But wait, there's more!

Fortunes are generated by stringing three or four pre-programmed phrases together. However, it's not completely random, because the fortunes generally make sense grammatically. In early 2022, Jellyneo took the English fortune data we collected and separated it into "pools," shown on their page here: http://www.jellyneo.net/index.php?go=islandmystic The phrase you pull from column 1 will determine the rest of your fortune. (Massive thanks to Dave and Quail for doing this for me so I didn't have to!) There are 30,914 total potential English fortunes, but only 1,227 of them contain the word "Kyries" and can award the avatar. Thus, simple math suggests that you would have a 3.97% chance of pulling an eligible fortune on a given day. But wait, there's more!

As mentioned above, we did not see 1,227 unique fortunes containing the word "Kyries." Of the 1,016 eligible fortunes, many were repeats or translations, so there really were only 659 unique fortunes. Of course, it's possible that we were just unlucky and that if we had collected data for longer we would have seen all 1,227, but given the number of repeats, it seems that you might need to get one of the 659 unique fortunes that we've seen people get already.
