Serene's NeoQuest Guide

Hello, my name is Serene. I have enjoyed playing the games NeoQuest and NeoQuest II ever since I first played them back in 2003/2004 respectively. Over time I have seen some good NQ resources come-and-go, and have been a part of a few really amazing NeoQuest groups. So I have decided to compile what I find to be very successful strategies for playing through NeoQuest. For the time being my focus is going to be on the first game, because for some reason, it seems to be less approachable to new players than its sequel.

What is NeoQuest?

NeoQuest is an open world RPG that utilizes traditional turn based combat. You will gain experience for defeating monsters, which you will use to level up. In the game you will guide a white Lupe through Ancient Neopia in order to try to quell the dangerous magics of the evil sorcerer Jahbal. Throughout the game you will battle countless foes, collect various items, and encounter several NPCs who will aid you on your quest. If you wish you can even converse with the many NPCs of the world to discover a fairly interesting story being told.

Difficulty Modes

Normal
  • This is the standard game.
  • There is only one boss in the final boss sequence: Jahbal.
  • A total of 30,650 NP can be earned from maxing out your level.
  • An additional 13,250 NP can be earned from beating all the bosses.
  • You will earn a Bronze Trophy upon defeating Jahbal.

Evil

  • Unlocked after beating Normal.
  • Monsters have 20% more HP (than Normal) and are stronger.
  • Item drops are more rare.
  • There are two bosses in the final boss sequence: Jahbal & Mastermind.
  • If you die, or flee, while fighting Mastermind, you are forced to start over.
  • A total of 30,650 NP can be earned from maxing out your level.
  • An additional 17,825 NP can be earned from beating all the bosses.
  • You will earn a Silver Trophy upon defeating Mastermind.

Insane

  • Unlocked after beating Evil.
  • Monsters have 20% more HP (than Normal) and are even stronger.
  • Item drops are even more rare.
  • You require twice as much experience to level up.
  • There are three bosses in the final boss sequence: Jahbal, Mastermind & Xantan Reborn.
  • If you die at any point during the game, you are forced to start over.
  • A total of 30,650 NP can be earned from maxing out your level.
  • An additional 25,325 NP can be earned from beating all the bosses.
  • You will earn a Gold Trophy upon defeating Xantan Reborn.

Tips & Tricks for Fast Play

Moving Fast
It is possible to click an arrows multiple times in a row. If you click the up arrow five times, your Hero will move up to 5 spaces. Switch into "Sneaking" mode and do this to cover a large amount of ground quickly.

Fast Battling
After selecting an action during a fight, you can press F5 to repeat that action. This can be done in short three-to-five move bursts. Or, if you feel you are far superior than your opponent you can press it five-to-ten (or more) times to quickly dipsatch them. Be careful not to press F5 after selecting "Do Nothing.

Experience Optimization
It is important to remember that while the experience gained from Monsters scales based off your level in relation to your opponents, Bosses give a fixed amount of experience. So it is best to only fight a boss once you are ready to leave an area. This is most important in the Mountain Fortress and Kal Panning. Doubly so for Kal Panning, because all the monsters will disappear after Faleinn is defeated.

Starting Out

When the game begins, feel free to get acquainted with the residents of Neopia Central. Once you are ready, start Hunting in the area immediately around the city to gain a few levels by fighting the Fire and Snow Imps. It shouldn't be long before you are Level 3. At this point walk a bit further away from the city so that you can fight Plains Aishas and Plains Lupes. If at any time you are low on health, switch to Sneak and head back to the city to receive healing from Boraxis. Your goals for now are to reach Level 6, find at least four glowing stones off of the Plains monsters, and receive the items for one of the following Upgrades from Morax Dorangis. Techo Caves (Armour Upgrade) - Especially Insane

Mr. Irgo, who is standing near the entrance to the first cave, is willing to make you new armour, if you bring him the right ingredients. Both armours are really good. I used to be a fan of the Energy Absorber, but after multiple playthroughs on the various difficulties - I tend to go for the Robe of Protection instead. It will negate enemy attacks about twice as often as the Energy Absorber will allow you to absorb enemy attacks - so mathematically they are at least very similar in terms of effectiveness. However, the ingredients for the robe are far easier to obtain. All of the items can even be found in the first Techo Cave from the various Drakonid opponents found there. To get the Energy Absorber you would need to venture into Techo Cave 2, and fight the stronger Serpentine Dervishes.

It is important to be careful inside Techo Caves. At this point you should be around Level 27 or 28, which is strong enough to battle the easier opponents in the first Cave, but not strong enough to fight the tougher opponents without exhausting your potion supply. Hunt near the north end of the first cave and gain experience from the monsters there. As you gain levels, you should feel safer to venture further south in the first Cave.

Once you have the ingredients for your armour upgrade, get your armour from Mr. Irgo. Continue to train in Techo Caves until at least Level 31. If you wish to overlevel, which I would recommend to Insane players, venture to the other Techo Caves for stronger opponents. Otherwise just stick with the first cave.

Temple of Roo

Once you are level 31, exit the Techo Caves back to the Desert of Roo, and head into the Temple. Congratulations! You are way overleveled for the enemies found near the entrance of the Temple. In fact you are overleveled for almost all of the enemies in the Temple. So you should sneak through the cavern portion and through the temple remains. Once your reach the tropical trees, switch back to Hunting and keep an eye out for the following item off of the Crystal Golems:
  • Piece of Living Crystal

While you can still find Crystal Golems on the second floor of the Temple, it is nice to not have to worry about finding this since you're going to be preoccupied searching for keys once you reach the second floor.

Here on the second floor we are going to search through various rooms to find a series of keys-in order to find the key to the Archmagus of Roo's room. Since you are still overleveled, just Sneak between each room, and hunt through the rooms to find the key. You can refer to my maps for the rooms, or follow the instructions here.

Key 1 - Go to the open room, which is conveniently just east of the entrance. Here you will need to find and defeat a Ghastly Initiate to receive a key to the next room.
Key 2 - Now head the northeast corner and enter the room there, to find a key off of a Ghastly Adept.
Key 3 - Head to the southernmost room now, and grab the key off of a Ghastly Priest.
Key 4 - Now head to the northwestern room to receive a key after slaying a Ghastly Master
Key 5 - Now head to the eastern room, and defeat a Ghastly Archon for a key to the next room.
Key 6 - The final key, the one needed to enter the Archmagus of Roo's room, can be found off of a Ghastly Templar in the central room.

Before fighting the Archmagus of Roo, make sure that you found the following item off of the Ghastly enemies on the second floor of the Temple:

  • Emerald

Once you have collected the final key, and your Emerald, it is time to enter the Archmagus's chambers. If you are not Level 33 yet, I would recommend training in this room until you reach that level. I would probably shoot for an even higher level if playing Insane. Archie has the possibility of being the hardest battle in the game, if you are not prepared.

Insane Players: Remember you can Sneak back to Techo Caves if you want to grind to higher levels, and not have to deal with the opponents in the Archmagus's chambers that have Drain Life.

BOSS: Archmagus of Roo
Recommended Level: 33 Normal - 200 HP Evil - 240 HP Insane - 240 HP
Archmagus of Roo is known for being one of the harder, and definitely one of the longer boss fights in the entire game. While he can be taken on as low as Level 31, I strongly suggest getting a couple extra levels so that you won't have to use quite as many potions. Good ole Archie here has a plethora of spells at his disposal. He will hit hard with fire and ice spells that hit for 50 damage. He can also stun you for three rounds using spells that also deal up to 40 damage. However, what makes the Archmagus of Roo a particularly noteworthy boss is that he can heal himself for 80 health or drain your life for 50, making effectively a 100 hp swing. He will often use these spells and in a couple of turns be back at full health. However, if you maintain your own health, you will eventually whittle him down.
1,200 Exp Normal - 1,200 NP Evil - 1,800 NP Insane - 2,400 NP

Mountain Fortress

The Mountain Fortress is a great place to train, with the added benefit that every regular opponent you face here has the opportunity to drop our final Armour upgrades. The Armour upgrade we are looking for is the Inferno Robe, which does 1 to 10 points of damage with each of your attacks. (While Evening Sun Energy Shield is still an upgrade, it randomly deals only 20 damage to your opponent, and only when they use a regular attack.) Which makes the damage from the Inferno Robe more consistent.

Train in the main hall here, until you are level 39. At which point you will want to head to the room where the Guardian of Life is located, the southeast room. This is probably the best room to train in because while the Life Elementals give good experience for the area, they won't hit incredibly hard if you are the right level. They do have the special ability to heal themselves, but that is okay. You will want to grind a couple levels fighting them.

It is important to note, that unlike the experience you gain from monsters (which decreases as you gain levels) the experience you gain from Bosses will always remain constant. So we are going to train here on the regular monsters until we are Level 42, preferably with 84,500 total XP (171,500 on Insane). Kal Panning & Two Rings Palace

Kal Panning

From the exit of Techo Caves, head to the eastern side of the continent by Sneaking. You will pass the Two Rings Mountain Range and the cave that allows you to enter the Two Rings. But you'll first need the key from Faleinn, who is in Kal Panning. Once on the eastern side of the continent, continue to Sneak heading north where you'll find Kal Panning, a large city surrounded by water. Kal Panning is a cursed city, Jahbal turned all of its inhabitants into undead monsters. Once inside switch to Hunting and make your way to the northeast side of the city to where Faleinn is. Before battling her, you'll want to train here for a bit because once she is defeated the curse on the city will be lifted and all of the monsters here will disappear. Continue to Hunt a couple spaces away from her until you are at least Level 46, just watch out for your opponents' ability to drain your health. The reason that training here is so important is because once you enter the Two Rings, you'll find significantly stronger monsters and it is easier to quickly gain levels here. And once you enter Two RIngs, you are unable to exit.

BOSS: Faleinn
Recommended Level: 46 Normal - 500 HP Evil - 600 HP Insane - 600 HP
Show Faleinn the Keladrian Medal and she will immediately be cured of the curse, and thus defeated.

If you choose to fight Faleinn, or if you forgot to get the Medal restored, know that she can deal up to 70 HP with her Fire & Ice spells. She can also stun you for 3 turns while damaging you for 70 HP. Her final special move is draining 90 HP from you.
1,500 Exp Normal - 1,500 NP Evil - 1,500 NP Insane - 1,500 NP

Two Rings & Two Rings Palace

Once Faleinn is defeated you will have the key to the Two Rings Mountain Range. Switch to Sneaking and make your way out of Kal Panning and head to the cave entrance you passed before. Now you'll be able to pass through the door no problem. Just remember you won't be able to leave after heading through the door. Once inside Two Rings continue north until you reach Two Rings Palace. Now switch back to Hunting. Inside the palace there are two categories of monsters here, Chia Soldiers and Chia Servants. (It is easy to distinguish the difference between these two.) The servants are actually more troublesome because they can drain 100 HP from you. So keep an eye out while fighting them. You will want to reach Level 48 on the first floor of the palace as you make your way to the stairs in the northeast. On the second floor you will continue to train to Level 50 as you make your way to the stairs in the west. Once you are level 50 and have a decent cache of potions, head up to the third floor for the final showdown(s).

BOSS: Jahbal
Recommended Level: 50 Normal - 700 HP Evil - 840 HP Insane - 840 HP
Jahbal is an incredibly tough opponent, with a vast spell list that he can use on you. He can deal up to 90 damage with fire or ice spells, 70 damage while stunning for 3 rounds, do 50 poison damage for 5 rounds, Disable you, heal 150 HP, or drain you of 100 HP.

Really keep an eye on your health, as he can combo many of these powerful spells together to defeat you. If you are ever poisoned, make sure you heal to full as quickly as possible. Over time you will find yourself victorious.

Do not waste a turn trying to cast Absorption. Jahbal will almost certainly resist it and you will have no chance to stun him. It is better to Attack him normally, and just make sure you stay healthy.

5,000 Exp Normal - 5,000 NP Evil - 5,000 NP Insane - 5,000 NP

If you are playing on Normal mode, congratulations on beating the game. If you are playing Evil or Insane, you will immediately head into the next battle. Even in Evil difficulty, a death in the next battle will end the game.

BOSS: Mastermind
Recommended Level: 50 Normal - N/A Evil - 1,000 HP Insane - 1,000 HP
Mastermind has more health than Jahbal, but he isn't nearly as troublesome. He does however have powerful fire and ice spells that can hit for up to 120 each. He also has a spell that will drain you for 50 HP for up to 3 rounds, but it has a tendency to fail. So keep your health bar above 170 HP and you should be able to outlast him.
7,500 Exp Normal - N/A Evil - 7,500 NP Insane - 7,500 NP

BOSS: Xantan the Reborn
Recommended Level: 50 Normal - N/A Evil - N/A Insane - 1,000 HP
Despite being the final boss of the hardest difficulty, Xantan Reborn likely won't be any issue at all. Like Mastermind, he is certainly significantly easier than Jahbal. However he doesn't have the powerful spells that Mastermind has. He will pretty much just hit you for 50 HP each turn, and that is it. As long as you have the appropriate build, you'll make it through the other end of this battle in no time!
10,000 Exp Normal - N/A Evil - N/A Insane - 10,000 NP

Once Xantan the Reborn is defeated, the long grind to your gold trophy is over. Congratulations!
