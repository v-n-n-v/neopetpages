Interested in gourmet hunting? If yes, then this ultimate Gourmet Club guide is for you! Qualifying gourmet foods are edible items with a rarities between r90-r100, so anything above or below those indices do not count as gourmet. These foods are sold all over Neopia and are some of rarest items on the whole site. If your pet eats enough of these foods, they'll make it into the Gourmet Club Hall of Fame!

Things to know before you start gourmet hunting:

  1. This is an extremely expensive hobby.

  2. Trophies are awarded to your pet and thus are found on their lookup, not your UL.

  3. Once achieved, gourmet status remains even if the pet switches owners or is sent to the pound.

  4. Retired gourmet foods are given new rarities (r180) and will not award gourmet points when eaten.

  5. Species with allergies are not recommended gourmet pets because of dietary barriers, but if a sick pet doesn't bother you, proceed.

  6. The "Dying" hunger status is the recommended level to keep a gourmet pet because of how quickly the status level drops between "Starving" and "Dying" (~80 mins.).

  7. Grarrls and Skeiths all-consuming behavior does not serve as an unfair advantage over other species, as only food items count towards the club.

  8. Highly recommend using this guide in tandem with a wishlist or checklist or spreadsheet or whatever else you may prefer to track your gourmet hunting with!

Feeding Fun Facts

  • Feeding a Kau a food with "milk" in the name will make them "Very Bloated".

  • Feeding a Pteri a food with "worm" in the name will make them "Very Bloated". "Worm" food is also cureall for them.

  • Kikos & JubJubs can transform into the Garlic colour by eating lots of "garlic" foods. (NT Editorial Q13)

  • Chias have a small chance of transforming into the Chocolate colour by eating lots of "chocolate" foods. (NT Editorial Q4)

  • Feeding Half-eaten Glowing Jelly to a pet has a small chance of transforming them into the Glowing colour.

  • Feeding a pet will increase HP if it has fallen below max.


  • Feeding Magic Ghost Marshmallows to a Ghost-colored pet will completely restore their HP.

  • Feeding a pet can increase their happiness.

  • Feeding a pet Asparagus or Organic Broccoli may give them a height boost.


  • Feeding a pet a Pumpkin Negg will increase their weight and make them shorter. (NT Editorial Q10)

  • Chias can be a fun choice in gourmet pets because they'll transform into different colours when they're fed Magical Chia Pops.

  • Your pet will reject any food with a name that has the same number of letters as it has in its name. (Changing the language lets them change their mind about foods released before 2015.)


  • Bloat B Gone changes hunger status from "Bloated" to "Not Hungry", can be used to hasten the starving process in order to feed your gourmet pet more food during sprees.


  • Rainbow Melt Pizza changes hunger status to "Very Full"; it can be used to hasten the starving process by downgrading hunger from "Bloated" or help feed a pet one more food during sprees.

  • Some species have allergies to certain foods. Kyrii - "apple"; Quiggle - "cream"; Skeith - "cheese"; Tonu - "negg".

  • Jetsams can be fed aquatic petpets.

  • Grarrls and Skeiths can eat random things out of your inventory without warning if their hunger status is too low.

FAQs

How many of my pets can be in the club?
All of them!

Can my side account pets be in the club?
Yep, just make sure all the foods are found/bought on your main account.

Is this the food club?
NO! THIS IS PATRICK! *I am not a crusty crab*

If my Grarrl or Skeith eat the gourmet food before I give it to them, do I still get a point in GC?
No, gourmet foods must be fed manually to count towards GC. (NT Editorial Q8)

Is account age important to GC?
Yes and no. No because an hours-old pet can be fed gourmet foods just like any other pet. Yes because only accounts 3mos+ can see all the rarities restock in the NPC shops and 36mos+ can shop at the AAA. And then there's the issue of having the NP to participate...

When are trophies awarded?
Daily at random, unannounced times.

Is one species a better gourmet pet than the rest?
No, there are no species with unfair advantages, like bottomless stomachs or something like that. (NT Editorial Q2)

Where are the names of the foods my gourmet pet has eaten?
They don't appear and likely never will. This is not an intentionally malicious attempt by TNT to stamp out happy gourmet hunting, its actually related to database constraints.

Do potions count as gourmet foods?
No, your pet may drink them but they are considered magical items, not food and do not affect hunger status.

Do cures count as gourmet foods?
No, while your pet may consume them, they are considered medicinal items, not food and do not affect hunger status.

What happens if a gourmet food is changed and is no longer gourmet?
If the food is consumed during the time it was gourmet, you will keep your point and the food in your log. If the item is consumed after it is changed, you're out of luck and no point is granted.

Will my pet lose their trophy if we drop positions?
Nope, once a trophy is earned, its theirs.

Will a gourmet pet be more inclined to choose gourmet food for their monthly freebie?
No, this process is completely random. (NT Editorial Q7)

Does a higher rarity mean more gourmet points?
No, it's 1:1 for every gourmet food.

Can you feed the same gourmet food for more points?
No, each unique food only counts once.
