Proem Welcome, visitor! You clearly came here searching for untaken names, right? Well, I sure hope so, because you won't find any shrines dedicated to Dr. Sloth here, nope.

The Forgotten was first established April, 2013. For quite awhile I hoarded lists of great untaken names I found, and I knew one day I was destined to share them with the Neopian public. Once I got into college I finally understood my talent for finding untaken names; my appreciation and base knowledge of linguistics clearly led me to create, and continue, this page. In naming it The Forgotten, I thought about how many of the words and names I was listing had, in some way, been forgotten/discarded/disregarded, and so I decided to name the site "The Forgotten." I'm lucky the title came to me so quickly, because usually I'm awful with titles.

So, with story time over, I hope you enjoy all the names I have to offer, and I hope you find some lovely new names for your pets!

P.S.A.: PLEASE MAIL ME YOUR TAKEN NAME(S) HERE!
Inquiry Allow me to be your apocrisiary! Here, you can request certain kinds of names or words, usually best based on themes you suggest to me, and I'll try my best to find the perfect fit for you. Just fill out the form that you will find below (not immediately next but below these helpful tips) and neomail it to me.

For a bit of an explanation beforehand:
  • Origin refers to the language the word or name originates from. For example: if you like French words, you would request a "French" origin, or if you like English or Spanish names, you would ask for "English" (you can specify British or American English if applicable) or "Spanish".
  • Max amount of letters asks for how many letters the word or name may contain.
  • Gender typically means whether or not you want this pet name to be masculine or feminine – usually this is based on the gender of the pet you plan to create, but of course I'm equally as fine with searching for ambiguous/ungendered names, as well.
  • Un/Common suggests whether or not you want a common or uncommon name/word.
  • Theme asks if you know the theme of the pet/character you're developing. I often search for names through meaning, so theme would give me a keyword or two (or more, which would be even more helpful) that would make my search easier. But, of course, if you don't have an idea plotted out for this pet, 'theme' is simply optional.
