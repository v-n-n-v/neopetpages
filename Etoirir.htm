Getting Started

This is a nifty guide I wish I had when I first came to Neopets years ago! So I made it for others that are stuck trying to find things to do in Neopia! Let's navigate this great big bowl of spaghetti code together, shall we?

From the moment you create an account on Neopets, it begins to record your account's age. Your account must be 24 hours old to do most things, like post on the boards and collect from dailies. You should keep track of your account's age as there are many perks to having an older account.

Neopets rewards its loyal members in several ways:

  • Shields If you navigate to your User Lookup page (click on your username!), you'll notice a shield with an image on it. This image and shield change as your account ages. The oldest accounts have the most intricate shields.
  • Cast Votes Players 1wk+ can cast votes for Neopian contests.
  • Freebies Players 1mo+ can collect on all daily activities (aka dailies, we will get to this!) and monthly freebies.
  • Neopian Stores Players 3mos+ can see all items (r1-r100) restock in the shops. Note: r100 items are glitched due to an incorrect number in the coding and because of this, they are not visible to any players, even if one has restocked in a shop.
  • Pet Adoption Players 4mos+ can adopt all levels, colors, and species (except Ice, Magma, and Lutaris - no one can abandon or adopt these pets) from the Pound. Note: This perk has since been granted to all players as of Y23!
  • Hidden Tower Players 4mos+ can shop and 60mos+ can shop with a 3% discount on the 3rd Wednesday of every month (yes, I know... very specific - mark your calendars!)
  • Quirks Players 24mos+ may express themselves with additional NeoTitles and interests, many of them are goofy and fun! Players 120mos+ have all available dropdown options.
  • Bookworm Players 24mos+ have a very small chance of a book not disappearing after it is read to a pet. Note: Only my well read pets (200+ books) have ever left their copy of a book behind, while I cannot confirm this part of the mechanic, I believe intell also plays a role.
  • Bonus Trades Players 36mos+ get 15 trade lots at the trading post instead of the normal 10.
  • Almost Abandoned Attic Players 36mos+ can shop - This Neovian shop sells items (r80-r99) at their "Estimated Value", this can mean amazing deals or grossly overpriced freebies!
  • Elite Boutique Players 48mos+ gain access to this special category at the NC Mall. 1337!!
  • Neomail Players 63mos+ can have up to 200 saved messages. The maximum bobbles below 200 for those less than 63mos.
  • Favorite Games Players 265mos+ can have 46 games in their favorites list. Tip: Accounts younger than 3 days do not qualify for high scores. (yes, even if you get one, sorry!)
  • NeoFriends Older accounts can see 7 friends online while younger accounts can see 5 at a time.
  • Quest Log - Completing 5 quests per day can earn you thousands of neopoints or random prizes or both; completing all quests for 7 days will earn you a grand prize. [DAILY]
  • Trudy's Surprise Logging in every day for 25 days in a row can earn you around 500,000NP. Remember to spin the machine every day, if you miss a day, the counter will reset. [DAILY]
  • Birthdays Your pet's birthday is the day they were created. You can locate the date on the pet's lookup page. When their birthday comes around each year, your pet will get the choice between 3 stat increasing cupcakes!

    +5 Strength
    +5 Defense
    +5 HP(base)
    Each of your pets may consume only one of each cupcake per calendar year.
    On your IRL birthday, you will receive the birthday theme and increased random event odds!
    Neopets birthday (Nov. 15) is a super special day, it's practically a holiday 'round these parts! Please check out the Annual Events section of this guide to find out more.
  • Betting Players' maximum bets are based on an equation using the number of days their account has existed. In other words, the older the account, the higher the bets!

If you need it, there's a search bar you can use to find other players, games, articles, and items (not places, that's why we're here!). Keep in mind that it cannot find super rare items (r99+). Try using the Shop Wizard (SW) to find these items instead. These are other players shops and their prices are fixed, the player does not haggle (usually ;)) so try looking for the lowest price; this may take a few searches but it can save you thousands. If the item cannot be found on the SW try the Trading Post (TP) or Auction House (AH).(Tip: get to know the freebie items before you start shopping!)

5. Lost Desert

The Lost Desert is split in two by the river that runs through it. On the left is the city of Qasala and on the right is Sakhmet. Each city sells their own food and books (sometimes in the form of scrolls), so be sure to stop in and pick up some desert-themed goodies.
Neopedia: Lost Desert, Sakhmet, Qasala
Population: 340,000 (27% Elephante; 22% Nimmo; 20% Lupe; 11% Aisha; 9% Kacheek; 7% Peophin; 4% Other)

While you're here, the things to do are:

  • Coltzan's Shrine The ghost of King Coltzan does not reveal himself to a player until their account is at least 48 hours old. From then on you can visit him once every 13 hours. He can increase stats, give out an item, or make you richer. Make sure you increase your pet's intelligence before seeing him as his rewards are greater for smarter pets. (Tip: There is a rumor that if you visit him in critical health (less than 10HP) on the 15th of the month at midnight (NST) he will give you a million NP!) [Odds: ~6,000,000:1 NT: Issue #8 Q3] [DAILY]
  • Pyramids A tricky card game that cannot always be solved. There's a 5,000NP daily winnings limit.
  • Temple of 1,000 Tombs Enter these tombs at your own risk.
  • Wheel of Extravagance Hidden away in the city of Qasala is a wheel that costs 100,000NP per spin. Its prizes are the best of all the Neopian Wheels, but they come at a cost, that's for sure! You can do this wheel daily but I don't recommend it for everyone, so I'm not going to mark it as a daily...
  • Qasalan Expellibox Drop a scarab down the peg board every 7 hours for a chance win some NC! Flash free [DAILY]
  • Coloring Pages Download and/or print these pages at your leisure.

  • Wadjets and Ladders Beat your opponent to the treasure to win. This game is just for funsies and does not award NP.

6. Faerieland

Faerieland was not always on the ground; it used to be floating on cloud islands in the sky. We won't talk too much about what happened because the faeries get upset. It's on the ground now and they're rebuilding, just show them your support, okay? It seems even more fruitful today than it ever did somehow... Check out Faerie City to find more magical places.
Neopedia: Faerieland
Population: 1.62 million (80% Faeries; 15% Faerie-painted Neopets; 5% Other)

While you're here the things to do are:

  • Rainbow Fountain This waterfall is different from the Rainbow Pool found in Neopia Central. Naia the Fountain Faerie is able to paint the pet of your choosing when you complete a quest of hers, including exclusive colors, like Alien Aisha. Her quests are extremely rare, so don't feel discouraged if she hasn't asked for your help yet. (Tip: Try to always complete the Grey Faerie's quests as she may call on Naia to reward you!)
  • Healing Springs Another water faerie you'll get to know well. She can heal your pets every 30 minutes. Her spells are different each time. She can also give you a healing item from her shop for free, so be sure to stop by every once in a while. Your odds for better magic blessings increase based on how often you visit her throughout the day. (Trick: If your pet comes down with an expensive illness, take them here. This is free and she will eventually heal them.) [DAILY]
  • Poogle Races Bet on a Poogle to win the race every 15 minutes. Feel free to feed them a food item before the race begins.
  • Wheel of Excitement Spin the wheel every 2 hours and win up to 20,000 NP! [DAILY]
  • The Discarded Magical Blue Grundo Plushie of Prosperity Hanging on a tree is the Magical Blue Grundo Plushie, you can try to talk to him. He may reward you with toys, fill your pets bellies, or sprinkle some NP on the ground for you to find. [DAILY]
  • Jhudora's Bluff Located away from all other faeries is the dark faerie Jhudora. You may complete a quest of hers every 12 hours... unless you've completed one of Illusen's (See Meridell) quests, which in that case she will ask you to leave and come back another day. (They don't get along!) [DAILY]
  • Faerie Caverns Take a trip through the caverns once a day in the hopes of finding some treasure. There are some very valuable prizes to find at this daily. [DAILY]
  • Faerie Personality Quiz Take this quiz to find out which type of faerie you are most like.
  • Hidden Tower Queen Fyora sells some of the more treasured items in Neopia in the hopes of raising funds for Faerieland. Fyora takes stock 3 times a day, and during those times, you will be unable to make a purchase. The HT will be closed during these 3 times: 1-2AM NST, 7-8AM NST, 10-11PM NST. You may make one purchase a day.
  • Faerie Employment Agency Bring job coupons here to preform a job - collecting items in exchange for NP. They offer some other jobs that don't require a coupon so check those out too (5 jobs per day).
  • Faerie Crossword Visit the Library Faerie to complete the daily crossword puzzle. Your pet may also gain intelligence points from completing the puzzle![DAILY]
  • Coloring Pages Download and/or print these pages at your leisure.
  • Faerie Quests If a Faerie has asked for your help finding an item, take the item here to be rewarded! Most rewards will be stat increases, save for the Fountain Faerie and faeries from the NC mall.

9. Terror Mountain

At the northern most region of the map is Terror Mountain. A snow covered happy-go-lucky part of Neopia home to the winter-loving residents of Happy Valley. It's also the hoppin' place to be during the Month of Giving (December). Inside the mountain is a series of Ice Caves for you to explore!

Population: 420,000

While you're here, the things to do are:

ECON:101

The Neopets economy was intentionally designed to have low and high points just like any other economy. This essentially means that price flux is almost guaranteed to occur on any item. It works on a supply & demand basis, for example, if supply is very high and demand is very low for an item, prices may hit 1NP in the item's lifetime.

  1. Things that may positively influence value:
  • Rarity - Items with high rarities tend to retain more value.
  • Exclusivity - Cannot be obtained from the NPC shops.
  • Collectability - Items gain value in collections (i.e. stamps)
  • Questing - Items frequently requested have increased value.
  • Utility - The more useful an item is the more value it holds.
  • Retirement - Items that stop restocking in the NPC shops can gain value.
  • Disappearing Acts - Single-use items keep supply low, which can increase value.
  • Inflation - High demand & low supply (even if artificial) can inflate prices above "average" value.
  1. Things that may negatively influence value:
  • Gratis - Any item received for free will lose some value.
  • Abundance - High supplies lead to price drops.
  • Uselessness - An item that serves no purpose (i.e. Rotting Driftwood) other than sheer existence, rapidly loses value.
  • Appearance - Unappealing items generally lose interest and lower overall demand.
  • Competition - Price drops may occur so a sale can be made; say you have 2 UB sellers, the one with the lower price will be more likely to score a sale.
  • Redundancy - Items that can achieve the same effect (i.e. cures), and for less cost, may cause other items' prices to drop.

Price Limits
Player Shops: 1-999,999NP
Trading Post: 0-2,000,000NP + 1-10 items
Auction House: 1-100,000,000NP


Easy to sell (ETS) items are those that can be sold the same day that they're put up for sale. (They are only ETS if they're priced reasonably!)
Hard to sell (HTS) items tend to be more niche and/or less desirable to the masses. They may take days, weeks, or even months to sell, which may lead to price drops for liquidation purposes by the seller.

An item is considered unbuyable (UB) when it cannot be found on the shop wizard. Basically, any item worth more than 1,000,000NP. These are often highly sought after because of their high market value, and some Neopians have decided to strictly deal in high end items such as these in their restocking endeavors.

Baby Paint Brushes are sometimes used as trade "currency" at the Trading Post. This is because they are sold at a stabilized price (600K) at the Hidden Tower and are relatively ETS on the market because let's face it baby Neopets are just the cutest! This means that they represent 600K NP within a transaction. For example, a seller may ask for "1.8M + 2 Baby PBs" totaling 3M in all. This method helps sellers bypass the 2M NP sale limit.

Using the Shop Wizard is the most accurate way to price your items that you suspect are worth less than 1 million NP. Any unbuyables (UBs) that you want to sell will take more effort. The fansite: JellyNeo has a great item database where you can view past sale records of the UB you'd like to sell. And while prices may have fluctuated since the last sale, if you have the only one on the market, you have free reign on the price.

When viewing an item's ID card (only possible in player inventory), you'll notice an "Est. Value" detail. This value is used by the site to generate sale prices in NPC shops. And as mentioned earlier in this guide, the Almost Abandoned Attic sells items at this exact price. However, this value is not accurate to its actual market value. For instance, an UB may be bought for 10,000NP at an NPC shop and sold for 10 million on the market. These are huge profit margins, and now it may be becoming obvious why so many players rely on restocking as a way to make NP on the site.

You may run into items being referred to as junk. There's a wide range of items that fall under this title, but essentially, junk is any item worth less than 1,000 NP on the player market. These items are mostly prizes from dailies or have low rarities and tend to decrease in value rapidly because supplies are very high, not necessarily that these items are truly worthless (some are though, I'm looking at you, Rotting Driftwood...).

Note: Our current economy is suffering from false supply shortages. What does that mean? Unfortunately, some of the items that should be entering the economy through normal means are not. This has caused drastic price influxes (and a very upset player base) to occur.
