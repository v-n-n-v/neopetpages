About Lennies

Lenny (LEN-ee) are one of the original Neopets species, having been released sometime prior to January 2000 (Neodex, 2021). They are most comparable to Earth flamingos, ostriches, storks, or cranes -- all known for being birds with large beaks and long legs. On-site, their closest relatives are likely Pteri or Vandagyre, which are shorter species of avian.

Lennies are the 42nd most popular Neopet, with approximately 1.5 million Lennies created (or about 0.56% of all Neopet species). They are one of the least popular Neopet species, and are the second most unpopular non-Limited Edition species (Neodex, 2021). They also average at around 60 cm tall, making them a medium-sized pet (Neocolours, 2021).

While not being popular, Lennies are one of the most intelligent and magically-gifted pets on the site. In fact, according to the official definition,

The Lenny is a highly intelligent and curious bird. In time they to master many forms and use their cunning to outwit any opponents.

Lennies are capable of learning powerful offensive AND defensive magic, allowing themselves to properly protect themselves in duels against monsters and other Neopets. In fact, it's possible that the only pet species more intelligent and magically-gifted is the Aisha -- and outside of Neopets, the Faeries.

The Lenny has undergone three redraws - the first was on January 30, 2001, and the second was on August 31, 2003. The third redraw, in which Lennies were able to be customized to wear clothes, took place on April 19, 2007, and affected all existing Neopet species.

The first version of the Lenny features the feathers as looking more like a jester's cap, extremely raggedy feathers, lanky legs, and long feet. The head and beak were proportionately larger compared to the body!

The second version looked much the same as the first, though now the feet were not as large, the pose was changed to a more happy-go-lucky "waving" look, and the feathers were well-preened.

The final pre-conversion Lenny tucked its wings back like the original artwork, but sports a huge grin. The head feathers are no longer drawn to look like a cap, and the beak and face are more proportional to the rest of the body. In addition, the feet only have two forward-pointing toes now!

Customization offered the final (and current!) look for the Lenny. It features a pose facing towards the right, with the wings outstretched to allow them to hold objects. The knees are more noticeably knobby, but apart from the pose not much has changed.

Lenny Day is celebrated every year on February 12th. On this day, all things Lenny are brought to light! This often includes the release of new items, new outfits, and even a new paint brush or lab-only color.

Famous Lennies

Throughout the land of Neopia, many famous Lennies (referred to affectionately as "NPCs") exist, whether as humble shopkeepers to heroic adventurers! The Lenny Lounge has compiled a list of these fantastic fowl to expand your appreciation of these funky birds.
Thanks to Jellyneo for image sources and extra information!


Antiquity-Fixity Shopkeeper
This eccentric Lenny used to run a shop dedicated to creating decorations to spruce up the island of Dacardia. He was eventually replaced by a Crabby Bot during the game's beta, but not before touching the hearts of all that knew him!


Dayla
While no longer a member of the team, Dayla served as a forward during Roo Island's 2006 foray into the Altador Cup. She was noted as being one of the fastest sprinters on any team, and was even nominated for Rookie of the Year award! However, she was not signed back onto Roo Island the following year, and hasn't shown up on any other team since then.


Dr. Ballard
This kindhearted professor was a part of the cancelled Neoschools project. He had his education in teaching basic mathematics, and would have started Neopets out with simple math concepts before moving onto more complex problems.


Finneus
Finneus is the wise and faithful (and forgetful) book-keeper to King Altador. During the Altadorian mini-plot, Finneus plays a vital role by helping the player solve clues, and giving advice on what to do next. He collects Meepit plushies in addition to keeping his records, and happily serves the land of Altador in the archives.


Haunted Butler
This spooky Lenny appears as an enemy in Neoquest II. They can be found in the Happy Fun Non-Haunted House in the Haunted Woods land, serving an unknown master. It is not known if they exist in "true" Neopia, or just within the game world of NQII.


Inah
This happy-go-lucky Lenny owns and runs the Super Happy Icy Fun Snow Shop on Terror Mountain. Her frozen treats are sure to keep you chilly in the blustering winds and sparkling snow! She is also the aunt of Trudy, an Ixi that runs a daily slot machine.

Katherine Reinard
Katherine was a mentioned character in the Tale of Woe plot. Her grave was located in the Haunted Woods -- and since it was disturbed during a section of the plot, her spirit may now be haunting the forest.


King Kawillawoa
Featured in The Adventures of Jake, this jolly Lenny had land named after him and was known for being a just and fair ruler. His people were protected by the Mystical Tiki Totem; when this relic was stolen by a neighboring clan, the adventurer Jake recovered it for him.


Lenny Conundrum Wizard
This enigmatic bird is the author of all the Lenny Conundrum clues. He is also the nephew of Finneus!


Lenny Curator
Ever wanted to visit one of Tyrannia's eminent prehistoric museums? This Lenny owns the largest collection of artifacts in the whole land, and is eager to share their knowledge with those willing to brave the heat.


Lenny Librarian
Stop by the Lenny Library in Neopia Central to meet this hassled chap! He deals with all sorts of books, including those charmed with magical abilities!


Lenny Smuggler
This Lenny is the partner of Goldhook the Bruce; both of these pirates smuggle goods to sell on Krawk Island.


Lenny Sorcerer
This neutrally-aligned Lenny seeks to find magical popularity, and isn't above trapping other Neopets in his magical cloak to obtain it.


Lightning Lenny
Speed is his middle name! Lightning Lenny first appeared in the second series of the Defenders of Neopia series, battling the evil mummy Eyrieki and saving Sakhmet. During calmer times, LL mans the computer system at headquarters, watching out for any new crimes.


Margoreth
This disgruntled history teacher featured in the orignal Neoquest campaign. She can be found in the Jungle Ruins, having been convinced to explore the old texts and tomes by the wizard Denethrir.


Master Brain
The second ever Better Than You Challenger, this Lenny claims to be the smartest being in Neopia. With an IQ of over 3600 he might just be right!


Moss Tullerby
The only known member of the Neopian Conservation Society, this Lenny made it his goal to restore all the Wheels in Neopia after noticing they had fallen into disrepair.


NC Mall Specialty Shopkeeper
A researcher and traveler, this Lenny has collected countless priceless and unique artifacts to sell in his store. While originally a suspect in the abduction of the Scorchio Alchemist, he was later ruled out during the investigation process.


Panari
A resident of Happy Valley in Neoquest II. She lives with her daughter, Koli.


Prigpants
Part of a pair of two of the finest tailors in all of Neovia. His wares can be purchased at Prigpants & Swolthy, Tailors.


Professor Cecil Rigby
An expert on petpets, and a past professor at the University of Petpet Park. He now serves as the university's dean.


Sakra
Sakra was a young bully. During her final Halloween, she and her two cohorts Trent and Mirk were taught a last lesson by the old Krawk Lady Doppel.


Sand Walker
Another enemy found in Neoquest II. These thieves prey on weary travelers in the southern region of the Lost Desert.


Tebalo
A Neoquest II NPC that lives in the village of Waset with his wife Paide. He doesn't care too much for the zombies lurking about...


The Duchess
The mysterious leader of the hidden organization known as the Sway. While not one to sugarcoat words, she often prefers to send messages through one of her pet Crokabeks instead. She was one of the leaders in the featured factions during the War for the Obelisk.


Undead Guard Captain
Once members of the guard of Pal Kanning, these Lenny instead rose as members of the undead when Old Neopia was cursed. They appear in the original Neoquest.


Vela Binal
A defender for team Mystery Island in the Altador Cup. He replaced an injured Maital Koric in 2007, and has been a constant player since.


Video Shopkeeper
The shopkeeper for the now-defunct Video Store. Unfortunately, his store went out of business and had to close.

He is (unofficially) recollecting his old stock and displaying it for all to see! Check out the gallery here!


Wheeler
A young Lenny employed by the Underwater Chef. He is tasked with finding cocounts across Mystery Island to complete recipes, and rides across dangerous terrain on his trusty bike.


Zika
While her old hobby was camouflaging in bushes to scare unsuspecting passerby, this young bird later found a new passion in golf. She was a featured challenger in Better Than You.

Some minor Lenny characters (Gerald, Jodri, ) appeared in Lenny Conundrum, discontinued collector's cards (Fire Elemental Lenny, Princess Pahara, Stubby Joe, The Great Suplendo, and Water Elemental Lenny), while others (such as Meredith P) were listed as Lennies in an earlier appearance on the site.
