What is neocash?

Neocash is the virtual currency proprietary to Neopets. Though it is purchased with offline currency at the value of 100 NC per $1 USD, it is nonrefundable and nonexchangable for any other currency including Neopoints. Neocash may only be used to purchase onsite items from the Neocash mall.

What are Neocash Cards?

Neocash Cards were officially released through a nickelodeon partnership when neopets was owned by viacom. Two varieties were produced in multiple denominations of each variety. The Nick Game Cards advertised neopets as well as several other now defunct games, but there were also neocash specific cards with recognizable neopets illustrations. Both versions of the cards are still redeemable for now.

Where do I find Neocash Cards?

Although once physically sold in gas stations and department stores, you are now only likely to find Neocash Cards and Nick Game Cards either on the website of a certain soulless worldwide chain, or through a third party dealer. Frequent oǹline sales of neocash cards by third parties may be found via poogle. In both cases, upon purchase, you'll receive a digital code emailed to you to enter on neopets.

What are the costs and values?

Neocash Cards are now only sold oǹline via retail in denominations of 10 or 15 USD, but you may be able to find other denominations through third parties, often with appreciable sales such as 25% off. There is a small risk that the code you purchase may have already been illegally redeemed, but the chance of that happening is so rare and stores or dealers may address the issue in customer service.

How do I redeem the Neocash Card code?

Once you have your code you'll use the onsite navigation bar to select NC Mall, and then in the dropdown menu click Redeem Neocash Cards link. Or, you know, just open this link in a new tab.


If you're on mobile or otherwise using the new layout, it's the same premise, but the navigation begins by clicking the Shop cart icon, and then tabbing over to NC tab at the top, before going back down to select Redeem NC Cards. The redemption page is the same for both classic and the more mobile friendly layout.

When you're redirected the URL of neopets.com will be updated to the URL secure.nc.neopets.com. Although it doesn't have any neopets navigation, don't be alarmed. It looks different but it's still on the official neopets site. You'll need to relog with your username and pass.

There you'll be prompted to enter the PIN code. This'll be a 10 digit series in the format XXX XXX XXXX. The code you purchased should have been sent to your email. If you had the rare luck to find a physical card, then you'll need to scratch the silver strip.


Upon successful redemption you'll be redirected to a revelation of which of the six available card exclusive NC items you're awarded, as well as an update to your NC amount and the number of gift boxes granted. Not shown in this screenshot for security reasons will be your reference number for the transaction. It's advised you record this and any other NC history for account recovery data you can use to prove account ownership to TNT if something ever happens to your account.


METHOD 2: BUYING DIRECTLY FROM NEOPETS

THIS METHOD HAS HISTORY OF ISSUES AND MAJOR DELAYS

  • If you need your neocash quickly for an event, it is NOT recommended to use this method as you may encounter delays. There's enough issues that we have NC Redemption Help Guide.
  • YOU WILL NOT RECEIVE ANY GIFT BOXES WITH THIS METHOD. Use cards if you need boxes.
  • It is against the official neopets rules to purchase directly onsite if you're under 18 years old.

What do I do if my NC doesn't show up?

If you bought directly from neopets.com and it's been a worrisomely long time to show, here are some ways to address the issues if you don't want to wait it out:

  • Check the Neocash Transactions Log, found in the classic layout dropdown menu, for any updated information on your transaction. Once new NC is marked as awarded it will be listed on this page, so you can confirm its data here. Refresh the main site once it's listed and your NC should show up.
  • If there's no update to the Neocash Transactions Log, then submit a ticket by emailing support@neopets.com. Retrieve your ticket number from the autoresponse email, navigate to the neoboards, enter the Help Chat subforum, and find the "Highway to Help" thread. Post your ticket number there and mention you're experiencing a delay for onsite NC purchase.
  • Check out the user created NC Redemption Help Guide for details on what to include in your ticket, and various other methods to try, including logging out and in, making small purchases, activating an Upcycle Fortune Cookie NC award, or purchasing an item from the neocash mall.

I rewrote this beginner guide to neocash to help others obtain the perfect items to customize their beloved pets with their own creative expression of fashion without spiraling into depression from the difficulty of learning and navigating neocash trades.
It's confusing to get into neocash trading, and obnoxious to participate in the drudgery of finding a trade. To be clear, thanks, I hate it. So I hope the information here clears the mysteries and gives a feeling of confidence and ease for those new or diligently working to bring their customization concepts to fruition. With many of my pets the themes I use in their stories aren't always apparent with first glance at my pet lookup designs. It's usually my customizations that give a big wow first impression and help others gain an understanding of the character and backstory. A lot can be done to depict a mood or evoke a plot by setting the scene with some accessories and a background. Aesthetic alters opinion and interpretation! I want all of my beloved pets to have customs that perfectly describe who they are and how dearly I adore them. For some neopet customization is an elaborate dress up game, and that's cool, too, but neopet characterization is my main interest oǹsite! When the 2007 Neopocalypse conversion hit, I was utterly devastated by the destruction of our beloved pixels. Most of my friends quit. A lot of tragic things happened. But there was this curious new feature where you could put clothes on these new, garish, cartoony versions of our virtual monsters. I wasn't immediately sold. For years I only used accessories and handheld items because the idea of backgrounds on a neopet threw me off. Now it's so accepted that not having a background feels wrong. It's developed into a feature I love. I'm still learning the intricacies of neocash, so please neomail me if anything in this guide needs to be updated.
I hope Cashier Basics will help you dress to impress!
