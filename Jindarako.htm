Petpet Protection League: Neopia's Forgotten, Glitchy Mess of a contest

Introduction... With guest appearance by famous conspiracy theorist Jindarako

Deep within the magical land of Neopia, in the heart of Neopia Central, lies the headquarters of the Petpet Protection League, an international organization devoted to the welfare of Petpet kind. At the helm of this league stands Weltrude, a supposedly kind-hearted Cloud Bruce who works tirelessly alongside other league members to celebrate Neopets who have proven themselves as exemplary petpet owners.

Neopets who have taken care of the same Petpet for an exceptionally long time are granted the opportunity to showcase their devotion to their furry, feathered, or scaly companions through the form of PPL Trophies. Normally, Neopets who are rewarded with this honor receive one single trophy for their efforts, which they proudly display on their petlookups. Once a petpet has been chosen for the PPL, that same petpet can never win again.

But, alas, with so many Neopets to honor, the Petpet Protection League has not been immune to its fair share of so-called "mishaps." Throughout its history, the PPL has encountered a series of perplexing, unexplained glitches.

Jinda: And we're not just talking about a few glitches here, folks–we're talking 57 separate instances of unexplained, glitched rounds! In addition to divvying out random amounts of trophies to seemingly random winners, Weltrude has also been known to mix up the types of trophies that Neopets have earned, with some receiving Participation trophies instead of the Bronze trophies they deserved.

The Red Weeks June 11th, 2003-July 21st, 2004

The Very First PPL

The Petpet Protection League began on June 11th, 2003, two days after the Beauty Contest began. In addition to announcing the very first winner, the Ghostkerchief, on the New Features page, they also provided a quick blurb about the PPL, as well as how the contest would function:



This is pretty much the only piece of concrete information we're given about the PPL, besides the information provided on the PPL page itself. So… not a lot to go on here.

In order to figure out a rough history of the PPL and how it changed throughout the years, I used a combination of New Features update blurbs, editorials, and ánalyzing High Score Tables (thank you, Keagan).

How The PPL Functioned in the Early Days

This section is a little lengthy, so if you want the TL:DNR, I've included one at the bottom :3

In the beginning, PPL winners were hand picked by TNT members. Sometimes they were decided on a whim, and sometimes they were picked to fit with a theme. For example, December 23rd, 2003 had a Snorkle theme, and July 7th, 2004 had a Faellie theme. The Robot Snorkle and Faellie were both selected as PPL winners on (or near in the Faellie's case) their respective themed-dates.



Though this was a bit later, TNT also posted an editorial on November 4th, 2005 that confirms they hand-selected PPL winners:


Jinda: Hand-picked winners, huh? Sounds like a clandestine operation to me–fraught with corruption!! What kind of agendas are they hiding behind their selections???


In addition to being hand-picked, PPL trophies were distributed manually, rather than through some sort of automatic system. How do we know this? Because they forgot one lol

On December 20th, 2003 the Polarchuck was announced as the winner of the 25th week of the PPL; this was four days after they should have been announced (December 16th). They even acknowledged their fopaux on the New Features page that day:


Jinda: Oh they forgot the PPL...A LIKELY STORY!! Simply a way to distract us from the truth! They're hiding something...SOMETHING HUGE!!


This chaotic system was destined to cause some serious PPL mishaps...And it certainly did! For example, two separate species of petpets were selected for the PPL twice in these early days: the Gulper(Weeks 14 and 31) and the Blibble (Weeks 22 and 48).

Speaking of the Blibble, we can use his High Score Tables to demonstrate another important aspect about the PPL: no name petpets were not allowed on the table until Week 45 (Moink). Because Blibble has two PPL dates, one before Week 45, and one after week 45, we can compare the two tables:


The no name petpet shown on Week 48 would have fallen right in between Oo13oO™ and Slurpie


You see no name petpets start to pop up on the High Score Tables during Week 45 (though this was short lived, and they would later be banned, but I'm getting ahead of myself).

One final note about the early PPLs before we get into the fun, glitchy part is that something was funky with the High Score Tables. If you're familiar with how the PPL functions now, you'll know that the 200 oldest petpets are listed on the High Score Table. But this was not the case in the early days of the PPL. If you go through a lot of the earlier rounds, you'll notice that the bottoms of the tables seem to cut-off before they reach 200. Let's take a look at an example from Week 29 (Pink Warf)


Pink Warfs were attached a fairly steady rate, but they seem to randomly cut-off at 101 Jinda: Robbing pets of their hard-earned PPL trophies...sounds like something that'd be right up their alley.

You see this cutoff during most of the early weeks of the PPL, especially with popular petpets.

TL:DNR: In the earliest rounds of the PPL, winners were hand-picked, manually distributed, no names weren't allowed on the High Score tables, and High Score Tables would randomly cut-off winners.

Off to a Glitchy Start

The PPL ran flawlessly for the first seven weeks, but week eight brought warning bells of what was to come. On July 28th, 2003, the PPL awarded its first ever glitched trophy to one single Neopet: The owner of Sparky the GX-4 Oscillabot.


He was painted spotted later, but this was the site's very first glitched PPL winner! Jinda: A true criminal. What kind of sick game was this guy playing?


But, what's one extra trophy for one extra pet? Well the chaos didn't stop there! Later on October 30, 2003 (the 19th round), glitched trophies were awarded to 25 of the 120 Ghostkerfish (as well as Sluggo, the UC version of Ghostkerfish) winners. These two minor mishaps could possibly be attributed to misclicks on TNTs end, but these glitch rounds were foreshadowing some of the wildest weeks in PPL history.

Problem: The system they created didn't vibe with no name petpets.

Remember how I said no name petpets weren't being included on the High Score Table? Well... they were still receiving trophies, and I believe this was causing significant problems with the automatic-script TNT was trying out for the PPL.

Let's take a closer look at the Spotted Ramosan week. There were 38 pets who appeared on the High Score Table, but there were 41 pets who were awarded trophies. That leaves three Neopets with glitched trophies who didn't make it on the High Score Table. And what's fantastic about the Spotted Ramosan is that every winner received 3 trophies...except for three winners who were, instead, rewarded 2 trophies. There was something different about these three Neopets that caused the script to run their trophies differently. When checking those three pets, I found exactly what I was hoping to find: a no name petpet.

Jinda: But why would no names be causing problems? And why were the amount of glitched trophies so random??? THERE HAS TO BE SOME HIDDEN MEANING BEHIND IT ALL!

Well, this is where we get a tad math-y (sorry, feel free to skip to the end for the TL:DNR), but I'm quite certain some of the rounds of the PPL were percentage-based. We know that the High Score Table wasn't displaying the top-200 like it does today, and the point where the High Score Table cut-off seemed to be fairly random across the board during the early-days of the PPL. We only have one instance of TNT sharing the total amount of PPL winners: the Pink Warf week. On the New Features page, we're informed that there were 1,115 total Pink Warf owners, but the High Score Table only displays 101 winners before cutting off. This means around 9.06% of the total amount of Pink Warfs made it onto the High Score Table. But.... wouldn't you expect this to be an even number, like, let's say... 10%? Well, it probably was 10%, but no names weren't listed on the High Score Table at this point in time.

So if no names were counted when calculating the total amount of petpets, but then they weren't displayed on the High Score Table... I'd imagine this would lead to some problems with how the script awards trophies. The amount of glitched trophies that were awarded was incredibly random during the cutoff weeks, which I think is due to the different amounts of no name petpets affecting the percentages.

I also think there were two separate ways the PPL was deciding how many winners to display on the High Score Table. On weeks where there were many Neopets with the attached petpet, the High Score Table would display the top 10%. These are weeks where there appears to be a sudden cutoff on the High Score Table. On weeks where there were few Neopets with the attached petpet, the High Score Table would display all the winners (besides no names). The weeks that lack cutoffs are (Robot Snorkle, Spotted Ramosan, Green Drackonack, Blue Pinceron, Green Kadoatie, Blue Harris, Turtum, and Blibble. These also happen to be rounds where glitched trophies were awarded to most of the winners (90-110%).

TL:DNR: My proposed theory about the 23-week-streak:
Polarchucks were late, so TNT tried to create a more automated system. There were two different ways this system displayed winners on the High Score Table. If many Neopets had the winning petpet attached, the High Score Table would display the top 10% (excluding no names). If few Neopets had the winning petpet attached, the High Score Table would display ALL the winners (excluding no names). While no names were excluded from being displayed on the High Score Table, they were still being awarded trophies, and they were throwing off scripts. No names were likely to blame for the string of glitches, and the two different ways winners on the High Score Tables were listed likely accounted for the randomness of it all.

Smooth Sailing

You'll notice this era of the Petpet Protection League is significantly longer than the previous era. That's simply because there weren't any major glitches or problems with the PPL for huge swaths of time. While the previous era had 25 separate glitched weeks over the course of its one-year-span, this period of time has a total of 10 glitched rounds over the course of a six year span. Whatever they did to fix the PPL back in 2004 held steady for the most part.

The huge gap is the period between between October 26th, 2005 (Week 113) and March 19th, 2008 (Week 235) where there wasn't a single glitched round of the PPL

When they started doing personalized New Features annoucements for each petpet, they had to turn off at least part of that automatic system. This was when we got the glitched Green-Streak. So, again, when they had to stop this automatic system for these Christmas-themed weeks, things would get.... weird when they started it back up.

The Blue Wave

On January 31st, 2019, three weeks after the holiday break and the bizarre Blue Garooda round, the Blue Lynins would kick off the glitched Blue Wave. The Blue Wave was a 9-week-long series of non-consecutive glitched rounds that happened between Week 688 and Week 707.

Of Mutant Miamouse and Men

Mutant Miamouse week, which happened on April 18th, 2019, was a strange little anomaly. There were two big standout's that Keagan and I noticed while sifting through the data. The first was that there were more glitched trophy receivers than there were pets on the High Score Table, which hadn't happened since the 23-week-streak during the Red Weeks. The HST table lists 33 winners, but 34 pets were awarded glitched trophies. Who was this mysterious extra pet?? The second strange thing about the Mutant Miamouse week was the fact that, according to our list, Mutant Miamouse only received 2 glitched trophies, but nearly every winner on our list had 3 trophies total! Well... nearly every winner except for one:



Meet Jexcora the Shadow Cybunny, the only winner from the Mutant Miamouse week with 2 glitched trophies instead of 3! Jexcora was a mystery, and I had lots of questions about him. I was, thankfully, able to track down his owner, Ariel, through an external website. On the subject of Jexcora, she had this to say:

Jex was a lab ray pet, and when he turned to Cybunny thats when I decided to step back and transfer him to hihelloitsariel .. Jex was never a main pet I used on my original account, only as a lab rat. However sometimes I attach a petpet to my neopet, to transfer the petpet over to my side instead of just trading/sellin the petpet .. I have a hunch that I did this with Jex, I attached a miamouse to Jex, then when Jex was on my account I removed my miamouse and either it's in my inventory, or its attached to one of my other neopets now


Sure enough, she was able to find the Mutant Miamouse in question attached to one of her other pets:



Jinda: Shark, that's all well and good, but it doesn't explain why Jexcora has two trophies instead of three, or why his Mutant Miamouse wasn't included on the High Score Table.

Ah, impatient as you are perceptive, my friend. Well, there was one other key detail about Mutant Miamouse week that Jexcora led me to realize: the glitched trophies were dispersed on April 24th, 2019, six days after the Mutant Miamouse PPL announcement. By this point in time, PPL trophies were being delivered the day before PPL announcements. If Mutant Miamouse were announced on April 18th, 2019, they should have received trophies on April 17th, 2019. So why didn't they receive their trophies until April 24th?

Well, the winners DID receive trophies on April 17th. Or I guess I should say, almost all the winners.

Because Jexcora didn't yet have a Mutant Miamouse attached on April 17th, 2019, he didn't receive a trophy from that date. Which explains why he's the only pet with two trophies rather than three. Ariel must have attached the Miamouse at some point in time between April 17th, 2019 and April 24th, 2019. The High Score Table only displays the Mutant Miamice from the first date, meaning Jexcora was the mystery pet who was left off.

It also means that the Mutant Miamouse round is the only other known instance of a PPL trophy dispersal being run twice besides Blue Ukali. This is why most Mutant Miamouse owners have three trophies–one unglitched trophy from the first dispersal date, and two glitched trophies from the second.

I'm unsure why the trophies were run twice, but I do know they were delievered the same day as the trophies for the next week's winners: Water Smiley. If I had to guess, TNT just sent out an extra trophy batch to the Mutant Miamouse instead of the Water Smilies.

I wanted to give a special thanks to Ariel, Jexcora's owner for helping me complete this section. Without her, I don't think I would have solved the Mutant Miamouse mystery!

Purple Glitches



As you can see, there have been a measly two glitched rounds between 2019 and present day. This is less than any other era of the PPL. Because the Petpet Protection League is running entirely automatically, it means TNT isn't messing with it as much. This means there's far fewer chances for them to make the changes that result in glitched rounds.

I can't find any discernible cause for the first glitched week of this era, the Fire Blobagus (Week 826).
