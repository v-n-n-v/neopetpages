Burlap Buzz


a creepy lil' guy

Welcome! My name is peitum (aka lollypopx2), and this is my proposal for the future burlap buzz, done in the original, creepier style!

Disclaimer: I'm not much of an artist, especially in the digital medium. I know little about colour levels or shading, and I'm working with a mouse and hereditary tremor. So please excuse the less-than-perfect linework.

My chief inspiration for this design were my two favourite burlaps from the original era: the quiggle and the ogrin. The fringe/comb is comprised of assorted wires (also found on the burlap lenny), and fabric tears are used to mimic the buzz's chin points and tail hairs. The wings are peppered with moth holes and other signs of wear and age.

Some other design elements that I think are important are: an uneven fabric weave (most prominent on the burlap quiggle) and a darker colour scheme of brown, with dark blue, black or silver details. The lack of patches or pale craft straw are also important aspects of the original style!

So, a bit of backstory as to why I care so much about this particular colour. I returned to neopets from a several-year hiatus in 2019, and one of the first things that I did after logging on was check out some of the stuff I missed, namely, all the new, cool colours. Among them was burlap. At the time, there were only three: the lenny, the quiggle, and the kiko.

I fell in love with the colour. It was so cursed, so creepy, and spoke to my love of haunted dolls. The quiggle in particular was the epitome of what the colour was, and I really looked forward to seeing it released for my favourite species. But then the burlap meerca was released, marking what I see as the shift away from the 'original' style and towards the modern, cuter burlaps. The zafara followed, adding patches and beginning with the blumaroo, bright colours.

While the changes have been well-recieved, it is a depature from the style's roots. New burlaps are like old, but well-loved toys. Not a bad thing, but they're missing that vibe of a doll that you'd throw in the trash only to find it sitting on your pillow when you go to bed at night.

With the recent release of the burlap kyrii following in the more modern style, there are only two of my favourite species left: the vandagyre and the buzz. The latter probably has the best chance of making a return to the original spirit of the colour, as it's a species that is already considered a little more menacing than most!
