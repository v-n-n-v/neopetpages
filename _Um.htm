The spreadsheet method

An easy and efficient way to find stuck pets



First described at ~Tileset, here is how I use the method

OPTION A: USING A CUSTOM LIST OF NAMES

This is the best option for finding stuck pets, as you can search many more names, and you are not limited to searching number pets. However, it takes a bit more setting up.

Step one-a:

Fill your spreadsheet like so! Columns B/E and C/F contain the pets names you want to search. You can fill columns C and F with numbers or a list of words - up to you!





Make sure each name starts with one of the stuck-letter combonations, as follows:

Stuck Letter Combinations
(Thanks to the amazing people at the Lost & Pound board! For an up-to-date list, check the Pound Chat for their current board)

ANG-, ARG-, ASH-, AUS-, AVA-, AZU-, A_B-
BEL-, BIG-, BLA-, BOB-, BRA-, BUB-
CHE-, CIN-, CLA-, COC-, CRA-, CUD-, CYN-
DIN-, DOL-,DRA-, DUD-
FLI-, FOF-, FRA-, FUI-
GOR-, GRA-, GUI
HOT-, HUN-
JOS-, JUB-
KEZ-, KIK-, KOK-, KRB-, KUK-, KYL-
LEO-, LIG-, LLA-, LOC-, LUC-, LYN-, L_X-
MAR-, MEE-, MIC-,
SCO-, SEB-, SHA-, SIL-, SKE-, SLI-, SME-, SNI-, SOF-, SPA-, SQU-, STA-, SUG-, SWE-, SYN-
TIG-, TOC-, TRA-, TUF-, TWE-, TYL-
_SN-, _TO-, __G-


Note! For the third letter, letters that come AFTER that letter get stuck also

For example SUG- is a stuck letter combination. Pets names SUH-, SUI-, SUJ- etc. will also get stuck! (But pets named SUF- won't).


Step two-a:

Copy the entire spreadsheet and paste it into a text editor. Press ctrl + H to Find and Replace. Select a gap (see image) and copy+paste it into the Find What box. Then, press replace all (leaving Replace With blank).





OPTION B: USING A PRE-MADE LIST



If you want to get searching right away, this is the method for you. However, you can only search for pets with numbers at the end of their names.

Step one-b:

Copy and paste each of the following into a text editor. (The first list is 1-1000; the second list is 0000-01000).



Once in a text editor, press Ctrl+H (or navigate to the Find and replace option). Type 'replace' in the find box, and then type the name you would like to search in the replace box

Tip!: You can search for higher numbers by putting a number at the end of the name you are searching - e.g. type Namehere1 in the replace box. Also, you can try putting underscores after the name as well.

Final Steps: Follow for both Option A and Option B



Step one:

Copy and paste onto a petpage*. Don't have too many images in the single text file at once otherwise the page won't load! How many you have depends how long you want to wait for it to load - I found 4000 per page works well but if you are impatient try 500.

* alternatively, save the text file as namehere.html and load with browser - this way you don't have to remove words neo's filters don't like. If you do this, you will have to replace the first column in the spreadsheet with this:

a href="//www.neopets.com/~ (add a triangular bracket before the a)



Step two:

Find those pets! Only click on pets that are uncustomised - customised pets are guaranteed to have an owner. In the petpage, look for 'my owner is none' or similar - that means the pet is in the pound! *Also take note of pets that say there owner is 'pound_release' or 'breik_u5' - these pets will be released into the pound in the future (for more information, see /~Lot).*

*NOTE: As of 22nd of November 2019, TNT has changed petpages so that all pets in pound_release/breik say their owner is none. I don't know if this is permanent - but for now you will have to manually confirm the pet is stuck by searching for their name in the pound. If they do not show up, they are in either pound_release or breik.




Tip: Techos, Gnorbus, Poogles, Korbats and Tuskaninnies do not have this on their page.
To find if they are in the pound, hover over the top bar in the page that says 'pets, games, homepages etc found at neopets.com' to see where the link leads to. If the pet is in the pound, the link will say username=none at the end of it.

This method was used to find Stuck Pets for Fowl's Pound Gems.
