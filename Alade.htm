    account world

    What's on the tin.

    Set primarily in London— a perennially rainy place with very good curry, and packed to the gills with people from all walks of life. There is a stark contrast between the haves and have-nots, but for every rich businessman there's a starving artist. The counterculture scene is wild and avant-garde, and London is famous for its creative output. In theory, that should make up for the crowded Tube, constant mist and rumors of the supernatural.

    Fae, demons, fallen angels... people innumerable have sworn to have seen them, and made a lot of psychologists quite a bit of money. (In fact, some psychologists are in on the whole bit. London isn't exactly a city of ethics, either.)

    Skeptical? Next time you ride the Tube, look — truly look at the reflection of strangers on the windows, and don't blink quite yet when you swear your mind is playing tricks on you. They're there. It's just that the whole world seems quite more sane when you deny it.
