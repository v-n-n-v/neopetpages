TOGA: Team of Glorious Altadorians

Welcome to TOGA, or the Team of Glorious Altadorians! We are the fans and supporters of the city of Altador, the Land of Heroes. We dream of putting Altador in the number one spot on the podium, but more importantly to us still, we strive to foster an atmosphere of support, sportsmanship, and fun for our members and all others who make the journey to our lovely city to participate in the Cup. Please make yourself at home and feel free to come chat in our TOGA thread at any time. We'd love to meet you.

Sol lucet omnibus! The sun shines on everyone!

How to Join & Participate

TOGA is the official support group of Team Altador in the Altador Cup; it was established during the second AC. Simply by supporting Team Altador in the Cup you are automatically a member of TOGA, if you wish to be!
If you would like to have your name added to our members section, add a pledge, or show off an Altadorian customisation please Neomail haisydeath.

All we ask is that you follow the TOGA Rules:
I. Be respectful to other Altadorians as well as members of other teams. We are, after all, the hosts of the Cup!

II. Play fair and play hard!

III. Follow the board creation method discussed below.

Scorekeeping

What is scorekeeping?

Scorekeeping is a way that we as a team keep track of our progress during the day's competition. Every day the team starts with 0 points in each of the 4 games (Yooyuball, Slushie Slinger, Make Some Noise, and Shootout Showdown) and as players send scores, their points are added to the total points for the day.

Keeping track of points on the TOGA board should look like this:

YYB: ##,###/##,###
SS: ##,###/##,###
MSN: ##,###/##,###
SOSD: ##,###/##,###

Where YYB stands for Yooyuball, SS for Slushie Slinger, MSN for Make Some Noise, and SOSD for Shootout Showdown. The numbers on the left all start at 0 at the beginning of the day and increase as scores are sent. The numbers on the right are the team's collective goals. So at the beginning of the day the scores might look like this:

YYB: 0/10,000
SS: 0/50,000
MSN: 0/1,000,000
SOSD: 0/1,000,000

When a particular goal is reached, a new goal should be established. For example…

YYB: 10,001/10,000 NEW GOAL: 25,000

How does the team keep track of scores?

At any point in time during the tournament, one person is designated the role of scorekeeper. The scorekeeper is in charge of keeping track of points as they're posted to the TOGA board. If someone can no longer be scorekeeper, they must find someone to take over before they leave (though this does not always happen).

As scorekeeper, you are responsible for keeping track of points. A good rule of thumb is to update points once per page. For example…

YYB: 1000/10,000
SS: 5000/50,000
MSN: 100,000/1,000,000
SOSD: 100,000/1,000,000
As of page 22

Or something like that. This lets anyone who takes over as scorekeeper know when the scores were last updated.

How do I post my scores on the board?

Whenever you send a score for a particular game, you should keep track of the scores you send either with a calculator or some other method. Then post your total scores to the board. It is preferred that you play several games and post your scores in bulk. When posting scores, it is preferred to do the following:

+ 100 YYB
+ 50,000 SOSD

Which means "Add 100 points to Yooyuball" and "add 50,000 points to Shootout Showdown."

IMPORTANT NOTE: When sending scores for YYB, the total score is goals scored against the opponent minus goals scored against you.

For example, if your final score in a game of YYB is 10-2 then the score for that game would be 8.

What is the minimum score for each game?

For YYB, min score is one goal. Losses count as nothing and should not be sent. Draws count as nothing but are considered half of a win when it comes to prize points.
For SS, min score is 300.
For MSN, min score is 3000.
For SOSD, min score is one goal (35 points).
