Cellblock Avatar In One Day

In order to get the Cellblock avatar you need to play 300 games of Cellblock. For most people (including myself) this is overwhelming, but there's good news. It doesn't matter what level you're on and you don't need any wins. This is your guide to losing efficiently, essentially speedrunning your way towards the avatar. It won't cost you that much in NP either, so no need to worry about that.

For me it took between 2 and 3 hours, and I already had 20ish games played previously. It should not take you longer than this in total. If this sounds like a lot for one sitting, then you can always play fewer games at a time or per day. It's really motivating when you keep count of the number of games you play. I counted 10 at a time to keep me motivated.

Then down in a vertical line towards the "make move" button, so you can click it faster. I'm on level 6, so your games make look different if you're on a different level.



When you cannot place pieces exclusive to a vertical line, place them randomly near the make move button at the bottom.



On level 6 at least (not sure about the others), you'll sometimes win but it won't be often enough to advance to the next level. It's just funny when the NPC plays like this.

Meepit Juice Break Avatar In Zen Mode

I personally have not tried this, but I have read claims from people who have. You need to send a score of 3,500+ points in Meepit Juice Break to earn the avatar.

If you attempt this, then you will need to send your score during the same day that you started. Meaning you cannot play it over multiple days and then send your score. The day resets at midnight Neopian Standard Time (which is also Pacific Standard Time) so make sure you know what time that is in your time zone.

Each Meepit earns you 1 point. In zen mode there is no timer, so you aren't racing against the clock like in the regular mode. I've read that this can take between 10 and 11 hours, so it is still a challenge. It's do-able for those who are more ski.lled in patience than in games.

While playing I highly recommend watching or listening to something. Music, podcasts, shows, movies, or videos are all good options. On Windows you can drag your browser window to the side to make it take up exactly half of your screen. Do this to have your media and game side by side if you're watching anything. It makes long gameplay a lot easier to get through (speaking from Altador Cup experience).

Make sure to start early enough in the day so you can finish before midnight NST. Give yourself a window larger than 10 hours if possible. Also, make sure to plan on taking breaks to take care of yourself. This is a marathon and not a sprint!

If you're interested in a high score not in zen mode, read Jellyneo's guide.
