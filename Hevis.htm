Career
The beginning

Hevis works full time at the Space Station, but lives in Neopia. The commuting is kind of boring...(she usually reads the sci-fi books she likes during the trip, she even wants to receive a trophy for reading so many of them!).

She has been working there for ten years now -- time flies! She started as a trainee when she was 17. Her work used to be pretty lame, but now she's an operative mechanic and that's cool.

Her job consists of fixing things, mainly spaceship parts. Besides working in the hangar, she also has to travel sometimes. Every ship needs an operative mechanic in mission, so Hev and her colleagues take turns.

Hev is great at her job, and that fills her with pride. The downside is, if she fails or her knowledge and abilities are doubted, she goes downhill. She feels ashamed, humiliated, tries to hide her feelings and may get depressed. She will also have to feel like she has to prove herself again.

She was satisfied with her own career, but she couldn't stop daydreaming of having a ship to call her own.









And then...

Throughout all these years, she thought of having her own ship, and would always save a little here and there with thoughts of maybe one day achieving her dream. To be honest, Hevis is not the determined or diligent kind of pet, but for once and once only in her life, she actually managed to stick with her goal.

And one day, she saw a spaceship for sale, and surprisingly it costed exactly the amount of NPs she had in the bank. She didn't think for a second and grabbed this opportunity as fast as she could. It was a miracle! Her ship looked somewhat like this:

It was her dream come true!! Now she could not wait to man it and go flying throughout the universe.

The crew and their adventures

With her ship in hands, Hevis looked to the workers of the Virtupets Space Station to find pets interestend in joining her adventure. Of course, pets with good and stable jobs were not that interested, so she ended up with a bunch of outsiders, each one with a different reason to join.

First, she talked to her good friend Kalissey, whom she knew from Virtupets since childhood. Kalissey also recommended her twin brother, Kurepu. The twins are Robot Kacheeks manufactured for work in spaceships, she is a software controller and he is a pilot. They could continue doing their jobs as they always did, and in part Kurepu thought that was the wisest choice, but both of them always felt uncomfortable in the Virtupets society. It was as if they were not recognized as full pets but a mere agglomeration of wires and metal, and they craved to prove they could do better than that. In the end, Kali convinced Pu, and the twins joined the crew.

Then, Hevis heard a rumour about a pet that was found hiding in a Space Station ship when it landed. Hevis found her suspiciously checking some cargo, and excitedly introduced herself. This pet, a Wocky, answered that she was named Fedi and was a space explorer. "Wow, so cool!", said Hevis, "I really need one of those in my space crew!!", and so Fedi joined the group.

neopets history

I love all my pets, but the one I have the strongest connection to is Hevis. We have been together for 7 years already, and much before that, in the times before the conversion, I already dreamt about an Alien Aisha. I was (am!) a sci-fi/Star Trek/Isaac Asimov/you name it fan, and to me an Alien Aisha was the coolest pet that could exist. Back then, I didn't have any painted pets, though, and could not even go in the boards searching for one because of the age restriction.

A little after the conversion, I started interacting more with other users, and found an Alien Aisha up for adoption! :O I made an app, and did my best. This is the oldest art I have of Hevis, that was included in that app:

It was made by a user named scarabi (no longer active) that I met on the Art board and kindly wanted to help me! It shows a very serious/badass character, but later on Hev evolved into a sillier character, and this drawing is more like how SHE sees herself!

One of the reasons why I love her so much is that she was the first pet that I was able to adopt through an app. I believe the effort that went in her app made me love and value her even more.

Because of this story and how emotional I am about it, I was inspired to create my beloved Alien Aisha themed gallery. It is named "The Alien Alley", and includes a variety of Alien Aisha, Virtupets and space-themed items. It has won the Gallery Spotlight and it makes me very proud and happy!
