Welcome!

This is my guide to avatar games that aren't purely luck-based, like the Wheel avatars or the Lottery. The advice on here is from people who have the avatar, and/or trophy, and from JellyNeo's game guides. While I do include things from JN's game guides, and link to them, the tips here are generally things other people and I have discovered that are not on those guides.

If you have any advice for any of the avatar games, please feel free to Neomail me!

Blanket advice for improving at a game: make it a daily. That is, when you have time to play games here and send scores, send your 3 scores for the game you want to get better at. You will improve over time. You don't have to play a lot each day, but it's that daily practice that gives you practical and intellectual knowledge of what will happen, what works, and what doesn't.

A great resource is the Neopian Times. There a bunch of guides for every game on there, and by experts.

You can also ask for help on the Neoboards. I recommend the Games and the Avatars/NeoSignatures boards. Of course, I especially recommend the Friendly Neopian Lending Board (FNLB), which is on the Avatar Chat.

The 2019 Charity Corner (CC) perk, More for Less (MFL), decreases the score needed for each Flash game avatar (though Shockwave games are excluded) to 80% of the normal score. I recommend getting an 80%+ score for one or more games and then activating the perk and sending the score/s. More information here: www.jellyneo.net/?go=charity_corner&id=2019_prizes
The above information is now irrelevant as the 2019 Charity Corner (CC) perk shop has closed and did not reopen in 2020. This may become relevant again in 2021 but will we even get a perk shop in Charity Corner 2021, if we get one at all? Will there be a game perk if there IS a perk shop, given that Flash is supposed to be dead?

I may include sections with advice for non-avatar games that I am good at at some point, such as Sophie's Stew. Hopefully this will help some people get trophies in those games.

Hannah and the Pirate Caves

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=hannah_and_the_pirate_caves
Score: 150 000+
  • Use JN's guide for the hidden gems, following the instructions exactly. For instance, in level 5, don't try and get the life before getting the boulder down, because you only have one shot at getting it.

Once you get the hang of this game, try out the Ice Caves for an easy trophy--even fewer people play it because it's both a Shockwave game and doesn't award an avatar.

Godori

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=godori
Win 250 hands (not matches) in one month
  • Play on slow mode until you get the hang of all the cards and scoring sets.
  • I generally go for the starred cards (lands) first, and then the flying petpets and various flag combos.
  • Capture cards already on the table before capturing cards given in your hand.
  • Since each land has 4 cards, it can sometimes be better to hold off on playing a card from that land until one pair has been captured. Then you can guarantee that if the next-drawn card fom the deck will not give you a 3rd card from that land and giving the opponent a chance to collect all 4 cards.
  • If you have 2 cards from 1 land, this is what I call "backup", in that you don't have to worry about a 3rd card being drawn, because you can then play the second card and capture all 4.
  • Though the "world" and non-flying petpet cards can award points if you have more than 10 or 5 respectively, they are the least valuable overall. These are the cards you play first if there is nothing to valuable to capture on the table and you are starting a new land pile.
  • I call it "burning" a card if you ditch it with no plans to capture it later.
  • If you have 3 cards from 1 land, wait until the 4th card shows up, or alternatively play it if you're out of cards to "burn" to see if you draw the 4th. If you don't, and your pet doesn't immediately play the 4th card, then you capture it with another card and continue waiting.
  • If you have all the remaining cards for a land that's been dealt, e.g. the last card of a 3-card pile, play them last or when you want to hold onto your other cards to capture specific cards. You are guaranteed all 4 cards, so there is no rush to grab them.
  • Burn the world, HW flag (not part of any set), and non-flying petpet cards first if you don't see anything worth capturing. These cards are most useful in capturing other cards from their world.
  • Godori works on your phone, though it occasionally needs a refresh if it gets too laggy.

Assignment 53

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=assignment_53
Christine's (_brainchild_) guide and walkthrough
Score: 20 000+
I have this avatar after following the JN guide as well as the linked guide I found in the NT. It does take a bit of practice, and knowing where the secret areas are really helps! You can get the avatar without defeating the boss, but you do have to get a fair way through the game--I got it on the last level before the boss. It's possible to get it by the end of Act 1 of Kreludor, but if you're that good you probably already have the avatar.

*Something to note that isn't mentioned on JN or any of the guides I could find: if you lose a life, your air bonus is cut in half. Or, at least, you lose 400 points. If you lose a second life, you lose more points. I'm not sure on the maths, exactly, and I've only tested it on level 1. So ideally you'll want to get levels done without losing lives (or taking damage).

The one time you should deliberately take damage? Wuba Secundus, Act 1. After dropping down to get the force field, getting past the checkpoint, there are no more gross foods to get. However, your gravity boots will give you trouble trying to go back left to get the bottom Nerkmids (if you drop down from the left and go right, you can't get the vertical line of Nerkmids). So at some point, if you haven't already lost the boots, lose them by getting hit--but in a safe spot! I recommend hitting one of the red slug-like enemies, in a spot where you won't get knocked into thin air--like a spot where there's a nice, safe wall, for instance. Then you can finish the level easily (to get past that orange swirly thing, either duck the blast, jump over it, or jump back onto the other platform to move out of range, depending on where you first activate it).

Carnival of Terror

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=carnival_of_terror
Score: 725+
  • At the start of the game, type piecrus. When you see your ammo metre getting low, wait until you use your last shot (the bar will appear empty), then type t to refill your ammo.
  • Use the cheat code custard to refill your health metre in a similar way to using the piecrust code. Since you generally run out of ammo before needing custard, once you use piecrust, type custar and type d when your health turns red. Most games, you don't need to use custard.
  • If you're running low on ammo and have used piecrust, one-shot the clowns by hitting the body. Otherwise, either go for the arms then body, or try hit every part (head, arms, legs, then body).
  • Don't click parachutes; they're worth 1 point and not worth the shot.
  • Don't bother with pies or health powerups unless you've already used custard and your health metre is getting low.
This avatar relies a bit on luck, because you need enough time AND ammo powerups to be able to survive long enough to earn enough points. Be patient, practise, and you'll get there eventually.

My own tips:
  • Restart until you get a reshuffler block in level 1, or occasionally I'll try with an overkill + fill block because you can get it down to one/two colours left and clear the entire level, but not always.
  • Once you get that reshuffler block, don't use it until you get another reshuffler block. Then clear all colours except the reshuffler block's colour (and, if you get a multiplier block, don't clear any blocks of that colour either unless you manage to get a great combo with most of that colour's blocks, in which case use the multiplier in the first round), then activate the reshuffler. Unless it's a really rare colour, most of the time you will be able to get the reshuffler power-up again in the new randomised setup. I have had this fail before though, where the reshuffler/multiplier block will be unusable (or the multiplier will show up in a spot where you can only connect fewer blocks than you could before the shuffle, which is annoying), even with a dominant colour, so it is a bit of a risk.
  • The level with the brown blocks (260 points to pass) is where I got the avatar, although I didn't finish the level.
  • You need a healthy dose of luck in multiplier blocks with dominant colours to get bonuses as well as enough reshuffler blocks to use and clear the most blocks possible each level. It's a bit like Spacerocked, Feed Florg, and Typing Terror--if you play enough games, eventually you'll get good enough and lucky enough to get that score.

Faerie Bubbles

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=faerie_bubbles
Score: 2 000+
Every level on the game has a set configuration of bubbles, but each time you play, the elements will change. That is, the bubbles will form the same pattern, but in different colours (elements). JN's guide has screenshots. You may not get the same exact level as they have shown, but you will get the same pattern as shown.
  • When you get bubbles you don't need, fire them so they don't hit the very top. Instead, get them to stick to bubbles from below so that when the pre-existing bubbles fall, the extra ones fall too.
  • Ricocheting bubbles from the sides of the screen can be very useful. In level 18, if you have the same element bubble as the highest 2-bubble layer, or a Nova bubble (you can get one by typing stardust), you can beat the level with one shot if you aim it correctly and it ricochets off the side and right next to that top layer.
  • Do your best to avoid activating the Dark, Earth, and Air combos (although in cases where they're only on one line, Air combos aren't always bad, and Dark is preferable to Earth). Light is a bit of a wildcard; you don't know if it'll give you a good element or bad one (or make no difference) …sometimes it's not worth the risk.
  • Activate Water and Fire whenever possible (though water is immune to fire), and before Light where possible.
    For instance, in JN's guide, in the level 8 screenshot given, I often go:
    1. activate a fire combo using the fire closest to the cannon, on the right (aim the Fire between the Dark and existing fire, then between the left Dark and Water, and then in the middle of those two to burn away chances of activating a Dark combo),
    2. activate the water combo (order here doesn't matter much), then
    3. do a light combo and eliminate the rest.
  • If you need to activate an Air combo, do it ASAP to give yourself time to deal with the generated bubbles.
  • Save the slumberberry powerup for level 14 (or later).
  • In level 12 and 13, if you have a Water layer in the layer at the top (furthest from you) or middle, where possible, try activate a Water combo without even activating the layer closest to you. Then the rest of the bubbles below will fall.
  • Incidentally, levels 12, 13, and 18 are the easiest places to deal with Air combos. Since they cover the entire width of the screen, activating them takes everything below them down with them with no extra bubbles you have to deal with.
  • In levels 12 and 13, Dark isn't too bad unless it's on the very top layer, because then you do have to eliminate all the bubbles without the higher layer's combo activating.
  • In level 18, if you get a configuration with a Dark line in the 2nd highest layer (like in JN's guide screenshot), try not to activate it. Use ricochet instead to try and hit a Nova or relevant element bubble to hit the topmost layer.
  • I generally beat level 15 with bubbles (and sometimes throw in faerieland for a one-shot win), but occasionally it's mean and gives you a dark combo -_-
  • The slumberberry powerup is most useful in level 20.
  • If you have trouble aiming, get something with a straight edge like a ruler and put it against the screen to help line up your shot.
  • Restart level 1 until you get a configuration which is mostly Fire, Water, Light, or occasionally, Air (because if you get the bubbles and eliminate the other element on one side, you can activate Air so it's only on one line and thus eliminate most of the bubbles)
  • Level 4's most common pattern seems to be the one shown on JN's guide. What I do here is aim a Fire bubble so you get it between the dark and light bubbles on the left or right. So the line closest to you will be: Fire, Fire, blank, Fire, Fire. Then aim another Fire bubble into that gap, which activates the Fire combo and eliminates the need to activate the Dark combo.
  • Note that once you get to level 20, even if you lose, you'll get a 300-point bonus, so if you get there you might already be eligible for the avatar.

Freaky Factory

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=freaky_factory
Score: 1 250+
Let me start by admitting there is definitely some luck involved in getting past later levels. The blobs can be kind and they can be cruel. But with some patience, practice, and a little luck, you can get the avatar.

All toys come in different colours, and there are 3 tiers of colours: Primary (red, blue, and yellow), Secondary (purple, green, and orange), and Rainbow. The higher the tier, the higher the points. JN's guide explains in more detail. A toy can range from 1 - 15 points.
  • The best way to increase your points is to make consecutive chains of toys as long as possible.
  • Try to finish levels as quickly as possible for the time bonus.
  • There is a slight delay between filling the vats and the paint disappearing. This is enough time to overfill your vat or put in an incorrect/evil blob, which means you have to refill the vat. If you want to refill your vat right after putting in a blob (which is good for chains), wait until the paint has disappeared before clicking the next blob.
  • If there are no toys on the belt, wait until you get the required blobs into the vats before clicking on the Grundo Thief. You get maximum Thief-free time this way. If you miss the vat, the Thief might be in the right spot to save you some overflow and get whacked down.
  • You'll learn to judge the best time to click a blob with experience. Note that faster blobs travel further forward before hitting the vat, so click accordingly. Ignore blobs going too fast to get into their vat.
  • The crucial levels are levels 6 and 7. If you're lucky, you can get the avatar on level 7, but if you're not, you might only qualify on level 8.
You can see a list of the blob types on JN, along with pictures. Ranking the good blobs from most to least useful:
  1. Atomic: I've only seen this in level 7 and beyond. Fills up all vats. Excellent!
  2. Angel: fills up whichever vat it falls into. Will give you 1 bonus point if it lands on a toy that gets processed.
  3. Time: adds 5 seconds to the timer.
  4. Soap: decreases waste metre by 20%, though it's useless if you have no waste.
  5. Winged: temporarily raises speed of conveyor belt. Can add to a chain, but can also break a chain once the toys slow back down. Use at your own discretion.
  6. Glowing: raises the tier of all toys on the belt (e.g. a blue toy will become a secondary colour, and an orange toy will become rainbow).
  7. Normal: good when in the right vat. Will also whack the Grundo Thief back down if it falls on him.
Ranking the bad blobs from worst to mildly annoying:
  1. Evil: empties whichever vat it's dropped in and adds to your waste metre. It's hardest to spot if it's the blue tiniest blob, so check all of those for little white dots (the grinning mouth). No white dots means it's normal. If it falls on a toy on the conveyor belt, it destroys it and that toy will not be processed.
  2. Gigantic: fills up most of the screen, and if dropped into the right vat, completely fills it but also adds to your waste metre. Could be worth it if you're pressed for time, but usually isn't.
  3. Ice: temporarily slows down the conveyor belt. Potentially useful for lengthening chains but very rarely.
  4. Normal: if it misses and hits a toy on the belt, turns it into that colour (bad if it's a secondary/rainbow toy). Adds to waste if it misses and doesn't hit a toy.

Ice Cream Machine

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=ice_cream_machine
Score: 14 500+
  • Stick in the upper-middle (lower-middle if playing converted version) third of the screen where possible.
  • I generally leave my mouse in place as long as I can and keep an eye on that column for when new scoops come out.
  • Use strawberryvanillachocolate to get the extra life before you even start level 1.The code does not appear to work in the converted version.
  • Prioritise extra lives and point scoops; they make quite a difference.
  • Since speed and size scoop effects get reset when you activate a bonus scoop that's not a speed/size scoop, it's up to you whether it's worth it. Extra lives are always worth it, but in later games you might want to hang on to a smaller Adee over getting a few seconds' shielding or extra points. I'd say the Ollie scoop is always worth it; it's worth 1000 points. It's very rare, though. I've only seen it a few times.
  • Only activate the Strawberry Bomb if you're in a tight spot, and/or there are too many normal scoops on the screen (you want those multipliers!) and it's a fairly easy level. It clears the screen, but you don't get any of the points from the scoops it cleared, and lengthens the level. It's useful for a quick breather in later levels too.
  • If you're in a tight spot, move your mouse to the top (bottom if playing converted version) and out of the game screen, and then back in another spot. Depending on the scoop spread, it may save you a life. Sometimes you end up popping back into an unsafe spot, though, because in these situations there isn't much time to plan your escape route. Worth a shot, but it's not a guaranteed save.
  • I always do this, but I can't say for sure if it works: at the start of the game, type in the life code, and leave the game screen inactive (you can go do something else) for a few minutes. Ollie will replace Adee. Some say it increases your chances of getting bonus scoops. Can't hurt, right?

Snow Roller

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=snow_roller
Score: 6 500+
If you press the up arrow, it slows the snowball down. If you press the down arrow, it speeds it up. You want to press the up arrow all the time, but know that it does not always work. Sometimes it will work at the start, and then you get a random item or get hit and suddenly it will stop working. The black slushie (super traction) almost always makes the up arrow lose its effect. The ice slick (whoops, skidding) also usually removes the up arrow's effect. Same with the yellow paint slick. If you are on the very last stage, 3-2, and you have the game slowed down, you might want to avoid all black slushies. Fortunately, with the game slowed right down, you essentially get all items once you hit 100%, and jumping over all obstacles is easy enough at that slow speed.

It is possible that with a slow computer, you can have an easy slow game, but I have never played Snow Roller on a slow computer.

Volcano Run II

https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=volcano_run_ii
Score: 1 500+
Full guide by Lily (pregnatress) here. The main tip I picked up from it was that the extra life code isn't really helpful unless you end up dying before collecting the max number of gems (470+ points if you play it right) in later attempts. The guide mentioned that it might even be better not to activate the code, since it might actually give you more fireballs. I'd rather not risk it. I got the avatar not long after I stopped doing this, but I can't confirm or deny the rumour for sure. Other tips are in the JN guide.

Crucial tips:
  • The first attempt is vital to get the maximum distance: ~1 030+ or 730+ if you've got the Charity Corner perk activated. If you don't get up to that before losing a life, keep restarting the game until you do.
  • Avoid gems in the first life because once you run past 450 they're replaced by fireballs. Of course if it's the best/only way to avoid a fireball, go for the gems. Once you get the distance, go for the gems. Once you get to 400 bonus points, try to arrange it so you only get 440 and then you can get a trio of gems (or if lucky, two trios and/or red gems which are worth more) to get 470+ bonus.
  • Fireballs either come in pairs in wider spaces or alone with gems. Often the best way to avoid the pairs is to go between them, but sometimes going over/under both is best if they're too close together.
  • The geysers aren't too bad; just speed past them with v once they fire.
  • If you have a choice to go under or over, go under, because it's faster to click to make Glubgar fly higher than to wait for him to sink lower on his own.
