The Many Faces Of Meryl Streep

A true queen

By Aaron (iamnotaaron)

You know that clip of Oprah Winfrey talking about her love for bread? That's me, but replace bread with Meryl Streep. Since her performance in Sophie's Choice, I've been obsessed with her. I loved her versatility - how she went from glam diva in The Devil Wears Prada to schlumpy in Mamma Mia. I loved her range of singing and acting. I love how she pours so much complexities into her roles, no matter how campy (Death Becomes Her) or serious (August Osage County) the role demands from her.

So, as a loving tribute to possibly the greatest actress alive presently, here are some of my Meryl Streep-inspired looks.

Life Imitates Film

Film-worthy style

By Aaron (iamnotaaron)

Like the different styles we love dressing our pets in, film genres are defined by a very specific set of codes. Whether it's something as obvious as computer effects and costumers, or something more subtle like different lighting techniques, each genre brings the audience into this world of make-believe and submerges you into the storylines and character arcs. As an homage to the various film genres, I've designed six looks to reflect the codes within each category.

Film Noir

Forget the whole thing like a bad dream - Laura

Description:

Film noir is classified by several hallmarks: the all-important anti-hero, dramatic light versus shadow and a plot filled with plenty of twists and turns. My main reference was Alphaville, which is a great film (and the inspiration for The Cranberries' Linger music video). The main point here was to work in a colour theme that reflected black and white film, and the Manor Room Crime Scene Background was a perfect find for this because of the dramatic light and the crime scene set-up. Adding a shadow in the foreground worked well with the codes of the film noir genre as well.

  • Species: Grey Chia
  • Casual Greatcoat*
  • Striped Neovian Trousers*
  • Chia Sailor Shoes

  • Count Voon Roos Floating Shadow

  • Jazzmosis Glasses

Period films

I like good strong words that mean something - Little Women

Description:

I LOVE period films. I'm a huge fan of costume arts, and you really feel the artistry come alive when you watch a period film. Remember the huge ruffs worn by Cate Blanchett in Elizabeth? Or the sugary pastels in Sofia Coppola's Marie Antoinette? In creating this look, I finally decided on a more toned-down take, referencing the heroines of Pride and Prejudice or Little Women. I've also liked headstrong, complex women who never believed their fate lied in being subservient to marriage. The starting point was the bonnet-topped wig, and then it was all about finding complimentary clothing options that worked together. I liked the idea of contrasting the blue outfit with pink florals, and I feel like this scene could be from a lovely English garden in the 1800s.

Meet the style stars of cartoonland!

Let's be real here: there was no way I was going to do a screen-inspired issue without throwing back to some of my favorite cartoon outfits. In deciding this list, I had to weigh a few different factors. Obviously the first being whether the site even had the proper items for a solid recreation, but also what aspects of these cartoon figures made them stylish in my mind.

I went through a whole list of characters that included The Powerpuff Girls and Sailor Moon, but ultimately, I arrived at these five names. Read on to see how I interpreted them!

Elsa

I never knew that winter could be so beautiful

Description:

Elsa was a given choice from the moment I started this list. I loved the combination of her dress and cape, and that slightly-undone-but-totally-perfect braid? Ugh, adore. Most of the pieces came together very easily for me, and I chose a snowflake shower over icicles because I wanted to have a softness. I also wanted to show her power through her hands, but unfortunately, there was no blue marking that I could use. Small Screen, Big Style: Villanelle

Meet TV's chicest assassin.

By Aaron (iamnotaaron)

If you haven't watched Klling Eve yet, you need to add that to your program list. Without going into too much details (I don't want to get into trouble with the filters), the TV show is a cat-and-mouse game between two well-written and complex female characters. The main antagonist is Villanelle, who's played by Jodie Comer. She's psychotic, cold, passionate and very stylish.

Given that this issue celebrates both movies and TV shows, I wanted to see if we could give Villanelle a Neopian spin. Here are the results!

Pink and poufy

Sometimes when you love someone you do crazy things

Description:

I don't use the word "iconic" lightly, so when I say that this look is iconic, you should believe it. Early on in the first season of the show, the audience is immediately clued into Villanelle's sense of fashion from this singular scene. Dressed in a frothy, bubblegum pink dress whilst standing in the middle of a Parisian courtyard, Villanelle contrasts the sweetness of the dress with a pair of rough-and-tumble black leather boots. It's the perfect way to introduce who she is, without saying anything. I stayed true to the original look, working with a pink dress, a cobbled walkway and an old looking lamp. The added flourishes of the makeup and the wig help to play on the sweetness of the look.

what is bespoke?

Bespoke was started from the idea of making a lookbook feel a little different. Rather than have one singular and unchanging theme, Bespoke tries to make lookbooks a little bit informative and engaging by having the site theme change every two months. So on top of getting thematic ideas on how to dress your pets, Bespoke tries to also give you styling ideas on what else you can do with your wearables, so you can get more mileage out of your pet's closet. After all, you've spent good neocash and neopoints on your wearables, so make your spending worth every dime!

What's more, Bespoke also tries to give everyone a voice, and make customisation more inclusive. With an ever-revolving roster of themes, it allows you to flex different creative styles and approaches!

At Bespoke, we hope that what you see will inspire you to get creative and have fun with the wearable options that Neopets has to offer.

Bespoke was launched on June 9, 2020.

story ideas

Here are some ideas or prompts to help you craft your story. As always, if you're unsure of anything, please do not hesitate to drop one of our staffers a neomail!

  • Lookbooks: These can be based on a theme, a character, or based on a real-life icon. You can include tips and tricks as well to achieving certain looks. The options are endless!
  • Editorials: Editorials are more text-heavy. For example, it could be an interview, a round-up of items, or an opinion piece about a certain wearable. If you're unsure, check with staff first to see if your idea can be done into an Editorial.
  • Guides: These helpful articles are chockful of useful tips and plenty of reliable information. These can range from guides for first-time NC traders or buyers, to something more complex such as layering different items to create new substitutes.
  • Columns: These recurring stories are more image-based, and usually have a set template. Examples include "Three Ways With", "This vs. That" and "Trend Watch". Columns can also include text-based stories such as "Ask A Stylist". To create your own column, please check with staff.

ask us anything

Q: What is Bespoke?
Bespoke is a lookbook, but it's presented in the format of a magazine. Each issue has a theme, and the types of customisation ideas change along the way.

Q: How often is Bespoke updated?
We update each issue with new contributions all the time. Every two months, we will revamp the issue as well.

Q: Why are you doing this?
Personally, I know many users love to customise their pets, but given the vast amount of wearables out there, it can be a bit confusing as to where to start. My hope is that Bespoke gives everyone new ideas so that they can explore the many choices that customisation offers!

Q: I really like a look on Bespoke. Can I reference it?
Yes you may, but please do leave a credit where possible.

Q: Can I enter a look into the Customisation Spotlight?
Absolutely not! All the looks here are lovingly made by fellow players, and it would not be fair if you won a trophy based on another person's work. If we see it happen, we will have to report this to TNT.

Q: Can I contribute to Bespoke?
Of course! Please have a look at our Contributors section!

Q: Can I contribute to an issue that is already launched?
Yes you may! We will update the issue every time we get a new contribution!

Q: Does Bespoke only feature NC items?
We try to have a mix of both NC and NP items, and we also try to include a list of the items used in our customisations for your reference as much as possible. NC items are tagged with a * sign.

Q: In customizing for Bespoke, must I already own the items?
Nope, you can use Dress To Impress or Jellyneo's Wardrobe to access an unlimited supply of wearable options!

Q: Can I contribute just a look without fitting it into a story?
Of course you can. Bespoke aims to be as accepting as possible, so if you have a look that you would love to show off, please let us know! We're happy to help you with the text, and we might even get some inspiration from you to build more looks to fit into the story! Please read our contribution guidelines for a more detailed explanation.

Q: Can I list my pet on a permanent lookbook?
We're working on a permanent lookbook based on species for everyone to show off their fave pet customisations. Also, we will eventually roll out a platform where you can trade NP and NC wearables. Stay tuned!

Q: I have a question that's not listed here.
No problem! Please neomail us and we'll try to get back within 48 hours.
