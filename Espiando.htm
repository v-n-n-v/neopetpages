SCRIVENER

Home

Hello and welcome to Scrivener, a private guild for adult writers. Scrivener's goal is to create a relaxed community where members can chat about writing, plus encourage and motivate each other. Check out our activities page for challenges, prompts, and events to get you writing!

Scrivener opened on August 16, 2023.

rules

We ask that members of our community follow the rules below to make this a pleasant space for everyone. If someone's behavior harms the community, they may be kicked from the guild.

1. No drama, no harassment, no spam. Follow general message board etiquette and just generally be nice to people.

2. Be truthful. Since we don't want to force anyone to post their writing on Neo, we trust members to accurately report word counts, challenge completions, etc. and to act within the spirit of these activities rather than trying to maximize or farm points. If you are found to have abused this trust, you will be removed from the guild.

3. Adults only. Those under 18 are not eligible to join Scrivener.

Joining Scrivener

Interested in joining Scrivener? Great!

Scrivener has no activity requirements and permits side accounts, though you may not join the guild on more than one account. We also do not require writing samples; Scrivener will never demand that you share your writing, though you are of course welcome to do so.

(Note: Guild journals haven't been designed yet, so you can't actually create one yet – consider this paragraph a glimpse of the future.) The optional first step to joining Scrivener is to create a guild journal that you'll use to keep track of your activities and points. This is optional; you can join the guild, chat, and participate in some activities without it, but you must have a guild journal to earn points and rank up. You can also always create a guild journal later and have it added to your profile, but creating it first makes things a little easier on the admin end.

The next step (or first step, if you choose not to make a guild journal right now) is to send in the following form. This information will be used to create a profile for you in the Members section.

Please note in your Neomail if you'd prefer not to have your age in your member profile.

Optionally, you can provide a 200x200 or smaller image to be included in your profile. Feel free to use a Neopets image for this, make your own, or use a graphic by someone else, as long as you provide credit. If the image you're using is not hosted on Neopets.com, you'll have to put it on a petpage or other spot for us to pick up – you can include credit for an image you didn't make in this pickup.

Be sure to send the form from the account you'll be joining the guild with, as that's where your invite will be sent.

Activities

Scrivener is always interested in members' suggestions for new activities — just post on the guild message board!

writing sprints

In a writing sprint, everyone writes for a specific timed interval, and then we come together to compare our word counts and talk about what we got done at the end.

Scrivener runs its writing sprints publicly on the Neopian Writers board – you do not need to be a member to join our sprints! Feel free to come in and write.

    story chain

    Our story chain is a collaborative project between our members, in which each person continues the story created by the previous writers. Each story chain has 7 pieces of 300-500 words each.

    To find our story chains both current and past, check out the story chain page.

    The story chain is hosted by Bay, and any submissions for it should be directed to her, as should questions about submitting or other aspects of the story chain.

    monthly word count goal

    Each month, we set a monthly word count goal. Members can contribute to this goal by getting words on the page for any project they feel like working on. These words do not have to be written during a sprint or other guild event; just let us know via the guild message board what progress you're making!

    If/when we reach our goal during the month, we move on to a larger stretch goal.
