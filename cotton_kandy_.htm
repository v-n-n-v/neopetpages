glossary of terms
just like any place on Neopets, the Beauty Contest has its own set of vocabulary. These things may seem foreign to you if you are just starting to BC, so I've included this handy glossary for you! Feel free to come back here and re-read them if you are ever unsure of what something means.

In this guide, I have a list of words you should know before reading each section. If you've forgotten what they mean, click them and you will be redirected back here.

Advertising - This is what you need to do in order to get votes! Spread your BC Link around a lot so that people will see it and vote! Talk up your entry and ask people to vote!

Advertspam - Same as "Advertising" but this term is usually used with negative connotations, such as saying the same thing over and over to get votes.

Anthro - Short for "Anthropomorphic." A pet who stands on 2 legs and has human-like characteristics.

Art Chat - (also known as: Art Board) Forum where you can advertise your BC entry, vote, and talk about all things art!

BC - Abbreviation for Beauty Contest.

Beauty Contest - A weekly competition in which you enter a piece of art of your pet that you drew. The number of votes you get by other members of the neo-community determines if you place. You can win 1st, 2nd, or 3rd Overall or in the individual specie.

Beauty Contest Boards - (uncommon abbreviation: BCb). Former name of the Art Board

Competition - The other entries that are in the contest. This mainly applies to pets in the same species category as you.

Critique - (abbreviation: crit). Offering advice on a person's picture. A good critique will include things the person did well, and things the person could improve on.

Digital Art - Used to refer to art that was drawn on the computer or other electronic device.

Filesize - How much space your file takes up on a computer's disk. This does NOT mean the physical dimensions of the image.

First Come First Serve - (uncommon abbreviation: FCFS). Voting for whoever gets to the topic first.

Free Votes - Usually in a topic title telling people that they have votes that are unused. Post your entry here!

Goldban - (past tense: Goldbanned). If you win 1st place you can't enter the contest for another 30 days. This is to prevent repeated winning by the same pet/person.

Kikocat - (also known as KC or the Contest Judge) Basically the coolest person ever! She is the TNT member who looks through all the entries and essentially runs the BC every week. She is the contest_judge TNT account and sometimes answers questions on the boards.

Manual Voting/Votes - (abbreviation: MV). Going through the entries on the BC voting page and picking which entry to vote for in each species.

Non-Silent Voting/Votes - (abbreviation: NSV). The voter is telling you who they vote for. Usually by posting: "Your vote has been cast for ____".

Quad - Short for "Quadruped." The pet is more animal-like, generally on all four legs.

Silent Voting/Votes - (abbreviation: SV). Not posting on the board who they have or haven't voted for. Usually done to prevent others from getting mad if they didn't get the vote.

Traditional Art - Used to refer to art that was not draw on the computer (with programs such as Photoshop or Paint). Examples are watercolors, colored pencils, or markers.

WIP - Abbreviation for: Work in Progress. An unfinished picture that someone is still working on. May want advice also.

☞ quick q + a

Q. What time does the BC start?

A. As of July 13, 2012: 12 PM NST every Friday!


Q. Can I enter on a side account?

A. As of April 4, 2008: YES! You can enter on a side account. You can only advertise from the account the pet is on though!


Q. How many pets can I enter at a time?

A. As many as you like! You can enter all of the pets you own, but keep in mind you need to advertise on your separate accounts for the pets!

A. One vote for each Neopets' species each contest.

A. You can get a shiny trophy for your pet's Trophy Cabinet! It will show up on their lookup as well.

A. Place 1st, 2nd, 3rd in species or overall with your entry! The avatar is not retro-active, so if you won the BC before the avatar was active, but haven't won a trophy since, you will need to re-enter and place with your entry to get the avatar.

now that you have a feel for what the BC is, you can learn about what you've been waiting for: how to actually enter the contest and compete! Entering can be super easy if you make sure your entry follows all of the Contest guidelines.

1. Files must be in a .jpg or .gif filetype
2. Files must be under 75kb in filesize
3. The artwork MUST BE DRAWN BY YOU
4. No unedited Neopets pictures

Those things seem pretty simple to follow right? If you stick to those rules, your entry should get uploaded to the Neopets server and entered into the contest!

There are still a few things to consider, though, before you enter the contest. Read on to learn more about those considerations

☞ species difficulties

Certain species of Neopets are harder to place in than others. Generally, the more popular species are harder because there are a larger number of people who can enter.

The number of entries in each species has decreased dramatically since around 2014. Fewer entries means more chances for your image to win. The species below are guidelines, but sometimes there are only 3 entries in categories--a guaranteed trophy!

The page below gathers data from different contests to determine how popular different species are. Check it out for some hard data and numbers about species popularity (and, therefore, difficulty)!

☞ time of year

As odd as it may seem, you also should consider the time of year you enter the BC. At certain times of the year more people enter, which could reduce the chance of a new trophy for your pet! Here are the times of year when the BC is most active:

◊ Winter holidays
◊ Summertime
◊ Holidays like Halloween or Valentine's Day
◊ User-run theme weeks

Usually the BC is busiest at the time when people are on breaks from school because there is more free time to go on Neopets! This might be the best time for you to enter as well, so just be ready for a lot of competition! Also, people like to dress their pet up for special holidays that come around, so Halloween, St Patrick's Day, or Valentine's Day can be busy times to enter.

If you can enter at an "off" time, you might have less competition. Be sure to pick a week where your schoolwork or job isn't so hectic, so you still have time to advertise.

☞ important do's and don'ts!

Here are some things to remember when you are drawing your entry and getting ready to enter the contest! These things are very important. Some of them will help to ensure that your entry is acceptable by the Contest Judge's rules, so be sure to pay attention!

things you can do in the bc

◊ DO be creative - Unlike other site spotlights on Neo, like the Art Gallery or Neopian Times, you are allowed to enter a picture of you pet with its "custom design" -- you don't need to stick to just a Red Gelert or an Island Ixi. If your pet has unique markings or clothing, you are allowed to draw them like that and enter the BC. Note: You may draw a pet a different COLOR than it actually is!

◊ You CAN have a background - Sometimes a background will show that you have put more work into your BC entry and will generate more votes. Be wary though, because backgrounds can make your image look cluttered or distorted and Manual Voters might shy away from viewing your entry. Note: You cannot use a photograph as a background image.

◊ DO make your image look like a Neopet - Your entry needs to resemble the Neopet species it is more than a human or animal when you enter the BC. Your entry won't be accepted by the Contest Judge if it looks "too human" or "too realistic". Also, you must draw your Neopet in the species it CURRENTLY is... not what you hope it will be one day! (So, if your pet is a Kau, you need to draw it as a Kau, not a Draik!)

◊ You CAN enter an animation - As long as you can get it under the 75kb file limit, you are allowed to enter an animation into the BC. Usually they are just eye blinks, but I've seen them go as complicated as a running Lupe!

◊ You ARE allowed to use Neopets images - If the image came from neopets.com artists you may utilize it for the BC. You do need to alter or edit it in someway.

things you can't do in the bc

◊ DO NOT use art that you found - Art that you enter into the BC must be made by YOU. This means: 1. no collaborations with another user 2. no makeables that you colored in 3. no image of a "Baby Xweetok" that you searched for and found on Google or another website. If it was not created by you or is official neopets artwork you altered you cannot enter it in the BC.


As seen on the "Enter Contest" page. (Drag to address bar for full view).

◊ no photography - Whether or not the photo was taken by you, the BC is not the place to use a photograph in any part of your image (including as a background). You can use a camera to digitally capture your draw or painted entry, but you should crop out everything but the drawing.


[source]

◊ DO NOT put (c) or your art website on your entry - It is important to put identification on your image incase it get stolen (and the art thief dumbly forgets to remove any text--it happens!). On the flip side, you can't put "(c) USERNAME" or your off-site art account (or any offsite website for that matter). You should simply put your username somewhere on your entry. To be on the safe side, I have started putting the username of the account the pet is on when I entered my image.

◊ AI "art" is NOT allowed - TNT has made a stance that AI "art" is not allowed in creative contests such as the Beauty Contest. [source]

◊ No 3D art is allowed - This includes both traditional 3D art (sculptures, plushies etc) as well as digital 3D art. If that is your medium, the Art Gallery should be where you enter your images and not the BC. [source]

how to save your entry
once you have drawn your entry, its time to save it to enter it in the contest! Saving an entry for the Beauty Contest is one of the hardest things to do when you first start out in the BC. There are specific requirements for the BC, so sometimes getting them to fit those requirements can be tricky, especially since the requirements are a little dated.

☞ saving: tips from a pro

First, I want to tell you some tips I've learned over the years of BCing. These don't necessarily have to do with getting your image to fit the requirements, but they are good to keep in mind when you are going to save your entry!

◊ resize your entry to 800 pixels wide or smaller - Whether you are working in a large canvas in photoshop, or your photo or scanned image file is really large, reducing the height and width of your entry will help you overall. Smaller dimensions means the filesize will be less, which will help you get it under 75kb.

The second reason to keep your entry to a maximum 800 pixels wide is because you will stretch out the entry screen. I entered my Kau, below, to show you what happens if you have a long photo. This can cause some people to have to scroll left and right to see your image! When you are in the BC you want everything about it to be as easy as possible for the potential voter, and making them scroll isn't helping you at all!


Keep your entries under 800 pixels long or you'll stretch the voting page!
(Drag to address bar for full view).

◊ Background vs. no background - I touched on this before in the Drawing & Preparing your BC Entry section, but its also important to consider when you're about to save an image for the BC!

As I said before, having a background makes your piece look like it has a lot more effort put into it. The drawback is it can deter from Manual Voters, because the thumbnail won't be as clear! When saving your image for the BC, you might consider erasing the background, or cropping the image so not all of the background is showing. These two things can also reduce the filesize, which again will help to get your image to the 75kb filesize limit!


Right: Image with background (used for petpage layout).
Left: Background removed, and ready to enter into the BC!

◊ Reduce the quality instead of cropping your image - A lot of times when people are trying to get their image under 75k, they will crop the image or resize it really small! Instead of cropping until all you have is a headshot of your pet, reduce the quality of the image first! (I will go over this in the "how to save in certain programs" sections below)


The one on the left isn't as awesome looking as the one on the right!
Before you crop that far, try reducing the quality of the image!

☞ faq about saving & resizing

One of the most asked things about entering the BC for the first time is "how do I resize and save my entry?". I hope to answer a couple of frequently asked questions about this process in hopes to make it easier for you. If you don't see the answers here, you can also make a board on the Art Chat and people who are more experienced in the contest will always be willing to answer!

Q. What program should I use to save my entry?
A. In most programs you can save directly in the program you are working in! Look for a "Save For Web" or "Export" option on your menu!

Q. I am using Procreate and I am having trouble resizing my entry. What do I do?
A. I realllyyy don't like saving my BC entries in Procreate. I usually export it as a PNG and use photopea.com (free photoshop) to save it as a JPG under 75kb. (File → Export As → jpg).

☞ common reasons for being removed

◊ You entered an "unedited" neopets image - This means that you used an image drawn by the artists at Neopets and entered it into the contest without making any alterations to the image. You may use official Neopets images, as long as you have made some changes to them to make the image your own! Here is an example of the difference between the two: (edited version by mindsend. Used with permission.)


On the left we have an unedited neopets picture. On the right we have an edited version.

◊ You entered a customization image of you pet - This is similar to the reason above. This is considered an unedited Neopets image that you did not draw on to alter. If you would like to show off your pet's customization, you can enter them into the Customization Contest.

◊ There was a (c) symbol on your entry - As I said in my Drawing and Preparing your BC Entry section, you are not allowed to have a copyright (c) symbol or a link to an off-site website on your image. Simply remove it, add your neopets username, and re-enter your artwork!

◊ Your pet looked too much like, or was dressed like a copyrighted character - Characters such as Pokemon, Harry Potter, or Disney are very recognizable. Because of copyright laws, Neopets does not allow images in the contest that closely resembles other copyrighted characters.

◊ Your image was too realistic - While you may be very good at drawing animals, or you may have used a photograph of a real animal as a reference for your image, this is a contest on Neopets, so your image must resemble a Neopet more than a real life animal. Try to include aspects of the Neopet in your drawing!

◊ Your anthro drawing is too human-like - Same as above, this is a Neopets contest, so your entry needs to look more like a Neopet than a human! Kikocat has said that she generally judges by the nose: if you have a human or human-like nose, then you will most likely be rejected. Also, be wary of the skin/fur color. If your Neopet is Anthro, but also has fur that couple be considered a skin tone, it may be mistaken as just a human rather than a Neopet. (Honestly, this usually happens with light beige or tan colored fur, which is a whole other discussion we could unpack). Another thing to be aware of is your anatomy. Make sure its neo-appropriate! In summary, your entry can be considered "too human" for these reasons:

◊ human-shaped nose
◊ fur is skin-toned
◊ inappropriate (not neo safe) anatomy

◊ Using firefox to upload your entry - If you tend to use Firefox as your primary browser, you may want to use another one (such as Chrome) to upload your BC image. Sometime Firefox can send in a blank image as opposed to your entry, and therefore the Contest Judge will reject it.

Besides those reasons above, Kikocat mentioned that she also looks for these things to reject an entry from the contest (either before it starts, or during the current contest):

◊ art theft
◊ photographs
◊ anything not drawn (sculptures)
◊ anything that violates any site or chat rules
◊ religious symbols
◊ URLs
◊ country flags

If your entry contains any of the above reasons it will be removed form the contest. One that is of special interest to me, and other BCers is art theft. If you'd like to learn more about art theft please click the link below!

☞ other reasons your entry was removed

The BC has gone through some subtle changes lately, so it's important to note that some entries have been removed during the contest for reasons other than what was mentioned above.

◊ Spamming your BC link - If you are advertising your entry on a board other than the Art Chat it is important to not spam people with your entry. Be sure to contribute to the conversation and stay on topic! Bumping your board with your BC link can also be considered spamming if done off the Art Chat.

◊ Your link was in the neoHTML part of your font - As silly as it may seem, your link to your BC entry must remain in the neoSignature part of the font, not the neoHTML part. This is because people are able to turn off seeing other people's signatures.

advertising

Terms to Know
Advertising | Art Chat

now that you have successfully entered the contest. . . Now what? NOW comes the hard part! A week-long trek of advertising! What is advertising and how do I do it? Read on to find out!

Advertising is when you display and post your entry so that others will see it and vote for you. The more people that see your entry, the more votes you can possibly get. The more votes you get, the greater chance you will place 1st, 2nd, or 3rd and get a shiny trophy for your pet! Cool, huh?

The number one thing you need to advertise is the URL to your BC entry:

* * * //www.neopets.com/beauty/details.phtml?pet=YOURPETNAMEHERE * * *

Replace "YOURPETNAMEHERE" with the name of your pet that you entered in the contest and you have your entry URL!

☞ where to advertise

There are several places where you can advertise so that people may see your Beauty Contest entry.

◊ Your Userlookup - Putting the image and a link to your entry on your userlookup can be helpful for people who lookup your username or click your name when you are chatting on the NeoBoards. You may only put the link on the account the pet you entered is on

◊ Your Pet's Lookup - Again, this is a great place to put a link to your entry. Someone may search your pet and visit their lookup, see your entry and vote!

◊ Your Pet's Web Page - This is the second best place to put your entry! Someone may be looking through pages via other pet pages and see you're in the BC and vote for you! Another great thing about petpages is they are linked from your pet's BC entry page. On a petpage you can put a better version of your picture, which may generate more votes!

◊ The Art Boards - The number one place to get votes is the board designed for us: The Art Board. Putting your BC entry URL into your neoHTML or pasting it on your board for people to see is the fastest way to get your entry out there! Who knows how many people were lurking (not posting) and saw your entry?!

If you are new to web coding, creating links, or neosignatures, please click on the links below to find out more information on how to use these while you're in the BC!

alright friends! Its time for a crash-course in the Art Chat (formerly known as the Beauty Contest Boards). If you are new to the board, this is good to read, so you can understand the dynamics of how we operate! In a nutshell, we are a quiet and self-sustaining board.

◊ The General Art Chat - Before the name changed, there was no one place to talk about art or other creative endeavors on neo. On the Art Chat you can talk about your art, petpage making, adoptables, as well as your BC entry!

◊ Trolls are Rare - It's very unlikely that you will find posts or topics just to annoy people. Occasionally we will get the newbie who thinks its a topic about make-up and celebrities, but 95% of the boards are on-topic.

◊ We aren't Report-Happy (unless you steal our art!) - If you are doing something wrong, a lot of times people will tell you (nicely), rather than just report you right away. If someone points out that you aren't following the rules. . . listen! :) There are a lot of BC oldies who have experience in the BC and know it inside and out. We want to educate you, not get you into trouble.

◊ The "Contest Judge" is a person named Kikocat - Unlike other places on the site where TNT is a huge entity that doesn't listen to you, here in the BC we have a member of TNT named Amanda, who is watching over us. She monitors the boards at times, admits entries into the contest, and deletes them if you are found not following the rules. A lot of BCers have her email and are in direct contact with her to clarify rules and report problems.

◊ This is NOT the Pound Chat! - Some people may spam the PC for votes (please see next section), but when you come to the Beauty Contest Board remember this: we don't care if your pet is Uncoverted or nicely painted or rare. We care about your art.

☞ types of boards

Since the Art Chat now encompasses both the BC and the general art chat, you will find a variety of topics here.

◊ Voting Boards - These boards are where people will advertise that they are voting for other's BC entries. There are a couple of different ways to vote. They may advertise that they are Silently Voting, or Non-Silently Voting, or voting Manually.

◊ Vote For Me! Boards - Boards advertising the person's pet. These are for other contestants and lurking voters to come take a look at their entry and hopefully gain a vote!

◊ Critique Boards - Critique boards--whether offering or asking for them--are to help artists improve. If you are giving a critique, be sure to mention what they are doing well in their art as well as what they can improve on. A "Critique Sandwich" is a good thing to keep in mind:

1. Something that they are doing well
2. Something to improve upon
3. Something they are doing well or an overall assessment of the artwork

Here's an example of the above "Critique Sandwich": Wow! I love that you added a background to your BC entry. The perspective on it is a little off though, maybe try looking at photographs as a reference to help with that. Overall, it is a strong piece!

When giving a critique it's important to remember there is a person behind that username! Don't be afraid to ask or receive feedback on your artwork. It's how you can grow as an artist!

◊ Art Requests & Trades - Sometimes people will advertise that they are willing to draw art for other people. Art Trade means you each draw art for each other. It's important to remember if you partake in an art trade, to get your half done in a timely manner! Art Request means you do not have to draw anything in return for a piece of art. They are doing this out of the goodness of their heart!

◊ Adoptable Boards - Adoptables are free images you can use. Usually they are done in multiples of the different pet colors and species.

◊ User-Run Competitions - Sometimes users like to host their own competitions (free of prizes, except bragging rights!). These are generally art or drawing competitions with rules, prompts, and community-building. Most notable examples are: Operation Style, shIIne

◊ User-Run Theme Weeks - Theme weeks have been going on for years. Recently, they have begun to pick back up in popularity. Theme weeks are generally organized by one or a few users together. They will pick a theme and a BC week that the theme week will take place. When users enter the BC that week they can enter a picture around a certain theme. Click here for a page on recent theme weeks.

◊ Art & Character Guild Topics - A lot of times users are looking to find others who have the same interests as them. People who are a part of art or character guilds will sometimes advertise them here. Keep a look out if that's something you're interested in.

◊ The BC Fun Board - Usually these are numbered, because once one board ends, another will begin! This is a general chat board to connect with other artists and BCers.

beauty contest etiquette
just like any other game, it's important to have good sportsmanship when competing in the BC. Here's a couple of suggestions that you should consider when entering the BC and when you interact with your fellow artists.

☞ good BC etiquette

◊ Comment on others entries - Often times your fellow BCers will make a board advertising that they are now voting. Its a common courtesy to look at their entry and comment about it. (It would be ill advised to say that you hate their entry... even if you do...)

◊ Limit how many times you use an image - There are no rules against entering the same piece of art into the BC more than once. That said, BCers feel that if you've already won a trophy with it once, you shouldn't enter it again. This is your chance to try something new!

Important Note: If you've won an Art Gallery trophy with a piece of art, you may not enter it in the BC!

☞ unfavorable BC etiquette

◊ Do NOT copy and paste the same message to every topic - Have a cookie-cutter "Vote for me!" message is just annoying (not to mention its considered spam, and you can be reported for it!). Instead, tailor your posts to the topic. Read the first post and interact with your fellow BCers! Its a good way to make friends!

◊ Do NOT ask for a vote from your same-species competition - Most of the time people vote for their own pets in the BC, so its pretty useless to ask for a vote from someone who is competing in the same species as you.

◊ Don't insult your competition - Claiming you have the "best entry" in your species is another way of saying your competition's entries aren't very good at all. Remember this contest is for fun, and all skil levels are welcome to compete in the BC!

◊ Don't start the Traditional Art vs. Digital Art argument - For the love of Fyora, Traditional Art isn't harder than Digital Art or vise versa. Both take time and skil to become good at. Some people prefer one over the other, but that doesn't mean there is a "right" way to create art.

☞ BC faux pas

A "faux pas" is a violation of accepted social norms and comes from the French meaning "false step". This doesn't mean these things are against the rules or necessarily rude behavior on the BC boards. Instead, they are things that are overused, annoying, or pet peeves.

◊ ❝It's my first time in the BC. Vote for me?❞ - Although it would be nice, you may not win a trophy on your first try. I see this line used over and over and by now its just stale and has lost any meaning to even get votes.

◊ ❝Vote for my UC/expensive color pet?❞ - The competition should be about your artwork that you entered rather than how rare your pet is considered. (Also it makes you look at bit pretentious).

☞ bc spam

This topic seems to be less and less important in recent years. It was commonplace to find BCers advertising on other neoboards besides the Art Chat. Although always against the rules, this "BC spam" was hardly ever enforced.

As of Fall 2014, TNT started removing entries from the Beauty Contest if they found them posting outside the Art Chat to get votes. Instead of warnings for spam, the punishment for "BC Spam" now is a lot harsher and doesn't seem to be worth the risk. Stick to the Art Chat to get votes from now on folks!

voting
whether or not you are entered in the Beauty Contest, you may want to participate in voting. You don't have to be entered in the contest to vote! In this section we will go over voting rules, and the different ways you may approach voting.

☞ beauty contest voting rules

◊ You can only vote from your main account - Like earning neopoints, and doing your dailies, voting in the beauty contest may only happen from your main account. Even if you are entered in the BC on a side account, you must log into your main account to vote.

◊ Buying votes is against the rules - Trying to buy votes with neopoints or items is considered cheating and against the rules. People must make up their own choice about voting, you can't bribe them!

◊ Vote trading isn't allowed - Similar to trying to buy votes with NPs or items, you cannot promise to vote for someone if they vote for you. This is considered cheating as well. (Plus then you only have like 50 votes, and you'll need way more than that to place overall!)

☞ ways to vote

◊ Manual Voting - Sometimes users will go through each species and look at every (or most) entries and pick which one they want to vote for. This is sometimes a long process, but those who are dedicated to picking the best entry will usually do this.

Sometimes users will make a board saying they manual voted. It's important to note that you can ask if this person voted for you, but you cannot post a list of who you voted for in each species. TNT is all about letting people make their own choices in voting, and this could be considered advertising for those entries. People might be more inclined to vote for them because a MVer thought they were great.

If you choose to MV and want to make a board topic saying that you did or are going to, just let people post and ask if you voted for them! That way you just have to say yes or no, and aren't considered advertising for them!

◊ Silent Voting - This is a very common practice among people who vote in the BC. Voting silently means you don't tell who you did or didn't vote for. This sort of lets you keep your votes to yourself, and (maybe) not hurt anyone's feelings along the way!

◊ Non-Silent Voting - You can vote non-silently if you want to tell the world who you voted for. Most commonly this is done by posting the massage that comes up after you have voted for a pet: You vote has been cast for PETNAME. If you post anything but that, people may question whether you really did vote or not.

◊ Voting First Come First Served - When you advertise that you are voting this way, that means that you are voting for the first one of each species that comes to your board. I am personally biased against this because there may be others out there who want your vote, but didn't quite make it in time. This means you are voting on speed instead of the quality of artwork. This is a good way to quickly vote for the contestants that week, though!

* * *

Special Note: It's important to note that however you chose to vote, that you don't lie to other users about voting for them. It's pretty easy to forget who you've said you voted to, and you will most likely get caught in your lie. Being honest and saying you didn't vote for someone may be hard and you may not want to hurt someone's feelings, but it's better in the long run to just tell the truth!

☞ the save our sides campaign

Currently you can enter the BC on your side account, and when the BC first started you were allowed as well, but there was a period of time where entering your pet into the BC from an account other than your main was against the rules.


{{ source }}

The reason for the change in the rules was that the BC used to offer Item and NP prizes for placing 1st, 2nd, or 3rd, both for the overall winners and the individual pet species. Here are what the prizes used to be before 2008:

OVERALL PRIZES

1st: 20,000 NP + ONE rare item + 1 gold trophy
2nd: 10,000 NP + ONE rare item + 1 silver trophy
3rd: 5,000 NP + ONE rare item + 1 bronze trophy

SPECIES PRIZES

1st: 10,000 NP + 1 gold trophy
2nd: 5,000 NP + 1 silver trophy
3rd: 2,500 NP + 1 bronze trophy

The BCers were very unhappy about the new ban on side accounts. This meant that they only had 4 pets to enter, or they would have to continuously rotate pets to their main account to enter them. The artists didn't really care about the NP and Item prizes in the BC. What they loved was drawing their different pets and getting a pretty trophy to show off on their lookup for winning!

To protest the new rule, artists came together and participated in what was called the Save Our Sides (SOS) campaign. The goal of this movement was to lift the ban and allow users to enter their pets on side accounts in the BC again. The campaign consisted of a few things. First of all, artists could add their name to a petition. The petpage that hosted the list was sent to TNT to show them how many users were against the new rule.


Former petpage of the Save Our Sides petition

BCers also sent in inquiries to the Neopian Times editorial about the new side account ban and entered art into the BC that drew attention to the. Here was my entry for a user-run theme week that promoted SOS:


I put a side account pet in a picture with one on my main so she could be in the BC too!

On April 4, 2008, the members of TNT changed their minds and allowed pets on side accounts to be entered in the BC again. They removed the NP and item rewards in order to make this happen.


{{ source }}

The Save Our Sides campaign is actually something really incredible. The artists of the website gathered together and actually made a positive change in an aspect of the website they cared about.

❄ Reserved » Osa prefers to keep to herself. This is probably why she enjoys her cave up in the mountaintop so much. Being alone gives her time to think and process and recharge her energy. Being around people or other living things makes her tired, so even when she's around them she most likely won't say much. She opens up to her PetPet, Tarn, sometimes, but even Osa can tire of him eventually.

❄ Loyal » Although she may come off as quiet and hard, Osa actually cares very deeply about those that have earned her trust. She will actively work to protect those that she loves and trusts. That trust must be earned from her--it's not automatically given.

❄ Hardworking » Osa will work extremely hard in order to complete a task that is asked of her (by someone she is loyal to of course!). She won't stop until she feels that her task is completed. She wants to make sure everything goes according to plan. (Her plan, that is). She doesn't like to stray from what was asked, or how she perceives things going in her head. This will really make her angry!

❄ Distant » Osa is not someone who examines her feelings very often, nor does she understand the feelings of others. She will become extremely uncomfortable if someone cries or shows emotional passion in front of her. To her, facts and logic are how one should be making decisions. She won't admit it, but she thinks lesser of those that trust their heart over knowledge. These attributes may have others think that she is distant or has a cold heart.

❄ Explosive » Although Osa is generally reserved, distant, and quiet, there are moments where she may "explode" with feelings of frustration or anger. These mostly come out when things don't go as she had planned. In this state, Osa may lash out verbally or physically, so you definitely don't want to be caught in the crossfire!

Osa's build is extremely large, athletic, and muscular. She stands twice as large as a horse, giving her the physical size to be able to carry an adult human on her back and maintain flight. She has deep red scales that cover most of her body. They will sometimes catch the sun, or reflection of the snow and give off a bright red shine. She also has smoother, sleeker scales that cover the underside of her neck and stomach. These ash grey scales aren't quite as hard as her top, red scales. While they would provide protection to most human-made weapons, this is a known weak spot of the Dragon Kau's when they are fighting each other. Her large tail is build more for balance while on the ground than use as a weapon. Along her back she has deep gold spikes that provide extra protection from above. (continue)

more about dragon kaus
Osa is part of a species of Kau's known as the Dragon Kaus. This species evolved around the rocky plateaus of Tyrannia. Neopians needed easier ways to get around and bred Kau's with favorable attributes to do so.

The human Neopians that lived in Tyrannia used them for various tasks around the villages in the plateaus. The most largest and most athletic were used for hunting, or into battle when the need was there. The slimmer and lighter weight ones were used for traveling to nearby villages for trade and commerce. Other Dragon Kaus were used to lift heavy objects or help with infrastructure.

Although the Dragon Kaus were bred in captivity to do the human's bidding, some of them did escape to surrounding areas, such as the caves in the mountains. These Kau's grew up wild and away from human services, so they were able to thrive as their own species. Different families or tribes of the Dragon Kaus eventually were established (known as "herds").
