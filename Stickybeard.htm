Hello and welcome to the Non-Flash / HTML5 Neopets Games Directory! The purpose of this petpage is to help players find what games are still available to play in Post-Flash Neopets. This guide will list all of the available games, dailies, and other activities that do not require Adobe Flash.

Key & Notes

All games have their own achievements, acronyms, and specific tips, but for here, we are just going to cover the basics. You can find more detailed guides and explanations in the resources provided below!

The Basics
Most games:
- Have three (3) score submissions for Neopoints per day.
- Reset their score submissions at Midnight NST / Neopets Standard Time (California).
- Reset their High Score Table (HST) on the 1st of every month.
- Do not accept score submissions after 12:02am NST if you started the game the prior day. This is only worth noting for Flash Games really.
- Award trophies at around 9:18 PM NST every day. There is an on-going glitch and there will be days where the games will not reward trophies for the day, referred to as a "skip".

HTML5 Games Basics
Most HTML5 Games:
- Do not have a High Score Table (HST). Only AAA's Revenge has one.
- Require you to wait 10 seconds to submit a valid score submission.
- Commonly prompt Suspicious User messages.

Neopoints (NP)
Neopoints are the free in-game currency that is obtained by playing games, doing dailies, and more! This currency can be used to play certain games, purchase in-game goods, etc.

Neocash (NC)
Neocash is the in-game currency that can be bought with real money. This currency is usually used to purchase Neocash goods and wearables to dress up your pet.

Invalid Session
Most commonly found in HTML5 games, this message is prompted when you try to submit a score in under 10 seconds. Getting this message does not use up your daily 3 score submissions (if possible) and does not have any serious consequences.

Review Scores
Review Scores are game score submissions that are automatically deemed too high by Neopets standards and need further inspection. Reviews are automatic and typically take a few days to a week to process, but may take up to several weeks depending on the popularity of the game. You will receive a NeoMail from TNT if your score was approved, but note that you will not receive any notification whatsoever if your score was rejected. You will also only receive the NP payout for your score after it has been approved, but if your score is worthy of an avatar in a specific game, you will receive the avatar immediately even if your score is sent for review. You are not allowed to publicly post Review Scores as of Editorial #?.

Suspicious User
Most commonly found in HTML5 games, this is a type of Review Score message. If you get this message from an HTML5 game, it means you've hit the review score, but what's different from usual score reviews is that all HTML5 game score reviews get rejected. This does not mean you're in trouble, nor does it use up one of your daily three game score submissions per day!

Understanding Ruffle

Ruffle is a Flash Player emulator written in Rust that can allow you to enable certain Flash features on Neopets. A list of notable Flash features that can be enabled with Ruffle will be provided below, along with an FAQ on the emultator. Note: This guide will not provide instructions on how to obtain Ruffle.

Flash Features That Can Be Enabled With Ruffle

- Altador Mini-Plot Star Mapping
- Coconut Shy
- Desert Scratchcards
- Maps of Neopia
- Most Flash Games (Score Submissions will not work)
- Most Non-Major Flash Animations (NeoQuest I & II, Tarla's Toolbar, etc.)
- Classic Neohomes (& Robo avatar)
- Qasalan Expellibox
- Test Your Strength
- Turdle Racing
- The Tutorial

Ruffle FAQ

➤ Is Ruffle safe to use?
Ruffle is safe to use. It's written in Rust, whose memory safety basis neutralizes common Flash problems and vulnerabilities.

➤ Am I allowed to use Ruffle?
TNT does not recommend any method of enabling Flash content for safety reasons, but they do permit the usage of third-party emulators and other Flash workarounds.

Flash FAQs

➤ Is it safe to enable Flash post-EOL?
NO, IT IS NOT SAFE, but due to the popularity of running Flash post-EOL to run certain Neopets features (i.e. Flash Games and Customization), it's become a common request for people to take their chances with the danger.

➤ Is it against the rules to run Flash post-EOL to play Flash Games?
It is NOT recommended due to the safety risks, but you are allowed to run Flash post-EOL to play Neopets Flash Games assuming you're not cheating with the workaround (See Neopian Times, Issue 927 and Neopian Times, Issue 956). Also, you are NOT allowed to submit scores for the same Flash Game and Beta Game within the same day (See Neopian Times, Issue 924 and Neopian Times, Issue 956).

➤ I keep getting an annoying pop-up to update my Waterfox browser. Do I update it, and if not, what can I do?
Don't update the Waterfox browser, since that will disallow you from using Adobe Flash (you could uninstall and reinstall the browser if you accidentally did so). You could download Waterfox Classic (Jun-2021), which will allow you to select the Never Search For Updates option to bypass the pop-up, but do know that this version is older than the recommended version and therefore more dangerous.

➤ What is the best browser/method to run Flash with?
There is no best browser/method to run Flash with, they're all equally dangerous in their own way. As for safety, you'd want to either use a Virtual Machine or run it on a separate computer.

➤ Can I use X browser/method to enable Flash instead?
There are many browsers/methods you can run Flash on, but for the sake of brevity, we will be covering Waterfox and Puffin. Here is a list of alternative browsers/methods that have been successful with Neopians:
- Pale Moon (Works with Windows)
- Virtual Machine (Works with Windows & MacOS and is safer than all mentioned, but is storage heavy and complicated to comprehend/teach)
- Basilisk Portable (Works with Windows)
- Cent Browser (Works with Windows, and probably better than Waterfox)
- Firefox ESR (Works with Windows & MacOS, but might not work with certain recommended versions and also don't use it if you use the regular Firefox browser often)
- Puffin Desktop Browser (Works with Windows & MacOS, but it costs money so nty glt)

➤ Should I use these browsers to browse other websites?
No. If you are going to enable Flash for Neopets, only use it for Neopets (not even for Neopets certified Fansites)

➤ Do I have to run Flash to finish the Altador Mini-Plot?
Fortunately, you do not, but you would have to download the Ruffle extension (don't worry it's safe to use and the instructions to download are provided under the Ruffle tab).
