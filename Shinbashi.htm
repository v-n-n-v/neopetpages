[Shinbashi • 新橋]

a Japanese name directory

新橋: [しん "new" + ばし "bridge"]

Welcome to Shinbashi • 新橋 - a directory for Japanese real words and real names co-managed by Cat and Titus.

As the site's name suggest, our goal is to create a database the that connects fans of Japanese names and words whether they are looking to trade, adopt, or simply wish to share their permanent pets.

We understand that names are often a source of inspiration for those who enjoy creative outlets like art and writing. For this reason, we do our best to verify the names in our listing by providing both the kanji and their meanings.


We do not offer advice on name trading or value. We might be able to talk about previous trades, but we are not an authority on trading.


Guidelines

Only Japanese words and names from official sources are allowed. This includes borrowed words, verb conjugations, and even alternate spellings (e.g. 'senpai' and 'sempai').

As of May 5th, 2021, we are accepting Word Stems (WS) to help users make informed decisions especially if their goal is to trade. Learn more about word stems here. Inclusion in our directory does not make them RW or RNs, so please advertise your pets accordingly.

For borrowed words, character names, etc., we will accept romaji spellings recognized by the Hepburn romanization, Kunrei-shiki romanization, Nihon-shiki romanization, and the JSL.

All pets were added to the database by their present owners. Please feel free to message us if you'd like to update a pet's information or have them removed from the listing.

  • Note from Titus: A big thanks to everyone who has ever helped out with this personal project of mine. My concept for [Shinbashi • 新橋] was simple: to create a resource that provides accurate and reliable information on Japanese names. Japanese is a language rich in symbolism and I hoped to provide these kinds of explanation to provide other users with inspiration and possibly a greater appreciation for a foreign language and culture.

    In line with this, I've selected an equally meaningful name. Shinbashi is both a place and a color. The word itself means 'new bridge' and is the name of a popular business area in the Minato Ward. This is admittedly a reflection of PC and the pet trading scene. On the other hand, the color is a type of teal or turquoise (which you see everywhere in the site's theme!). It's funny because most Japanese bridges are red and the color refers to how much the sky's blues stand out rather than the bridge itself. Much the same way, the site is simply a 'bridge' that helps the sky of names we listed stand out.

    [Shinbashi • 新橋] has been a fun project and learning experience. I hope the people who visited our site enjoyed it and as much as Cat and I did working on it and learning more about a language and culture we were already fond of. I won't be here anymore but it would be nice if more people joined the team and kept the ball rolling.

    ではお先に失礼します~!

    - Titus

Word Stems (WS) are the combining form of words which carries its meaning and is added before certain endings to create either new words or verbs with different voices.

Example: tobu (飛ぶ/とぶ ) is a verb which means to fly, to kick, to skip, to blow. Its word stem is tobasa- (飛ばさ~/とばさ~) which can only combine with -nai (~ない), -nakatta (~なかった), -zu (~ず), and -reru (~れる).

It will form tobasanai (飛さない/とばさない), tobasanakatta (飛さなかった/とばさなかった), tobasazu (飛さず/とばさず), and tobasareru (飛される/とばされる) which mean does not fly/kick/skip/blow, did not fly/kick/skip/blow, does not get flown/kicked/skipped/blown, and did not get flown/kicked/skipped/blown respectively.
