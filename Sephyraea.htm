  • Looking for a guide on how to get started with the Stock Market? Try these pages: https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=neopian_stock_market or https://thedailyneopets.com/articles/stock-market-guide or http://sunnyneo.com/games/stock/index.php and get ready to make some neopoints!
  • Check out this guide for a pretty complete run down of the stock market including different strategies for buying and selling stocks, such as the 15/30, day trading and set price selling methods.
  • Here is a more in depth guide specific to the 15/30 strategy if you want to read about that.
  • Once current prices get above ~45, the fluctuations can be bigger so rises can happen quicker. Keep an eye on your stocks when they get above this level!
  • Always try and buy shares at 15np and buy 1000 at a time (you can only buy a maximum of 1000 shares a day). If you sell them when the current value is 30, you will double your money. Selling at 45 triples your money and 60 quadruples your money. Most people aim to sell around 60, and some may hold on for a much higher value. It can takes months for shares to pay off at all so be patient!
  • Neopian share prices are not affected by how many people buy them or hold them, so players cannot manipulate or affect the price of the stock market in any way.
  • To give yourself the best chance of profit, you're probably better off investing in lots of different stocks rather than one or two and waiting for those to pay off.
  • If there are no stocks valued at 15 that day, it is better to buy at 16 or 17 than skip a day, as losing out on 1000 stocks for the day is a potential loss of the profit you'd have earned in the future. The stocks update their prices every half an hour so you can refresh to check if any have dropped to 15 if you have time before buying at a higher value.

RESTOCKING
NC TRADING
  • Want to know how to get started with restocking? Check out these guides! https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=restocking or https://thedailyneopets.com/articles/restocking
  • Create Jellyneo wishlists of the items you are looking for when restocking to quickly remind yourself of the high value things while you're waiting.
  • Zooming out your screen lets you see more stuff at once when restocking in a large shop. Don't zoom out too far though or you will make the captcha on the haggle page more difficult!
  • Despite what it says in the shops, they no longer restock every 8 minutes and instead restock randomly. New items can appear after less than a minute or can take over an hour, and each shop is different.
  • When restocking in large shops, you could keyword search (Ctrl F) after a refresh to immediately check for specific items, for example any Magical plushie, or search for 10,000 (as in 10,000np) and so on.
  • When using regular Shop Wizard, you apparently have a 1/13 chance of seeing the lowest price at any given time, and you can keep track of which sections of the site have been searched until you have seen 13 unique sections according to this topic.
  • Items stocked at 10,000np are often profitable and sell out very quickly. There are some shops (such as Spooky Foods) where there are more loss-making 10,000np items than others, such as Faerie Foods or Super Happy Icy Fun Snow Shop. The Magical Book Shop is a particularly great place as 10,000np and 5,000np books are both often highly profitable.
  • When planning what to restock, be aware that r100 items are currently bugged and do not show up in shops.
  • Do not price your items by the estimated value when reselling them, or even by Jellyneo's price guide. You will probably lose neopoints or never sell your item. The way to price your item is to find out its current lowest price on the Shop Wiz or Super Shop Wiz (if you have premium). Then you can choose to price at or near the lowest price, or you can hold off and hope the price rises if you will make a loss.
  • On very desirable lots, it is best not to haggle with the aim of pushing the price down too far, because the item is likely to be snatched up before you can agree a price with the shopkeeper. The shopkeeper is usually willing to take at least 300 off the listed price if you really want to push it, but it can be best just to offer the asking price (or an easily input number e.g. 9999np) to get the item quickly.
  • Great places to restock include any food shop (because many players are working on their Gourmet Club pets), book shops (because of Book Award and Booktastic Award), and the Post Office, Shell and Coin shops (because many people are trying to fill their stamp albums). These places also have profitable more common items so are good learning grounds. The following items are commonly requested in quests so can also be good sources of income: clothing, trading cards, beauty products, magic items, food, books, and toys. Food items are also needed in the Kadoatery so are great choices all round!
  • Check out this great petpagefor a genre-by-genre breakdown of each NPC shop! I used this guide when I was first getting started with restocking and I still think it's great.
  • You can also check the Auction House and browse lots at the Trading Post for good deals. Make sure that you double check how much items being auctioned are actually worth before bidding (via Super Shop Wizard/Shop Wizard search or checking Jellyneo) as some lots are extremely overpriced.
  • You can also try and snipe deals from other user shops by searching the Shop Wiz or Super Shop Wiz for any bargains and reselling them at a higher price (though it doesn't hurt to be kind and return something if you think someone has accidentally mispriced something - I once sold a 150,000 stamp for 1np because I was on pain meds and trying to take it out of my shop but used the wrong box. Unfortunately, that's just the way it goes sometimes!).
  • Kauvara's Magic Shop is a place where you can find Morphing Potions, many of which restocking for thousands or millions of NP. However, competition is fierce here so you need to be very dedicated.
  • Tyrannian Weaponry sells one of the most popular battle weapons on the site. Restocking the Turned Tooth usually costs around 40,000np and resells for around 90,000np. The good thing about it is that unlike many other items, it is only rarity value r83 (uncommon) so shows up fairly regularly!
  • Also keep an eye on the Igloo Garage Sale and the Almost Abandoned Attic. The igloo stocks anything below r89 and the Attic stocks anything r80 to r99 at a fraction of what they can sell for on the secondary market! There are limits on the amount of items you can buy from each of these places but they are well worth watching.
  • Petpets are generally considered Hard to Sell (HTS) so reselling them takes a long time. Restocking them may or may not be worth it.
  • For best results, restock where you can with a wired internet connection rather than a wireless one, and fast internet such as fibre. Having a hand to refresh and use the number pad and a hand to control the mouse is also best.
  • When pricing items in your shop, make sure not to leave any spaces blank (0s are fine). There is apparently a glitch that means if you leave a blank in a price field, all your items will be randomly priced when you hit save and you may sell things worth hundreds of thousands of np for very little.
  • To keep your interest up, it's best to restock in more than one shop at a time. Have a few different shops open in different tabs and keep refreshing them regularly. Whenever one restocks with new items, concentrate on that tab and check for any good stuff! Then go back to refreshing.
  • However, if you're focused on only the really popular shops or want to get the r99s, it is better to concentrate on one shop at a time. Whenever the shop clears, refresh as often as you can until the shop restocks. You should hopefully get first choice of the items, but be quick with haggling and know what you're looking for! Once you're finished haggling there you can try the next empty shop and repeat your refreshing until the shop stocks, and so on, or you can do something else until the original shop clears again. If you refresh often you may well get restock banned quickly, but for some shops that can be worth it. It just depends on your prefered play style. You can still get r99s with a more relaxed refresh rate but it will happen less often and be more down to luck.
  • I've noticed that often shops that restock with a few items tend to have a bigger restock only a few minutes later. I've not tracked restocks to check this is true, but I believe it to be the case.
  • Sometimes shops empty. This can happen while you're haggling for an item or while you're browsing. It usually means one of two things: the shop restocked with 0 items in it; or you are restock banned, which means the site thinks you have been refreshing too much or restocking for too long and is making you take a break. During a restock ban, no NPC shops in Neopia will appear to have items in them for you. There are no consequences for a restock ban, it doesn't mean you've done anything wrong, it is just making sure you aren't a bot and also giving other players a chance to restock. Bans can last from an hour to a day.
  • If all shops empty at once, this is known as a Clear. This happens pretty regularly and is used to advantage in high value item restocking strategies! For these strategies, concentrate on the one shop you're interested in. Once a clear happens (the shops empty), refresh the page as often as possible until the shop restocks. When it restocks, if nothing profitable appears you can move on to the next shop and see if it has restocked yet - if not, repeat the refreshing and so on. This will give you the best chance at getting first choice of the items. If you're not sure whether a Clear happened or if you've been restock banned you can check other shops to see if any have items or check with other restockers to see if they have also experienced a Clear.
  • The third day of every month is half price day in the NPC shops, so get your restock on! However, if you have stock in your shop bought before half price day, be prepared for prices to lower on the Shop Wizard for several days after. You can either adjust your prices to sell or hold out and hope that they come back up.
  • The third Wednesday of every month is 3% off at the Hidden Tower for accounts old enough to buy things there. It makes a real difference to the prices!
  • Restocking is often harder on weekends during daytime NST, when most players seem to be online_ I tend to avoid these times as competition for items becomes fierce and you need to be fast.
  • The current maximum price you can sell items for in your shop is 999,999np. Items are called Unbuyable (UB) if they are worth too much to sell in your shop. The current maximum price you can offer on the Trading Post is 2,000,000np (plus up to 10 items). If you want to sell an item for pure np over this amount, it is a good idea to find a buyer and then set up an auction for them. You can set it to Neofriend only (if you've added the buyer as a Neofriend) and set the start price at anything up to 50 million np with the buyer having to bid an increment that adds up to the agreed amount to get the item. Jellyneo recommends if you have a total of np agreed with your buyer that you halve the amount and put half the total in as the start price and the other half in as the starting increment.
  • If you have items you wish to sell that you are not allowed to find on the Super Shop Wizard for (such as Nerkmids), you can still bring up the Super Shop Wizard to find the average price of the lowest few listings. Call up Super Shop Wizard, type in the name of the item you wish to search and in the Max price box set a maximum price that seems just above the price you were thinking of paying. Tick the "Price Only (limited)" box and then hit search. The results will give you the average of the all the listings below the price you put in. This will work for any item 999,999np and below.
  • Want to get into NC Trading? Here is a great article about the basics.
  • NC stands for Neocash, the premium currency on the site. You can buy Neocash direct from Neopets or you can buy Neocash cards from third party and redeem them on site (often cheaper and also comes with free gift boxes and sometimes items!).
  • You can get NC items from the NC Mall, from spinning the NC Archives Wheel and even from visiting the Money Tree.
  • NC trading is exchanging items bought with Neocash with other players. You find items that you like and offer something you own or can buy of similar value to exchange for the thing you want. Item value is often determined by checking out The OWLs page, the OWLsTwo page, the Waka page which is no longer being updated but is a useful guide if OWLs doesn't have a value guide, and the Valisar page for non-wearables such as mystery capsules. This guide is also helpful for traders who are trying to work out how to value an item that doesn't seem to have a value listed anywhere. You can also ask for a value check on an item or a proposed deal by making a post (or commenting on the current OWLs thread) on the NC Mall neoboards. The community is very helpful and kind, so don't be afraid to reach out!
  • Anything that currently sells for 250nc or less is usually considered to be worth 1. Everything not currently for sale has a different value depending on how much people want it, and that's where Dress to Impress (DTI) tells you the OWLs value. This is why some popular items in the NC Mall may be worth hanging onto if they are only on sale for a limited time, as their value may rise after they are no longer available.
  • A Gift Box Mystery Capsule (GBC) is the standard measure when we talk about "caps", meaning 1 cap = 1 GBC. Most cookies are also valued at 1, although sometimes Faerie Quest Fortune Cookies might be considered a half by some people, you just have to check to be sure.
  • The easiest way to trade is to make a list of all the items that you are looking for on DTI (https://impress.openneo.net/) and make sure the list is set to "Items You Want" and "Trading". Then make another list of items that you own and would be happy to trade and make sure this is set to "Items You Own" and "Trading".
  • You can then go through the items that you are looking for by clicking on them in DTI and seeing who has them listed as Up for Trade. You can click on the person's username and see their trade list. This lets you look for items you are interested in that they have available. DTI 2020 (https://impress-2020.openneo.net/) includes the OWLs values of the items with each one!
  • Add up the OWLs number of the items you want from one person's list and then see if you have anything they want of roughly equal numbers (you can offer multiple items). Many people will also be interested in Gift Box Capsules which are regularly on sale in the NC Mall. These will have different names but will always end in "Gift Box Mystery Capsule". People may also be interested in trading for items like Faerie Quest Fortune Cookies, Lab Ray Cookies, NC Upcycle Cookies or NC Archive Cookies, all of which can also be bought from the NC Mall.
  • When you're checking out another person's lists, Classic DTI (http://impress.openneo.net/) has a handy "Compare With Your Items" button which will hide all items on the other person's trade list and wish list except for items which you have marked as owning or wanting. This saves a lot of time! DTI 2020 (https://impress-2020.openneo.net/), on the other hand, will tell you if you have any items each person offering the item is seeking when you check the item page itself (here is an example: https://impress-2020.openneo.net/items/77195/trades/offering) and on individual lists will place any items that you have marked as wanting or owning at the beginning and place a star and the word "Match" over.
  • The next step is to neomail the person! Message them to say you are interested in x items and let them know what you are offering in return. You may wish to ask the other person what they value the items at in terms of OWLs to make sure that you are on the same page, as OWLs often gives a range value such as 3 - 5 for items so you may need to have a discussion about what you both value items for.
  • Another useful piece of information is to check how many people are offering an item vs how many people are seeking it. If a lot of people are offering but nobody is looking for it, you are more likely going to be able to get it in a trade and may be able to snag a good deal. If hardly anyone is offering it, then the people who do have it may be reluctant to part with it and you may struggle to get hold of it.
  • Some people do not want to trade for GBCs, cookies etc. They only wish to trade items for items. Most people state this in the DTI if this is the case but don't be surprised if you are sometimes turned down for a trade if you don't have anything on someone's wishlist.
  • Making an intro on DTI can be really helpful! You can do this by creating an "Items You Own" "Trading List" with no items in it, and clicking "Edit". This will allow you to write a description where you can include details such as how often you are online_ whether you are interested in GBCs and cookies, and whether you have used the "number of items" feature to record how many of each item you have/want or whether you are using it as a marker for whether an item is hard to part with, or currently may be subject to a trade. Many people use "99" as a good number to denote these kinds of things.
  • Set any other lists you have to private! If you also keep track of your closet or what your pets are wearing, making sure the lists are set to private means that they won't show when people are looking at your list for potential trades and makes life much easier.
  • Make sure you have your Neopets profile name listed on your DTI. You can do this at the top of your "Lists" page. This is the only way people can contact you from DTI so it is very important if you want to receive Neomail offers.
  • You can also create Trading lists on Jellyneo, using the Wishlist feature (https://items.jellyneo.net/wishlists/). Fewer people use Jellyneo to find Trades but there is no harm in using this platform if you prefer and/or using both this and DTI.
  • Help keep OWLs up to date! You can report your trades on the latest OWLs thread on the Neoboards so that item values are updated. Check it out and submit your Trade data today! :) here is a great guide for how to figure out the values of items to let the OWLs team have your report.
GENERAL TIPS
  • In order to move NP to your side, you can complete the following steps:
    • Send your side account an item
    • On your side account, put the item up on the Trading Post and copy the link to the lot
    • On your main account, paste the link into your browser to load the trade and then offer another item (or any number of items you wish to move to your side account) along with the number of NP you wish to move to your side acccount
    • On your side account, accept the trade and ta da! You now have Neopoints
  • You can use NP on your side for the following activities: create and expand your gallery, complete faerie quests, adopt and trade neopets.
  • On a side account You CANNOT buy from NPC shops, get interest from a bank account, do dailies, submit scores for games, use a lab ray cookie (unless this is your only copy of the lab map), or buy premium. You CAN have the lab map, have a gallery, join a guild, have pets, buy extra pet slots, keep whatever you win from random events, buy from the Hidden Tower IF your main account is not old enough but your side account is. These are not exhaustive lists, if you are unsure then check the Help board or submit a ticket to TNT. Do not take any risks with your accounts.
  • You can also use your side accounts to buy NC and trade NC items, and collect free NC and NC bags from the NC Mall assuming those bags do not contain any NP prizes.
  • Here is the full Neopian Calender if you want to check what date a festival is coming up on. Remember that pets under level 40 can train for free at the Swashbuckling Academy on their day and TNT also comes out with new colours and items related to them!
  • Have you submitted a ticket to TNT and not heard back? Post your ticket number on the latest Highway to Help board located on the Help boards to get your complaint seen and dealt with.
  • Galleries have no maximum size, you can keep expanding them if you can afford it for as long as you want. If you want to delete a gallery category you have previously set up, go to this page of your gallery.
  • There is a limit to the amount of Neopoints you can have in total, on hand, in the bank and in your shop till. The limit is 2.147 billion NP. If you try and deposit into the bank when you are already at the limit, you will lose NP.
  • If you struggle with impulsively withdrawing neopoints or find yourself spending too much then you can make 15 small withdrawals from your bank account. You are allowed a maximum of 15 bank withdrawals per day so if you do this, you will be unable to withdraw any more on a whim.
  • Random events will occur a maximum of 8 times per day per account, not including premium events such as the space faerie showing up.
  • Some random events, especially ones which take away NP, are based on percentages so the more NP you carry on hand the more you may lose. The safest thing to do is to keep NP you don't need in the bank.
  • If you are aiming for any kind of daily trophy then you should know that the trophy script is run at around 1:35am NST currently, at which time the leaderboard is checked and trophies awarded. It doesn't matter where you stand on the leaderboard when you submit your score, only where you stand when the script is run.
  • Whenever you get a "Nothing Happens" result on the Lab Ray, it means that you were due a colour change but the Lab selected a colour unavailable for the species you were zapping. You'll get more colour changes if you choose to zap a species with lots of colour options available!
  • If you want to change your pet's gender, you can either use a Strange Potion on your pet or if you have the Lab Ray and refresh this page until you get the Lab Ray Scientist as a battledome challenger. Fight him in the Battledome with the pet whose gender you wish to change and in the first couple of hits he should change your pet's gender! You can then withdraw from the battle. You could also use a Royal Girl Kyrii/Royal Boy Kyrii Morphing Potion to morph your pet into either of those colours and also change their gender to match, or use a Witchy Negg to potentially change their gender (or turn them into a Lenny, Tuskaninny or Moehog, gain some maximum HP or intelligence or be cured of a disease... it's a random outcome).
  • Do you like stats? Do you want to know all the chances of the things? Check out this amazing petpage! Seriously I am drooling over this page. If that's not enough for you, check out this awesome petpage for even more stats! This one covers battledome training, scratchcards, kitchen quest and more.
  • Worried about how to get your account back if you ever get frozen? Take screenshots of important pages to help you remember information you may need, for example your NC Mall purchase history or the first page of your safety deposit box.
  • Want to change your avatar to something else you've collected but don't know how? Go to this page and choose your new avatar from your drop down menu.
  • Wonderding about Faerie Quests? They're random events that can occasionally happen as you browse the site, but if you have NC you can also buy Faerie Quest Fortune Cookies from this section of the NC Mall. They will grant you a random quest every day for 9 days. This is a great way to quickly gain stats for your Battledome pet and have a shot at getting a Fountain Faerie grant you a dip in the pool to change your Neopet's colour!
  • If you get premium, then every Friday you will get access to a Space Faerie scratchcard! Check out your current premium scratchcards at this page to win NP, items and even a couple of Neocash wearables.
  • Like adoptables? Check out this huge directory or this page for some Neopet adoptables :) you can also scroll through the Art Board to find other adoptable pages.
  • Ever heard of paperclip trades? This can be a nice way to work towards your goals. Post an item (or several) on the trading post and in the wishlist box state it is a paperclip trade. It helps if you include a line about your ultimate goal too, for example a paint brush or a petpet!
  • Items with a rarity of 500 are neocash (NC) items and cannot be sold, only traded for other NC items with players or given away. You will need a Basic Gift Box (or one of the limited edition versions such as April Showers Gift Box) to send NC items to other people. Some NC items are no trade and cannot be given away!
  • You can carry a maximum of 50 items in your inventory. If you want to carry more, move some to your Safety Deposit Box, open a gallery of things you like or start a shop and sell some things to make NP. You can also use the Discard button to throw away items you don't need to keep which are not worth any money - these are then deleted from the site. The donate button will send the item to the Money Tree for other players to collect - it's best not to send your rubbish there because it clogs up the tree and makes the game less fun for other people.
  • Really enjoy Neopets and want to spend a bit longer doing your dailies? Maybe work out which ones are most profitable and which aren't worth doing? Keep spreadsheets to start tracking all sorts of info! You can record what you get from your dailies, what you buy when restocking and how much you sell it for, which pets you have on which accounts and what their stats and birthdays are. If you can imagine it, you can probably track it.
  • If you spend at least 10,000nc in one calender month in the NC Mall then for the next month you will get access to the Future Fashions section! This section will stock many new items releasing in the NC Mall 72 hours before they are available to the general site.
  • Neohomes are flash dependent and are no longer working unless you have the Ruffle extension or another Flash workaround.
  • You can trade for other people's spare Paint Brush clothing. There are some wonderful pages that can help you find spare Paint Brush clothes such as this page and this page. Paint Brush clothing must be moved on a pet of that colour and species, so either send your pet of that type to the person or they will send you a pet of the appropriate type wearing the clothes. The person should be able to transfer your pet back to you the next day with the clothing on, but if you need to return their pet you borrowed, you will have to abandon the pet in the pound for them to pick up, or change its colour or species to return them, as moving a pet means it automatically takes its clothing with it.
  • You cannot give your account to another person. If TNT finds out (e.g. through checking IP addresses) the account will be frozen. You can give all your items, pets etc to other people but the account must stay yours.
  • You can switch accounts to use a different main account and keep your old account as a side account. It is best not to do your Dailies on either account for a couple of days either side of changing which account you intend to use for your Main account just in case, as you must not do your Dailies on more than one account.
  • Something going wrong and you need to contact The Neopets Team? Go to the bottom of this page and hit Create a Ticket. Don't forget to post your ticket number on the current Highway to Help board on the Help boards afterwards to get your ticket dealt with asap.
